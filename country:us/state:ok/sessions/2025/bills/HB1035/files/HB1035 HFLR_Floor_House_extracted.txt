16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 73 O.S. 2021, Section 83.1, as
18 amended by Section 1, Chapter 91, O.S.L. 2022 (73 O.S. Supp. 2024,
19 Section 83.1), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 83.1. A. There is hereby re-created to continue until
21 July 1, 2025 2030, in accordance with the Oklahoma Sunset Law, a
22 Capitol-Medical Center Improvement and Zoning Commission to exercise
23 the functions and perform the duties hereinafter prescribed.
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. The Commission shall be composed of eleven (11) members as
2 follows:
3 1. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
4 Services or his or her designee, who shall be ex officio chair of
5 the Capitol-Medical Center Improvement and Zoning Commission;
6 2. The Director of the Transportation Commission or his or her
7 designee;
8 3. The President of the University of Oklahoma or his or her
9 designee;
10 4. Two members appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the
11 Senate;
12 5. Two members appointed by the Speaker of the House of
13 Representatives;
14 6. The Chair of the Planning Commission of Oklahoma City or his
15 or her designee;
16 7. The Chair of the Long-Range Capital Planning Commission or
17 his or her designee; and
18 8. Two members of the Commission appointed by the Governor,
19 with the advice and consent of the State Senate, for four-year
20 staggered terms with one term expiring on January 31 of each even-
21 numbered year.
22 C. The Commission is authorized to appoint and hire a Director,
23 who shall serve as the chief administrative officer of the
24 Commission, and other necessary personnel. The Attorney General of

2 in the same capacity as he or she is to other boards and

6 Representatives shall be a resident within the boundaries of the
7 Capitol-Medical Center Improvement and Zoning District as set forth

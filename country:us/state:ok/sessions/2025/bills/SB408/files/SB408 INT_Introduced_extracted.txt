1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA

6 AS INTRODUCED

7 An Act relating to higher education; defining term;
7 prohibiting certain institutions from taking certain
8 actions due to a student’s pregnancy; requiring an
8 institution to make reasonable accommodations for
9 pregnant students; providing for reasonable
9 accommodations; providing for certain leave of
10 absence to prepare for and take certain examinations;
10 providing for certain leave of absence extension;
11 requiring an enrolled student who takes certain leave
11 of absence to return to certain program after certain
12 period of time; providing exemption; directing
12 certain consideration; directing certain office to
13 investigate complaints and facilitate forwarding of
13 certain complaints; requiring institutions to have
14 certain written policies and procedures; requiring a
14 copy of policies and procedures to be made available
15 to faculty, staff, employees, and students; providing
15 for codification; providing an effective date; and
16 declaring an emergency.
16
17
17
18
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19
20 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3251 of Title 70, unless there
21
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22
23 A. As used in this section, “institution” means:
23
24
24
Req. No. 1091 Page 1
1 1. An institution within The Oklahoma State System of Higher
1
2 Education; or
2
3 2. A private institution of higher learning located within this
3
4 state and accredited pursuant to Section 4103 of Title 70 of the
4
5 Oklahoma Statutes that receives awards pursuant to Section 2604 of
5
6 Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes, Oklahoma Tuition Equalization
6
7 Grants pursuant to Section 2632 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma
7
8 Statutes, or other scholarships or tuition aid funded with state
8
9 appropriations.
9
10 B. An institution, including the faculty, staff, or other
10
11 employees of the institution, shall not require an enrolled student
11
12 to take a leave of absence, withdraw from a program, or limit her
12
13 studies solely due to pregnancy. An institution, including the
13
14 faculty, staff, or other employees of the institution, shall
14
15 reasonably accommodate pregnant students so they may complete their
15
16 courses of study and research. Reasonable accommodation within the
16
17 meaning of this subsection may include, but is not limited to:
17
18 1. Allowances for the pregnant student’s health and safety,
18
19 such as allowing the student to maintain a safe distance from
19
20 hazardous substances;
20
21 2. Allowing the student to make up tests and assignments that
21
22 are missed for pregnancy-related reasons; or
22
23
23
24
24
Req. No. 1091 Page 2
1 3. Allowing a student to take a leave of absence. Reasonable
1
2 accommodations shall include the excusing of absences that are
2
3 medically necessary consistent with the laws of this state.
3
4 C. An enrolled student who chooses to take a leave of absence
4
5 because she is pregnant or has recently given birth shall be allowed
5
6 a period consistent with the policies of the institution, or a
6
7 period of six (6) additional months, whichever is longer, to prepare
7
8 for and take preliminary and qualifying examinations and an
8
9 extension of at least six (6) months toward completing the degree,
9
10 or while in candidacy for a degree, unless a longer extension is
10
11 medically necessary.
11
12 D. An enrolled student who chooses to take a leave of absence
12
13 because she is pregnant or has recently given birth shall return to
13
14 her program following a leave period of up to one academic semester,
14
15 unless there is a medical reason for a longer absence, in which case
15
16 her standing in the program shall be maintained during that period
16
17 of absence.
17
18 E. The institution shall consider the student’s requests for
18
19 housing accommodation due to her pregnancy.
19
20 F. The Office of Civil Rights Enforcement within the Office of
20
21 the Attorney General shall investigate complaints from students
21
22 alleging pregnancy discrimination in violation of this act. The
22
23 Office of Civil Rights Enforcement within the Office of the Attorney
23
24 General shall facilitate the forwarding of complaints to the U.S.
24
Req. No. 1091 Page 3
1 Depa rtment of Education’s Office for Civil Rights to investigate the
1
2 institution’s compliance with Title IX of the Education Amendments
2
3 of 1972, 20 U.S.C., Section 1681 et seq.
3
4 G. Each institution shall have a written policy for enrolled
4
5 students on pregnancy discrimination and procedures for addressing
5
6 pregnancy discrimination complaints under Title IX or this section.
6
7 A copy of this policy shall be made available to faculty, staff, and
7
8 employees in their required training. The policy shall be included
8
9 in the student handbook and the institution’s policies and
9
10 procedures and made available to all students attending the
10
11 institution.
11
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
12
13 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
13
14 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
14
15 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
15
16 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
16

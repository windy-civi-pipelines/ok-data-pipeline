6 An Act relating to vagrancy; amending 11 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 22-123, which relates to vagrancy; defining
7 term; authorizing business to contact law enforcement
under certain circumstances; requiring removal of
8 vagrant within certain distance of business upon
certain determination; and providing an effective
9 date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 22-123, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 22-123. A. As used in this act, “vagrant” means a
16 person who is in, remains in, or wanders about a public or private
17 place for any illegal purpose.
18 B. The A municipal governing body may provide by ordinance for
19 the arrest, fine, and imprisonment of vagrants.
20 C. The proprietor or manager of a business has the right to
21 contact law enforcement to remove a vagrant if the vagrant is within
22 twelve (12) feet of any doorway of the business, even if such
23 vagrant is on public property.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB537 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. Upon arrival at the business, the law enforcement officer
2 shall determine, through personal observation or viewing of video
3 footage, whether the person accused of being a vagrant meets the
4 definition provided in subsection A of this section. Upon
5 determination that the person meets the definition of a vagrant, the
6 officer shall relocate the vagrant to public property that is at
7 least one-half (1/2) mile away from the location of the business
8 which made the initial contact to law enforcement. The location for
9 relocation shall not be within one-quarter (1/4) mile of any other
10 business.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
12 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON LOCAL AND COUNTY GOVERNMENT

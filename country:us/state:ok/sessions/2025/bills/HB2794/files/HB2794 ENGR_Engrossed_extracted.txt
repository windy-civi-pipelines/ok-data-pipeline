8 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Department of
Commerce; making appropriations; identifying sources;
9 establishing amounts; providing purposes; and
providing for noncodification.
10
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
15 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
16 There is hereby appropriated to the Oklahoma Department of
17 Commerce from any monies not otherwise appropriated from the
18 Progressing Rural Economic Prosperity Fund created pursuant to
19 Section 256 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes the sum of Five
20 Million Dollars ($5,000,000.00) or so much thereof as may be
21 necessary to provide funding for a municipal park located in a
22 county with a population greater than seven hundred fifty thousand
23 (750,000) according to the latest Federal Decennial Census and north
24 of I-344 and west of I-35.
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2794 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3 There is hereby appropriated to the Oklahoma Department of
4 Commerce from any monies not otherwise appropriated from the
5 Progressing Rural Economic Prosperity Fund created pursuant to
6 Section 256 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes the sum of Four
7 Million Dollars ($4,000,000.00) or so much thereof as may be
8 necessary to relocate a naval submarine located east of State
9 Highway 165 and north of State Highway 62.
10 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
11 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
12 There is hereby appropriated to the Oklahoma Department of
13 Commerce from any monies not otherwise appropriated from the
14 Progressing Rural Economic Prosperity Fund created pursuant to
15 Section 256 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes the sum of One
16 Million Eight Hundred Thousand Dollars ($1,800,000.00) or so much
17 thereof as may be necessary to implement infrastructure improvements
18 at an industrial park located south of State Highway 62 and east of
19 State Highway 283.

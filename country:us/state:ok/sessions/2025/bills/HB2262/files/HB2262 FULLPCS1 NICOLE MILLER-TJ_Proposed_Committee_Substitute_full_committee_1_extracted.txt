8 PROPOSED OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to Alzheimer's Dementia and Other
Forms of Dementia Special Care Disclosure Act;
10 amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-879.2c, which
relates to disclosure of care, Alzheimer-Dementia
11 Disclosure Act Advisory Council, and website listing
disclosing facilities; including failure to provide
12 provision; providing for citation for certain
discrepancies; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-879.2c, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 1-879.2c. A. 1. Pursuant to rules promulgated under
19 the provisions of the Alzheimer's Dementia and Other Forms of
20 Dementia Special Care Disclosure Act, any nursing facility,
21 residential care facility, assisted living facility, adult day care
22 center, continuum of care facility, or special care facility that
23 publicly advertises, intentionally markets, or otherwise engages in
24 promotional campaigns for the purpose of communicating that said
Req. No. 13074 Page 1
1 faci lity offers care, memory care, or treatment methods within the
2 facility that distinguish it as being especially applicable to or
3 suitable to persons with Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of
4 dementia diagnoses shall disclose the type of care, memory care or
5 treatment provided that distinguishes it as being especially
6 applicable to or suitable for such persons.
7 2. The disclosure shall be made using a form developed by the
8 State Department of Health and shall be made to:
9 a. the Department,
10 b. any representative of a person with Alzheimer's
11 dementia or other form of dementia who is considering
12 placement within a special care unit, program, or
13 facility, and
14 c. the State Long-Term Care Ombudsman.
15 3. A referral agency, when disclosing documents pursuant to
16 Section 1-866.2 of this title shall provide any representative of a
17 person with Alzheimer's dementia or other form of dementia who is
18 considering placement within a unit, program, or facility the form
19 for each facility the referral agency recommends.
20 4. The facility shall submit the disclosure form to the
21 Department prior to entering into any agreement to provide care or
22 services.
23
24
Req. No. 13074 Page 2
1 4. 5. The Department shall examine each disclosure for
2 completeness and accuracy at the time the disclosure is submitted to
3 the Department.
4 5. 6. The Department shall review the most recent disclosure in
5 the Department's records during the facility's regular inspection to
6 verify that the disclosure is current and that the services
7 described in the disclosure are provided to residents as described
8 in the disclosure.
9 7. The facility shall conspicuously post or display in the
10 facility or designated area that is providing care for persons with
11 Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of dementia a complaint poster
12 that if they were not given a copy of the disclosure form that they
13 may file a complaint with the Attorney General's Consumer Protection
14 Unit.
15 B. The information disclosed as required by this section shall
16 include the following areas:
17 1. A written description of the special care unit, program, or
18 facility's overall philosophy and mission as it relates to the needs
19 of residents with Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of dementia;
20 2. The process and criteria for placement in, or transfer or
21 discharge from, the unit, program, or facility;
22 3. The process used for assessment, establishment, and
23 implementation of a resident plan of care, as it relates to
24 Alzheimer's dementia and other forms of dementia, including the
Req. No. 13074 Page 3
1 meth od by which the plan evolves, the frequency of assessment, and
2 how the facility will respond to changes in the condition of the
3 resident;
4 4. Staff-to-resident ratios, staff training and continuing
5 education that are in addition to all regularly prescribed training
6 and are commensurate with the need for increased care and
7 supervision for residents with Alzheimer's dementia and other forms
8 of dementia;
9 5. The physical environment and design features appropriate to
10 support the functioning of cognitively impaired residents;
11 6. The types and frequency of resident activities designed for
12 residents with Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of dementia and
13 descriptions of those therapeutic activities designed to address
14 cognitive function and engage residents with varying stages of
15 dementia;
16 7. The involvement of families in care planning and other
17 aspects of care, and the availability of family support programs;
18 8. The fees for care and any additional fees; and
19 9. Any accreditations or certifications issued to the facility
20 related to the care and services provided to residents with
21 Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of dementia.
22 C. Any individual employed or contracted by a registered long-
23 term care facility who publicly advertises, intentionally markets,
24 or otherwise engages in promotional campaigns for the purpose of
Req. No. 13074 Page 4
1 comm unicating that said facility offers memory care, Alzheimer's
2 care, or dementia care if said facility does not meet the criteria
3 of a memory care, Alzheimer's care, or dementia care facility shall
4 be subject to penalties as outlined in section H of the Alzheimer's
5 Dementia and Other Forms of Dementia Special Care
6 Disclosure Act.
7 D. The Department, with input from the Alzheimer-Dementia
8 Disclosure Act Advisory Council, shall develop a standardized
9 disclosure form. Any change in the information initially submitted
10 by the facility or other entity shall be reported to the Department
11 at the time the change is made.
12 D. E. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
13 preclude a facility without a special care unit or program from
14 admitting a person with Alzheimer's dementia or other forms of
15 dementia.
16 E. F. The State Commissioner of Health, with input from the
17 Alzheimer-Dementia Disclosure Act Advisory Council, shall promulgate
18 rules to effectuate the provisions of the Alzheimer's Dementia and
19 Other Forms of Dementia Special Care Disclosure Act.
20 F. G. There is hereby created the Alzheimer-Dementia Disclosure
21 Act Advisory Council. The Council shall make recommendations to the
22 State Commissioner of Health regarding the disclosure form and rules
23 promulgated pursuant to the Alzheimer's Dementia and Other Forms of
24 Dementia Special Care Disclosure Act.
Req. No. 13074 Page 5
1 1. The Council shall consist of nine (9) members to be
2 appointed by the State Commissioner of Health for such terms as he
3 or she chooses. The members shall be individuals who have knowledge
4 and expertise in the field of memory care or individuals who are
5 consumer representatives directly impacted by memory-care services,
6 provided that neither the members with knowledge and expertise in
7 the field nor the members who are consumer representatives shall
8 comprise more than two-thirds (2/3) of the total membership. The
9 members who are consumer representatives shall be individuals with
10 immediate family members who have received or are currently
11 receiving memory-care services in Oklahoma and shall not be
12 employees or board members of any facilities or entities subject to
13 the Alzheimer's Dementia and Other Forms of Dementia Special Care
14 Disclosure Act.
15 2. The members of the Council shall serve without compensation.
16 3. The Council shall conduct an organizational meeting at a
17 date and location to be chosen by the Commissioner and as frequently
18 thereafter as necessary to perform the duties imposed upon it. A
19 majority of the members present shall constitute a quorum.
20 4. Staff assistance for the Council shall be provided by the
21 staff of the State Department of Health.
22 G. H. Violation of any of the provisions of this act or the
23 rules issued pursuant to this act shall subject the offending
24
Req. No. 13074 Page 6
1 faci lity to the notice and enforcement provisions established for
2 the facility's license by the Department.
3 H. I. Any facility that offers specialized memory or dementia
4 care through a specific unit or program shall post the disclosure
5 form, including the information required to be included by this
6 section, on the facility's website.
7 I. J. The Department shall establish a website with a list of
8 those facilities that have filed a disclosure form pursuant to this
9 act. The website shall contain a link to the submitted disclosure
10 form and a table containing items from the disclosure form,
11 including staffing ratios, staff training, special programming and
12 activities, and accreditations or certifications.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
14

2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
3 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
FOR
5

7 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to state property; permitting
residents to forage for nuts and edible plants or
9 fungi on state-owned or state-managed property;
providing exceptions; limiting times for foraging
10 during certain seasons; encouraging certain apparel
during foraging; defining terms; providing for
11 codification; and providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 335 of Title 61, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. Any person may forage for nuts and edible plants or fungi on
19 state-owned or state-managed property for personal use and not for
20 resale except when such nuts and other edible plants or fungi are:
21 1. From crops certified by the Oklahoma Agricultural Experiment
22 Station system or the Oklahoma Cooperative Extension Service of the
23 Oklahoma State University Division of Agricultural Sciences and
24
Req. No. 1805 Page 1
1 Natu ral Resources, as provided in Section 8-33 of Title 2 of the
2 Oklahoma Statutes;
3 2. Located on state-owned or University-owned test or research
4 farms;
5 3. On property otherwise restricted for access by state or
6 federal law; or
7 4. From plants that are threatened or endangered.
8 B. 1. Any person foraging pursuant to this section on a state
9 or federal wildlife refuge or a Wildlife Management Area shall limit
10 his or her foraging to between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 p.m.
11 during those months coinciding with hunting seasons when the gun is
12 a legal means of take.
13 2. Any person foraging pursuant to this section is encouraged
14 to wear an outer garment of hunter orange that is clearly visible
15 while in the field.
16 C. As used in this section:
17 1. “State-owned or state-managed property” means property owned
18 or managed by an agency, municipality, or political subdivision of
19 this state, unless otherwise stated in subsection A of this section;
20 2. “Edible plant or fungus” means a plant or fungus commonly
21 found in the wild, which is edible to humans in part or in its
22 entirety and may require cooking to make safe for consumption; and
23 3. “Nut” means a dry fruit that has a single seed, a hard
24 shell, and a protective husk.
Req. No. 1805 Page 2
1 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

6 [ state property - nuts and edible plants or fungi -
exceptions – times for foraging – promulgation of
7 rules - codification - effective date ]
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
10 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 335 of Title 61, unless there is
12 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 A. Any person may forage for nuts and edible plants or fungi on
14 state-owned or state-managed property for personal use and not for
15 resale except when such nuts and other edible plants or fungi are:
16 1. From crops certified by the Oklahoma Agricultural Experiment
17 Station system or the Oklahoma Cooperative Extension Service of the
18 Oklahoma State University Division of Agricultural Sciences and
19 Natural Resources, as provided in Section 8-33 of Title 2 of the
20 Oklahoma Statutes;
21 2. Located on state-owned or University-owned test or research
22 farms;
23 3. On property otherwise restricted for access by state or
24 federal law; or
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB447 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 4. From plants that are threatened or endangered.
2 B. 1. Any person foraging pursuant to this section on a state
3 or federal wildlife refuge or Wildlife Management Area shall limit
4 his or her foraging to days and times authorized by the Oklahoma
5 Wildlife Conservation Commission.
6 2. The Commission shall promulgate all rules necessary to
7 implement the provisions of this section.
8 C. As used in this section:
9 1. “State-owned or state-managed property” means property owned
10 or managed by an agency, municipality, or political subdivision of
11 this state, unless otherwise stated in subsection A of this section;
12 2. “Edible plant or fungus” means a plant or fungus commonly
13 found in the wild, which is edible to humans in part or in its
14 entirety and may require cooking to make safe for consumption; and
15 3. “Nut” means a dry fruit that has a single seed, a hard
16 shell, and a protective husk.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

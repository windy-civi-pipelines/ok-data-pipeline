7 An Act relating to absentee voting; amending Section
7 1, Chapter 286, O.S.L. 2022 (26 O.S. Supp. 2024,
8 Section 14-115.12), which relates to accessible
8 absentee ballot for a blind person; requiring certain
9 documentation to receive certain absentee ballot;
9 updating statutory references; and providing an
10 effective date.
10
11
11
12
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 286, O.S.L.
14
15 2022 (26 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 14-115.12), is amended to read as
15
16 follows:
16
17 Section 14-115.12. A. 1. A registered voter who swears or
17
18 affirms that the voter is physically unable to vote in person at the
18
19 voter’s precinct on the day of the election because the voter is a
19
20 blind person and who provides documentation from a licensed
20
21 physician confirming that the voter is a blind person, as defined by
21
22 Section 72 of Title 7 of the Oklahoma Statutes, may request that an
22
23 accessible absentee ballot be delivered to the voter electronically
23
24 in a manner prescribed by the Secretary of the State Election Board.
24
Req. No. 1578 Page 1
1 A sy stem for electronic delivery of absentee ballots to voters who
1
2 are blind persons may include the same or similar requirements as
2
3 the electronic delivery system authorized for uniformed services
3
4 voters as set forth in Section 14-118 of Title 26 of the Oklahoma
4
5 Statutes this title.
5
6 2. An accessible absentee voting packet for voters who are
6
7 blind persons, which shall include all necessary materials except
7
8 the absentee ballot, shall be prescribed by the Secretary of the
8
9 State Election Board. The accessible absentee voting packet may be
9
10 delivered to the voter by United States mail or electronically.
10
11 B. 1. The electronic delivery system may provide the voter the
11
12 opportunity to use the voter’s personal computer to mark an
12
13 accessible absentee ballot privately and independently and then
13
14 print the marked ballot.
14
15 2. The absentee ballot shall be accompanied by an absentee
15
16 ballot affidavit, which shall be executed in the same manner as
16
17 provided for affidavits in Section 14-113.2 of Title 26 of the
17
18 Oklahoma Statutes this title.
18
19 3. The voter shall return the absentee ballot and executed
19
20 affidavit to the secretary of the county election board in the same
20
21 manner provided in Section 14-113.2 of Title 26 of the Oklahoma
21
22 Statutes this title. Nothing in this section shall allow for an
22
23 absentee ballot to be returned to the county election board
23
24
24
Req. No. 1578 Page 2
1 elec tronically, and no absentee ballot returned electronically shall
1
2 be eligible to be counted by a county election board.
2
3 C. A registered voter who is a blind person, as defined by
3
4 Section 72 of Title 7 of the Oklahoma Statutes, may choose to
4
5 request the assistance of another person to complete any
5
6 requirements of this section, as provided in Section 7-123.3 of
6
7 Title 26 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title.
7
8 D. Upon return of an absentee ballot described in this section,
8
9 the secretary of the county election board shall cause it to be
9
10 processed in the same manner as prescribed by law for other absentee
10
11 ballots.
11
12 E. Any person who is not qualified for the electronic delivery
12
13 of an absentee ballot as provided in this section, but who applies
13
14 for an absentee ballot described in this section, shall be deemed
14
15 guilty of a felony as provided in Section 16-102.2 of Title 26 of
15
16 the Oklahoma Statutes this title.
16
17 F. The Secretary of the State Election Board may promulgate
17
18 rules or procedures to implement the requirements of this section.
18
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective January 1, 2026.

8 An Act relating to education; amending 70 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 6-122.3, as last amended by Section 162,
9 Chapter 452, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section
6-122.3), which relates to adjunct teachers;
10 clarifying instructional levels that may be taught by
adjunct teachers; requiring adjunct teachers employed
11 to teach in certain programs to enroll in certain
training; requiring completion of certain training
12 within certain time period; prohibiting adjunct
teachers who have not completed certain training from
13 teaching in certain programs until certain training
is completed; prohibiting adjunct teachers from
14 teaching special education classes; amending 70 O.S.
2021, Section 11-103.7, which relates to early
15 childhood education programs; authorizing adjunct
teachers; providing an effective date; and declaring
16 an emergency.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 6-122.3, as
20 last amended by Section 162, Chapter 452, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp.
21 2024, Section 6-122.3), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 6-122.3. A. The State Board of Education shall grant
23 an alternative placement teaching certificate to a person who makes
24 application to the Board and meets the following criteria:

4 attained a retention grade point average of not less
5 than 2.50 on a 4.0 scale, or
6 b. has successfully completed a terminal degree, such as
7 a doctorate of philosophy, a doctorate in education,
8 professional doctorates, a master of fine arts degree
9 or a master of library science degree, from an
10 institution accredited by a national or regional
11 accrediting agency which is recognized by the
12 Secretary of the United States Department of
13 Education. The Oklahoma State Regents for Higher
14 Education shall be consulted to verify other terminal
15 degrees, or
16 c. holds at least a baccalaureate degree from an
17 institution whose accreditation is recognized by the
18 Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education and has
19 qualified work experience in a field that corresponds
20 to an area of certification as determined by the State
21 Board of Education, and
22 d. in addition to the requirements of subparagraphs a, b
23 and c of this paragraph, has demonstrated competency
24 or completed a major in a field that corresponds to an
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 area of specialization for an Elementary-Secondary
2 Certificate or a Secondary Certificate as determined
3 by the State Board of Education or a vocational-
4 technical certificate as recommended by the Oklahoma
5 Department of Career and Technology Education;
6 2. Declares the intention to earn standard certification by
7 means of an alternative placement program in not more than three (3)
8 years. The State Board of Education shall determine the subject
9 matter and the number of clock or semester hours required for the
10 professional education component for each person making application
11 for an alternative placement teaching certificate based on the
12 criteria of paragraph 1 of this subsection.
13 The State Board of Education shall establish a core minimum of
14 six (6) semester hours or ninety (90) clock hours and a maximum of
15 eighteen (18) semester hours or two hundred seventy (270) clock
16 hours for the professional education component.
17 The requirements set forth in this subsection shall exclude all
18 student teaching requirements pursuant to the provisions of
19 subsection E of this section;
20 3. Has passed the subject area portion of the competency
21 examination required in Section 6-187 of this title in the area of
22 specialization for which certification is sought; and
23 4. Either presents a document from an accredited public school
24 district in this state offering employment in the area of
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 spec ialization for which certification is sought on condition that
2 the person enroll in an alternative placement program approved by
3 the State Board of Education or declares the intention to seek
4 employment as a teacher at an accredited public school district in
5 this state. The certificate granted pursuant to this subsection
6 shall be considered a "valid certificate of qualification" for the
7 purposes of Sections 6-107 and 6-108 of this title, and the holder
8 of the certificate shall be considered an inductee for the purposes
9 of Section 6-195 of this title.
10 B. An alternative placement teaching certificate shall be
11 renewed for not more than a maximum of three (3) years upon
12 presentation of a document from an accredited public school district
13 in this state offering renewed employment in the same area of
14 specialization and a document from a teacher education institution
15 verifying satisfactory progress in an appropriate alternative
16 placement program.
17 C. Persons enrolled in an alternative placement program shall:
18 1. Have never been denied admittance to a teacher education
19 program approved by the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education,
20 the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools and by the
21 Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation to offer teacher
22 education programs, nor have enrolled in and subsequently failed
23 courses necessary to successfully meet the minimum requirements of
24 the program, except those persons who hold a certificate;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Have on file with the director of teacher education at an
2 Oklahoma institution of higher education a plan for meeting standard
3 certification requirements within three (3) years; and
4 3. Participate in an induction program as required in Section
5 6-195 of this title and have the same duties and responsibilities as
6 other inductees.
7 D. The State Board of Education may grant an exception to the
8 requirements for certification and, upon demonstration by an
9 individual of specific competency in the subject area of
10 specialization, may grant a certificate to the individual. The
11 State Board may establish other requirements necessary to grant
12 exceptions.
13 E. The State Board of Education, in consultation with the
14 Commission for Educational Quality and Accountability, may grant an
15 exception to the requirement to complete a subject area examination
16 for initial certification in a field which does not require an
17 advanced degree pursuant to this section if the candidate has an
18 advanced degree in a subject that is substantially comparable to the
19 content assessed on a subject area examination. The degree shall be
20 from an institution accredited by a national or regional accrediting
21 agency which is recognized by the Secretary of the U.S. Department
22 of Education. The Commission shall provide the Board with the
23 necessary information to determine comparability.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 F. Student teaching and a prestudent teaching field experience
2 shall not be required of alternative placement program participants
3 for standard certification.
4 G. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules
5 authorizing adjunct teachers who shall be persons with distinguished
6 qualifications in their field. Adjunct teachers shall be authorized
7 to teach at all grade levels including in early childhood education
8 programs. Provided, however, an adjunct teacher employed to teach
9 in an early childhood education program shall be required to enroll
10 in a State Department of Education approved training on the
11 cognitive science of how students learn to read during his or her
12 first year of employment as an adjunct teacher and complete the
13 training by the end of his or her second year of employment as an
14 adjunct teacher. An adjunct teacher who fails to complete the
15 training after two (2) years of employment shall be prohibited from
16 teaching in an early childhood education program until the training
17 has been completed. Adjunct teachers shall not be authorized to
18 teach special education classes. Adjunct teachers shall not be
19 required to meet standard certification. A person employed as an
20 adjunct teacher pursuant to this subsection who does not hold a
21 valid certificate to teach shall not be considered a teacher as
22 defined by Section 1-116 of this title.
23 H. Each teacher education institution shall provide the
24 Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation an annual report of
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 info rmation as specified by the Commission regarding participation
2 in the alternative placement programs offered by the institution.
3 I. The Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation shall not
4 accredit, renew the accreditation of, or otherwise approve any
5 teacher education program of any institution of higher education in
6 this state that has not implemented alternative placement programs
7 in at least four areas of specialization including mathematics,
8 science and a foreign language. Each institution shall allow
9 individuals who meet the criteria of subsections A and C of this
10 section to be:
11 1. Admitted to an alternative placement program without further
12 qualification; and
13 2. Offered the opportunity to complete the requirements for
14 standard certification set forth in subsection A of this section
15 during the summer preceding and the summer following the first year
16 of teaching with an alternative placement teaching certificate. Any
17 person seeking standard certification through an alternative
18 placement program shall be permitted to take necessary courses
19 during regular semesters if offered.
20 J. The criteria specified in subsection I of this section can
21 be met through a cooperative arrangement entered into by two or more
22 institutions of higher education.
23 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 11-103.7, is
24 amended to read as follows:
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 7
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 Section 11-103.7. A. Each school district may offer to four-
2 year-old children the opportunity to participate in an early
3 childhood education program.
4 B. The State Board of Education shall promulgate standards for
5 early childhood education programs for children who are at least
6 four (4) years of age on or before September 1 of the ensuing school
7 year. The standards shall include both half-day programs consisting
8 of not less than two and one-half (2 1/2) hours per school day, and
9 full-day programs of six (6) hours. The standards for all early
10 childhood education programs shall require a certified teacher, as
11 specified in this section, or an adjunct teacher as authorized by
12 subsection G of Section 6-122.3 of this title to be present in the
13 classroom for the length of the school day. Such program shall:
14 1. Be directed toward developmentally appropriate objectives
15 for such children, rather than toward academic objectives suitable
16 for older children;
17 2. Accommodate the needs of all children and families
18 regardless of socioeconomic circumstances; and
19 3. Require that any teacher employed by a public school to
20 teach in such early childhood education program shall be certified
21 in early childhood education.
22 C. The superintendent of any school district providing
23 classroom space or other school facilities for a federally sponsored
24 Head Start program that is planning to make a material change in the
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 8
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 arra ngement, shall give notice to the director of the Head Start
2 program at least seven (7) days prior to a school board hearing on
3 the matter.
4 D. A school district may offer such early childhood education
5 program within the district, in cooperation with other districts,
6 through the use of transfers as specified by law, or by contracting
7 with a private or public provider of early childhood education
8 programs, or by contracting for classroom space with a licensed
9 public or private child care provider based upon selection criteria
10 established by the district. If the program is provided through
11 contract with a private or public provider other than a school
12 district, the contract may only be continued if each teacher serving
13 the school on and after January 1, 1993, is certified in early
14 childhood education, except that all teachers, without such
15 certification, hired by such provider prior to January 1, 1993, and
16 serving in the school as an early childhood education teacher shall
17 be required to obtain certification on or before the beginning of
18 the 1996-97 school year. Any person who has been employed as an
19 early childhood educator with the Head Start Program program, has a
20 child development associate degree (CDA) and has at least five (5)
21 years of experience in such employment shall be certified in early
22 childhood education for purposes of employment in the public schools
23 of this state to teach in early childhood education for children
24 four (4) years of age and younger; if such person is recertified in
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 9
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 chil d development by the Council for Early Childhood Professional
2 Recognition within five (5) years prior to the expiration of the
3 person's early childhood certificate that was issued by the State
4 Board of Education, such person shall be granted a renewal
5 certificate in early childhood education by the State Board of
6 Education upon expiration of the early childhood certificate.
7 Provided, private or public providers shall meet such other
8 standards required by law and by the State Board of Education.
9 E. If an early childhood education program is provided by a
10 private or public provider pursuant to a contract as authorized in
11 this section, the contract shall address the requirements for
12 implementing the induction program as required in Section 6-195 of
13 this title. Teachers employed by a private or public provider in an
14 early childhood education program provided through contract with a
15 public school district shall receive in salary and/or fringe
16 benefits amounts not less than the amounts specified in the schedule
17 set forth in Section 18-114.14 18-114.15 of this title.
18 F. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
19 provide for the implementation of such program.
20 G. An early childhood education program may be offered jointly
21 by school districts that have formed interlocal cooperative
22 agreements pursuant to Section 5-117b of this title.
23 H. The term "prekindergarten" shall mean early childhood
24 education for purposes of this title.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2125 SFLR Page 10
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 I. The State Board of Education shall ensure that the standards
2 for early childhood education are aligned with any new subject
3 matter standards adopted pursuant to Section 11-103.6a of this
4 title.
5 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
6 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
8 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

12 An Act relating to children; providing sunset;
creating the Teacher Recruitment and Retention
13 Program; directing that Program be administered by
Oklahoma Partnership for School Readiness; directing
14 promulgation of rules; providing income exemption for
certain individuals when determining eligibility for
15 child care subsidy program; directing provider to
provide certain notice to the Department of Human
16 Services; providing that all other conditions for
eligibility apply; directing the Department to waive
17 certain co-payment; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 461 of Title 10, unless there is
23 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
HB2778 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 A. 1. There is hereby created, until November 1, 2028, in
2 accordance with the Oklahoma Sunset Law, the Teacher Recruitment and
3 Retention Program to be administered by Oklahoma Partnership for
4 School Readiness under the direction of the Department of Human
5 Services.
6 2. The Department of Human Services shall promulgate rules as
7 necessary to implement and enforce the provisions of this section,
8 including verifying income eligibility, employment status, and
9 administering subsidy benefits accordingly; and
10 B. The Teacher Recruitment and Retention Program, administered
11 through Oklahoma Partnership for School Readiness, shall ensure
12 that:
13 1. A child care employee working at a child care facility
14 licensed pursuant to the Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing
15 Act shall be eligible for the Teacher Recruitment and Retention
16 Program, provided that:
17 a. total annual gross household income does not exceed
18 One Hundred Twenty Thousand Dollars ($120,000.00) for
19 a two-parent household,
20 b. total annual gross household income does not exceed
21 Sixty Thousand Dollars ($60,000.00) for a single-
22 parent household, or
23
24
HB2778 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 c. children must attend a program participating in the
2 Department of Human Services’ Child Care Subsidy
3 Program;
4 2. As used in this section, child care employee shall mean any
5 individual who is employed for a minimum of twenty (20) hours per
6 week, averaged monthly, who provides direct care, supervision or
7 educational services to children or who performs duties onsite that
8 support the daily operation of the facility participating in the
9 Child Care Subsidy Program and which is licensed pursuant to the
10 Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing Act;
11 3. An employee who qualifies under the household income
12 limitations in paragraph 1 of this subsection:
13 a. shall have household income exempted from
14 consideration solely for purposes of calculating cost-
15 sharing or co-payment responsibilities,
16 b. shall have co-payments waived if eligible under the
17 Department’s Child Care Subsidy Program, and
18 c. shall be eligible to participate in the Teacher
19 Recruitment and Retention Program, even if his or her
20 income exceeds the Child Care Subsidy Program
21 requirements;
22 4. When an employee who is employed by a licensed child care
23 facility leaves employment of the licensed provider, the provider
24 shall provide written notice within thirty (30) days to the
HB2778 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Depa rtment of Human Services that the employee is no longer
2 employed; and
3 5. Other than the income exemption pursuant to paragraph 1 of
4 this subsection and the reporting requirements established pursuant
5 to this subsection, all other conditions of eligibility for the
6 Child Care Subsidy Program located in Oklahoma Administrative Code
7 (OAC) 340:40-7-1 shall be followed.
8 C. Subsection B of this section shall not apply to employees
9 who are employed by a child care facility licensed pursuant to the
10 Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing Act and who are eligible
11 for the Child Care Subsidy Program without household income being
12 exempted.
13 D. The Department of Human Services shall waive co-payments for
14 employees who are employed by a licensed child care facility and who
15 qualify under income eligibility requirements of the Child Care
16 Subsidy Program.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

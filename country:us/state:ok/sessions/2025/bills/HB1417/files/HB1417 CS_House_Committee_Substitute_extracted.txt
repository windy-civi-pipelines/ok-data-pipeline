4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-149.6 of Title 70, unless
5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 As used in the School Access For Emergency Response Act:
7 1. "Best practices in interoperable emergency communications"
8 means to identify the interoperable emergency communications needs
9 at a school level, to help target technical assistance, funding, and
10 other support, and to prepare for next-generation communications
11 technologies as exemplified by interoperable communications
12 technologies to enhance and better ensure school safety throughout
13 the State of Oklahoma;
14 2. "Board" may alternately or jointly reference the State Board
15 of Education and the Oklahoma Office of Homeland Security;
16 3. "Crisis management plan" means a plan including tactical
17 strategies and actions for responding to an emergency as set forth
18 by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) Manual IS-0100.C.
19 The key elements of a crisis management plan shall include, but
20 not necessarily be limited to:
21 a. threat assessment, recurring risk assessment, and
22 proactive identification following said assessments,
23 b. crisis response team structure,

1 c. communication protocols, including implementation,
2 training, as well as maintenance and timely upgrading
3 of interoperable communication systems,
4 d. emergency response procedures,
5 e. resource allocation guidelines, and
6 f. recovery and continuity strategies;
7 4. "Department" may alternately or jointly reference the State
8 Board of Education and the State Office of Homeland Security;
9 5. "Grant program" means the School Access for Emergency
10 Response Grant Program created in Section 3 of this act;
11 6. “Incident Command System” (ICS) means the standardized
12 approach to incident management as set forth by Federal Emergency
13 Management Agency (FEMA) Manual IS-0100.C: An Introduction to the
14 Incident Command System. ICS shall be adopted by grant applicants
15 as a precedent to be considered for funding through this act. The
16 Manual includes:
17 a. unified command structure,
18 b. common terminology,
19 c. modular organization,
20 d. integrated communications via interoperable
21 technology, equipment, and training, and
22 e. comprehensive resource management;
23 7. "Interoperability" means the ability of one or more radio
24 systems to communicate with another radio system in real-time.
Req. No. 13259 Page 3
1 Inte roperability includes the teamwork that occurs over real-time
2 radio broadcast channels between school district safety teams,
3 school-based safety teams, and professional emergency responders;
4 8. “Interoperable communications provider” means the supplier
5 or suppliers of interoperable communications technology, both
6 hardware and software, as well as related training, maintenance, and
7 upgrades of interoperable communications systems;
8 9. "Interoperable technology" means software and hardware that
9 enables two different radio systems and other communications devices
10 to communicate seamlessly in real time via radio broadcast channels
11 regardless of underlying technologies or manufacturers;
12 10. "Memorandum of Understanding" (MOU) means an agreement
13 between two or more parties that is designed and intended to
14 formalize a working relationship. Among other components as
15 identified by the stakeholders, the MOU is designed and intended to
16 outline the identified parties agreement to share private, assigned
17 Federal Communications Commission (FCC) licensed radio frequencies
18 or channels as well as other protocols and technologies needed to
19 achieve two-way radio interoperability as contemplated by NIMS IS-
20 0100.C for schools;
21 11. a. "National Response Framework" (NRF) means the manner
22 in which the United States responds to all types of
23 disasters and emergencies and includes the following
24 key elements:

1 (1) enumerated core capabilities of prevention,
2 protection, mitigation, response, and recovery,
3 (2) a component of the National Strategy for Homeland
4 Security that establishes a comprehensive,
5 national, all-hazards approach to domestic
6 incident response,
7 (3) an explanation of the roles and responsibilities
8 of federal, state, local, tribal governments,
9 private sector, and non-governmental
10 organizations in disaster response, and
11 (4) scalable, flexible, and adaptable coordination
12 structures to align key roles and
13 responsibilities across the nation, including
14 (and specifically for the purposes of this act)
15 for small, medium, and large school districts and
16 associated public safety agencies;
17 b. For the purposes of the School Access for Emergency
18 Response Act, NRF:
19 (1) enumerates the interrelationship between school
20 access for emergency response and the National
21 Incident Management System (NIMS), and
22 (2) outlines the operation of NIMS Incident Command
23 System in relations to school access for
24 emergency response;

1 12. "Public safety answering point" means a facility equipped
2 and staffed to provide emergency telephone service;
3 13. "Radio system" means a private network for voice
4 communications;
5 14. “Real-time communications network” means private licensed
6 radio frequencies, channels, or talk-groups, on either a wire or
7 wireless communication network, that allows for real-time voice or
8 data transmissions between a public safety answering point, specific
9 school safety team members, school district safety teams, and first
10 responders;
11 15. "Revolving fund" means the School Access for Emergency
12 Response Revolving Fund created in Section 6 of this act;
13 16. "Safety team" means personnel who have a role in crisis
14 management plans, including school district safety teams and school-
15 based safety teams. In regard to school-based safety teams,
16 training includes NIMS IS-0100.C protocols as well as training with
17 interoperable communications technology itself;
18 17. "School" means elementary, middle, and secondary schools
19 supported by public taxation and shall include charter schools
20 established pursuant to the Oklahoma Charter Schools Act; and
21 18. "Standards-based" means radio specifications defined by a
22 national association of the telecommunications industry that every
23 manufacturer can follow to implement equipment interoperability.

1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-149.7 of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created in the State Department of Education
5 the School Access for Emergency Response Grant Program to provide
6 grants to schools for needed interoperable communication hardware,
7 software, equipment maintenance, and training to allow for seamless
8 communications between first responder communications systems, new
9 or existing school district communications systems, and new or
10 existing school-based systems.
11 B. Grant recipients may use the money received through the
12 grant program for the following purposes:
13 1. To deliver training programs to teach district safety teams
14 and security personnel as well as appropriate school-based safety
15 teams and other personnel basic procedures for effective
16 communications with first responders in an emergency. The training
17 shall be based upon Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
18 Manual IS-0100.C: An Introduction to the Incident Command System.
19 As required by federal law and associated regulations, these derived
20 training elements shall include:
21 a. completion of the entire course, which is usually
22 available online through the Federal Emergency
23 Management Agency (FEMA) Emergency Management
24 Institute (EMI) website,
Req. No. 13259 Page 7
1 b. an expectation of understanding the basic principles
2 and features of ISC, including its purpose,
3 organization, and the roles and responsibilities of
4 key positions within the structure established by the
5 FEMA Manual,
6 c. familiarization with how ISC is applied in various
7 types of school-based incidents, including planned
8 events, natural disasters, and other types of crisis
9 incidents or emergency situations,
10 d. scenario-based exercises to reinforce learning and
11 ensure comprehension of the material,
12 e. a final exam to assess the participant's understanding
13 of the course material. A passing score, often
14 seventy-five percent (75%) or higher, is necessary to
15 receive a certificate of completion,
16 f. a certification upon successful completion of the
17 course and final exam, which may be necessary for
18 certain federal positions or for compliance with
19 federal emergency management directives, and
20 g. periodic refresher courses or continuing education to
21 maintain certification and stay current with any
22 changes or updates to the system;
23 2. To implement an interoperable technology solution to provide
24 or to upgrade the following:
Req. No. 13259 Page 8
1 a. a system or technology that can be activated and
2 deactivated by a public safety answering point, a
3 network administrator, and a school over both the
4 radio system and other communications networks,
5 b. radio and other technology bridge ability that is
6 nonradio vendor specific for connecting independent
7 school networks, school district networks, and public
8 safety networks in the region, and
9 c. an interoperable real-time radio broadcast solution
10 that operates over specifically managed radio networks
11 and other proactively identified communications
12 networks; and
13 3. To maintain or improve a school's existing interoperable
14 communication hardware or software or to provide interoperable
15 communication hardware and software to a school that does not yet
16 have it.
17 C. The proposed interoperable communications solution shall not
18 increase or impact public safety system loading in existence at the
19 time of the enactment of this act. School-based and school district
20 interoperable communications systems shall consider coverage,
21 capacity, and related features in regard to any necessary radio
22 system capacity expansions where school loading is determined to
23 potentially have more than a negligible impact on preexisting public
24 safety system loading.
Req. No. 13259 Page 9
1 D. The State Department of Education shall administer the grant
2 program. Subject to the availability of funds, grants shall be paid
3 out of the "School Access for Emergency Response Revolving Fund"
4 created in Section 6 of this act.
5 E. The State Board of Education, in consultation with the grant
6 selection committee created in subsection F of this section, shall
7 implement the grant program in accordance with this act. The
8 Department shall promulgate rules necessary to implement the
9 requirements of this act. At minimum, such rules shall specify the
10 time frames for applying for grants, the form of the grant program
11 application, and the time frames for distributing grant money.
12 These rules shall require that school districts and schools making
13 application shall adopt the National Incident Management System
14 (NIMS) to train personnel in the Incident Command System (IS-0100.C)
15 and conduct joint exercises involving professional first responders
16 with schools and school districts to better coordinate teamwork and
17 overall response to incidents.
18 F. There is hereby created the grant selection committee to
19 work with the State Board of Education to determine whether a grant
20 applicant satisfies the criteria to receive a grant. The committee
21 shall be composed of seven (7) members appointed as specified in
22 this subsection. The members of the committee shall serve without
23 compensation, but shall be reimbursed for all actual and necessary
24 expenses incurred while engaged in the discharge of official duties
Req. No. 13259 Page 10
1 purs uant to this act in accordance with the State Travel
2 Reimbursement Act. Members of the committee shall meet as often as
3 deemed necessary by the Board to accomplish the work of the
4 committee. Members of the committee shall be appointed on or before
5 November 1, 2025, as follows:
6 1. The Governor shall appoint one member;
7 2. The Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives shall
8 appoint one member who represents urban or suburban school
9 districts, and one member who represents the chiefs of police;
10 3. The President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate shall
11 appoint one member who represents rural school districts, and one
12 member who represents county sheriffs;
13 4. The Commissioner of Public Safety shall appoint one member;
14 and
15 5. The State Superintendent of Public Instruction shall appoint
16 one member.
17 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-149.8 of Title 70, unless
19 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. A school is required to have a Memorandum of Understanding,
21 as defined in Section 2 of this act, with its regional public safety
22 answering point and the local law enforcement agency or agencies
23 that serve the school for communications interoperability to be
24 eligible to apply for a grant under this act. To receive a grant, a
Req. No. 13259 Page 11
1 scho ol must submit an application to the Department in accordance
2 with rules promulgated by the Department. At minimum, the
3 application must include the following information:
4 1. A description of the school's current interoperable
5 communication technology, if any;
6 2. A description of efforts that the school has taken to
7 coordinate emergency communication with law enforcement, 9-1-1
8 system administrators, and other schools; and
9 3. A description of the purpose or purposes for which the
10 school would plan to use the grant money, including, but not limited
11 to, proposed training programs, joint exercise plans, and
12 interoperability technology solutions.
13 B. The grant selection committee created in Section 3 of this
14 act shall review the applications received pursuant to this section.
15 In awarding grants, the grant selection committee shall consider
16 whether the school has a crisis management plan in place with safety
17 team members designated for communications with first responders.
18 C. Subject to the availability of funds, on or before June 30,
19 2026, and on or before June 30 each year thereafter for the duration
20 of the grant program, the Board shall award grants as provided in
21 this act. The Board shall distribute the grant money within thirty
22 (30) days after the grants are awarded.

1 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-149.9 of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. On or before January 15, 2028, and on or before January 15
5 each year thereafter for the duration of the grant program, each
6 school district with schools that received a grant through the grant
7 program provided in this act shall submit a report to the
8 Department. At minimum, the report shall include the following
9 information:
10 1. The number of schools within the district that used grant
11 money to provide training programs to appropriate personnel
12 regarding effective communications with first responders in an
13 emergency;
14 2. The number of schools within the district that used grant
15 money to provide or upgrade its interoperable technology, including
16 interoperable communication hardware or software; and
17 3. The estimated number of enrolled students that benefitted or
18 will benefit from how the grant money was utilized.
19 B. On or before May 30, 2028, and on or before May 30 each year
20 thereafter for the duration of the grant program, the Department
21 shall present a summarized report of the activities of the grant
22 program to the Oklahoma Legislature.

1 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-149.10 of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
5 fund for the State Department of Education to be designated the
6 "School Access for Emergency Response Revolving Fund". The fund
7 shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations,
8 and shall consist of all monies received by the Department of
9 Education from:
10 1. Reimbursements, grants, or other monies received from other
11 state agencies and entities for establishing or maintaining
12 interoperable communication between schools and public safety
13 officials;
14 2. Gifts, donations, and bequests; and
15 3. Monies appropriated or apportioned by the Legislature.
16 B. All monies accruing to the credit of the School Access for
17 Emergency Response Revolving Fund are hereby appropriated and may be
18 budgeted and expended by the State Department of Education for the
19 purpose of providing grants for the School Access for Emergency
20 Response Grant Program as created in Section 3 of this act.
21 C. Expenditures from the School Access for Emergency Response
22 Revolving Fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State
23 Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by law with the
24 Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise Services for
Req. No. 13259 Page 14
1 appr oval and payment. Expenditures from the School Access for
2 Emergency Response Revolving Fund shall not exceed Five Million
3 Dollars ($5,000,000.00) in any fiscal year and shall be divided
4 equally among grant applicants who were approved by the State Board
5 of Education and grant selection committee as provided for in
6 Section 3 of this act.
7 SECTION 7. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

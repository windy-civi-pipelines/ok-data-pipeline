1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
1
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
2

6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to workers’ compensation; amending
7 85A O.S. 2021, Section 40, which relates to failure
8 to secure compensation; granting Attorney General
8 concurrent authority with the Oklahoma Workers’
9 Compensation Commission to investigate and enforce
9 certain violations; authorizing certain inspections
10 for specified purpose; updating statutory language;
10 and declaring an emergency.
11
11
12
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 85A O.S. 2021, Section 40, is
14
15 amended to read as follows:
15
16 Section 40. A. 1. Any employer who fails to secure
16
17 compensation required under the Administrative Workers’ Compensation
17
18 Act, upon conviction, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject
18
19 to a fine of up to Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) to be deposited
19
20 in the Workers’ Compensation Commission Revolving Fund.
20
21 2. The Attorney General shall have concurrent authority with
21
22 the Oklahoma Workers’ Compensation Commission to investigate and
22
23 enforce any violation of the laws of this state regarding workers’
23
24
24
Req. No. 1145 Page 1
1 comp ensation and may perform unannounced on-site inspections of any
1
2 employer to determine compliance with this section.
2
3 3. This subsection shall not affect any other liability of the
3
4 employer under the Administrative Workers’ Compensation Act.
4
5 B. 1. Whenever the Oklahoma Workers’ Compensation Commission
5
6 has reason to believe that any employer required to secure the
6
7 payment of compensation under the Administrative Workers’
7
8 Compensation Act has failed to do so, the Commission shall serve on
8
9 the employer a proposed judgment declaring the employer to be in
9
10 violation of the Administrative Workers’ Compensation Act and
10
11 containing the amount, if any, of the civil penalty to be assessed
11
12 against the employer under paragraph 5 of this subsection.
12
13 2. a. An employer may contest a proposed judgment of the
13
14 Commission issued under paragraph 1 of this subsection
14
15 by filing with the Commission, within twenty (20) days
15
16 of receipt of the proposed judgment, a written request
16
17 for a hearing.
17
18 b. The request for a hearing does not need to be in any
18
19 particular form but shall specify the grounds on which
19
20 the person contests the proposed judgment, the
20
21 proposed assessment, or both.
21
22 c. If a written request for hearing is not filed with the
22
23 Commission within the time specified in subparagraph a
23
24 of this paragraph, the proposed judgment, the proposed
24
Req. No. 1145 Page 2
1 penalty or both, shall be a final judgment of the
1
2 Commission and shall not be subject to further review
2
3 by any court, except if the employer shows good cause
3
4 why it did not timely contest the judgment or penalty.
4
5 d. A proposed judgment by the Commission under this
5
6 section shall be prima facie correct, and the burden
6
7 is on the employer to prove that the proposed judgment
7
8 is incorrect.
8
9 3. a. If the employer alleges that a carrier has contracted
9
10 to provide it workers’ compensation insurance coverage
10
11 for the period in question, the employer shall include
11
12 the allegation in its request for hearing and shall
12
13 name the carrier.
13
14 b. The Commission shall promptly notify the carrier of
14
15 the employer’s allegation and of the date of hearing.
15
16 c. The carrier shall promptly, and no later than five (5)
16
17 days before the hearing, respond in writing to the
17
18 employer’s allegation by providing evidence of
18
19 coverage for the period in question or by
19
20 affirmatively denying the employer’s allegation.
20
21 4. Hearings under this section shall be procedurally conducted
21
22 as provided in Sections 69 through 78 of this title.
22
23 5. The Commission may assess a fine against an employer who
23
24 fails to secure the payment of compensation in an amount up to One
24
Req. No. 1145 Page 3
1 Thou sand Dollars ($1,000.00) per day of violation payable to the
1
2 Workers’ Compensation Commission Revolving Fund, not to exceed a
2
3 total of Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) for the first
3
4 violation.
4
5 6. If an employer fails to secure the payment of compensation
5
6 or pay any civil penalty assessed against the employer after a
6
7 judgment issued under this section has become final by operation of
7
8 law or on appeal, the Commission may petition the Oklahoma County
8
9 District Court or the district court of the county where the
9
10 employer’s principal place of business is located for an order
10
11 enjoining the employer from engaging in further employment until
11
12 such time as the employer secures the payment of compensation or
12
13 makes full payment of all civil penalties.
13
14 7. Upon any penalty becoming final under this section, the
14
15 Commission may institute collection proceedings against any assets
15
16 of the employer independently or in district court including, but
16
17 not limited to, an asset hearing, garnishment of income and wages,
17
18 judgment lien or an intercept of an income tax refund consistent
18
19 with Section 205.2 of Title 68 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
19
20 8. Information subject to subsection A or B of Section 4-508 of
20
21 Title 40 of the Oklahoma Statutes may be disclosed to the employees
21
22 of the Commission for purposes of investigation and enforcement of
22
23 workers’ compensation coverage requirements pursuant to this title,
23
24
24
Req. No. 1145 Page 4
1 and such information shall be admissible in any hearing before an
1
2 administrative law judge of the Commission.
2
3 9. Litigation files and investigatory reports of the Commission
3
4 arising from enforcement of the provisions of this section shall be
4
5 confidential pursuant to Section 24A.12 of Title 51 of the Oklahoma
5
6 Statutes.
6
7 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7
8 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
8
9 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9
10 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

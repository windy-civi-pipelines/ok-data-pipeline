7 An Act relating to students; authorizing students

19
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-140.1 of Title 70, unless

21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21
22 A. Students enrolled in a charter school or virtual charter
22
23 school authorized pursuant to the Oklahoma Charter Schools Act shall

4 students shall be determined in accordance with the resident school
4
5 district’s eligibility rules and policies and any rules and policies
5
6 of a school athletic association, as defined in Section 27-102 of
6
7 Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
7
8 C. For the purposes of this section, “resident school district”
8
9 shall mean the public school district in which the student resides
9
10 as defined in Section 1-113 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.

24 residence. If capacity is insufficient to enroll all eligible

3 preference to eligible students who reside within the boundaries of
3
4 the school district in which the charter school is located and who
4
5 attend a school site that has been identified as in need of
5
6 improvement by the State Board of Education pursuant to the
6
7 Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965, as amended or
7
8 reauthorized by P.L. No. 114-95, also known as the Every Student
8
9 Succeeds Act (ESSA). A charter school may limit admission to
9
10 students within a given age group or grade level. A charter school
10
11 sponsored by the Statewide Charter School Board when the applicant
11
12 of the charter school is the Office of Juvenile Affairs shall limit
12
13 admission to youth that are in the custody or supervision of the
13
14 Office of Juvenile Affairs.

15 B. A brick-and-mortar charter school shall admit students who
15
16 reside in the attendance area of a school or in a school district
16
17 that is under a court order of desegregation or that is a party to
17
18 an agreement with the United States Department of Education Office
18
19 for Civil Rights directed towards mediating alleged or proven racial
19
20 discrimination unless notice is received from the resident school
20
21 district that admission of the student would violate the court order
21
22 or agreement.

23 C. A brick-and-mortar charter school may designate a specific
23
24 geographic area within the school district in which the charter
24
Req. No. 1034 Page 3
1 scho ol is located as an academic enterprise zone and may limit
1
2 admissions to students who reside within that area. An academic
2
3 enterprise zone shall be a geographic area in which sixty percent
3
4 (60%) or more of the children who reside in the area qualify for the
4
5 free or reduced school lunch program.
5
6 D. Except as provided in subsections B and C of this section, a
6
7 charter school or virtual charter school shall not limit admission
7
8 based on ethnicity, national origin, gender, income level, disabling
8
9 condition, proficiency in the English language, measures of
9
10 achievement, aptitude, or athletic ability.

11 E. A sponsor of a charter school shall not restrict the number
11
12 of students a charter school may enroll, and the Statewide Charter
12
13 School Board shall not restrict the number of students a virtual
13
14 charter school or charter school may enroll. The capacity of a
14
15 charter school or virtual charter school shall be determined
15
16 quarterly by the governing board of the charter school or virtual
16
17 charter school pursuant to the provisions of the Education Open
17
18 Transfer Act.
18
19 F. Beginning July 1, 2024, each statewide virtual charter
19
20 school which has been approved and sponsored by the Statewide
20
21 Charter School Board or any virtual charter school for which the

22 Board has assumed sponsorship as provided for in Section 1 Section
22
23 3-132.1 of this act title shall be considered a statewide virtual

4 in a statewide virtual charter school sponsored by the Statewide
4
5 Charter School Board shall not be authorized to participate in any

6 activities administered by the Oklahoma Secondary School Activities

7 Association pursuant to Section 1 of this act. However, the
7
8 students Virtual charter school students may participate in

9 intramural activities sponsored by a statewide virtual charter

10 school, an online provider for the charter school, or any other
10
11 outside organization.

13 wishes to enroll in a virtual charter school shall be considered a
13
14 transfer student from his or her resident school district. A
14
15 virtual charter school shall pre-enroll any public school student
15
16 whose parent or legal guardian expresses intent to enroll in the
16
17 virtual charter school. Upon pre-enrollment, the State Department
17
18 of Education shall initiate a transfer on a form to be completed by
18
19 the receiving virtual charter school. Upon approval of the
19
20 receiving virtual charter school, the student may begin
20
21 instructional activities. Upon notice that a public school student
21
22 has transferred to a virtual charter school, the resident school
22
23 district shall transmit the student’s records within three (3)
23
24 school days.

1 2. The State Department of Education shall notify the
1
2 Legislature and Governor if it determines that the information
2
3 technology infrastructure necessary to process the transfer of

4 students to a virtual charter school is inadequate and additional
4
5 time is needed for implementation.

6 3. A public school student may transfer to one statewide
6
7 virtual charter school at any time during a school year. For
7
8 purposes of this subsection, “school year” shall mean July 1 through
8
9 the following June 30. After one statewide virtual charter school
9
10 transfer during a school year, no public school student shall be
10
11 permitted to transfer to any other statewide virtual charter school
11
12 without the concurrence of both the resident school district and the

13 receiving virtual charter school. A student shall have a grace
13
14 period of fifteen (15) school days from the first day of enrollment

15 in a statewide virtual charter school to withdraw without academic

16 penalty and shall continue to have the option of one virtual charter
16
17 school transfer without the concurrence of both the resident school
17
18 district and the receiving virtual charter school during that same

19 school year. A statewide virtual charter school student that has

20 utilized the allowable one transfer pursuant to this subsection
20
21 shall not be permitted to transfer to another school district or
21
22 another statewide virtual charter school without first notifying his

23 or her resident district and initiating a new transfer. Upon
23
24 cancellation of a transfer, the virtual charter school shall
24

7 transfer in order to remain enrolled.

9 to enroll in a statewide virtual charter school sponsored by the

10 Statewide Charter School Board pursuant to Section 1 Section 3-132.1

14 pursuant to an official military order.

16 by electronic means for enrollment and course registration for

16
17 amended to read as follows:
17
18 Section 27-103. A public school or school district shall not be
18
19 a member of any school athletic association unless that association
19
20 has adopted a written policy that requires the following:
20
21 1. All records of the association to be made accessible
21
22 consistent with the provisions of the Oklahoma Open Records Act;

23 2. All meetings of the association to be open and conducted in
23
24 a manner consistent with the provisions of the Oklahoma Open Meeting

2 executive session requirements; and

3 3. That a student enrolled in a charter school or virtual
3
4 charter school is allowed to participate in interscholastic

6 defined in Section 1-113 of this title. Student participation shall

7
8 4. An annual financial audit and a compliance audit of all

13 conducted no later than December 31, 2014, and by December 31 every

14 five (5) years thereafter.

15 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.

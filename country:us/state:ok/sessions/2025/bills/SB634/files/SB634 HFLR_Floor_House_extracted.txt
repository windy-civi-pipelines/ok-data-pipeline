9 An Act relating to impaired driving; amending Section

10 Section 6-212.7), which relates to the Impaired
Driving Prevention Advisory Committee; adding certain
11 members; updating statutory language; and providing
an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 265, O.S.L.
16 2024 (47 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 6-212.7), is amended to read as
17 follows:
18 Section 6-212.7. A. There is hereby created the Impaired
19 Driving Prevention Advisory Committee. The Committee shall be
20 comprised of:
21 1. The Commissioner of Public Safety, or a designee, who shall
22 act as the Chair chair;
23 2. The Chief of the Oklahoma Highway Patrol, or a designee;
24 3. A member appointed by the District Attorneys Council;

2 17. The Executive Director of the Department of Transportation,
3 or a designee;
4 18. The Executive Director of the Oklahoma Medical Marijuana

11 or a designee; and
12 22. The Executive Director of the Oklahoma Indigent Defense

14 B. The Department of Public Safety shall provide administrative
15 support necessary for the Committee to accomplish the goals and
16 objectives assigned by this section.
17 C. The Committee shall meet at the direction of the chair, and
18 shall meet not less than once a year, but not more than four times a
19 year. A vice chair shall be elected by the Committee from among its
20 members. The vice chair shall act as chair of the Committee in the
21 absence of the chair.
22 D. The Committee is authorized to collect, analyze, and
23 interpret relevant crash data on impaired driving and associated
24 traffic crashes. In addition, the Committee is authorized to
SB634 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 revi ew, evaluate, and monitor the impaired driving system of this
2 state and provide a network of communication and cooperation among
3 the various stakeholders to coordinate and integrate efforts and
4 resources to reduce the incidence and severity of impaired driving
5 crashes.
6 E. The Committee shall annually complete a statewide strategic
7 plan to reduce the incidents of impaired driving and impaired
8 driving crashes. The plan shall be submitted no later than December
9 31 of each year to the Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the
10 Oklahoma State Senate, and the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of
11 Representatives.
12 F. Members of the Committee who are otherwise employed by a
13 state agency or political subdivision shall serve without additional
14 compensation. Members of the Committee who are otherwise employed
15 by a state agency or political subdivision shall be entitled to
16 reimbursement for any actual and necessary traveling expenses in
17 accordance with the State Travel Reimbursement Act from the
18 employing agency.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

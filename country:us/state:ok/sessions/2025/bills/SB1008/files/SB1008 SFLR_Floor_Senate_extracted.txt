7 An Act relating to water and water rights; amending
82 O.S. 2021, Section 1020.19, which relates to
8 metering of wells; requiring all permit holders to
utilize certain system for use of water; authorizing
9 the Oklahoma Water Resources Board to implement
certain allocation to encourage conservation after
10 certain date; specifying requirements for
participation; prohibiting excessive use; exempting
11 certain wells from provisions; and providing an
effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 82 O.S. 2021, Section 1020.19, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 1020.19. Upon request of a majority of the landowners
18 residing within a basin or subbasin, the Board is authorized to
19 require that water wells be metered and that such meters as the
20 Board shall approve be utilized by the applicant and placed under
21 seal, subject to reading by the agents of the Board at any time.
22 The Board may also require that the applicant report the reading of
23 such meters at reasonable intervals.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1008 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 A. The Oklahoma Water Resources Board shall require all permit
2 holders authorized to take and use groundwater to implement a system
3 of measuring groundwater usage from all wells associated with the
4 permit. The Board shall determine which measurement systems may be
5 utilized, which shall include, but not be limited to, pump and
6 irrigation monitoring telemetry devices capable of reporting usage.
7 Such measuring systems shall be in proper operating conditions at
8 all times when groundwater is being produced. Data from the
9 measuring system shall be utilized for the annual groundwater use
10 report.
11 B. To encourage groundwater conservation, beginning January 1,
12 2026, the Board shall provide a five-year flex allocation of
13 groundwater usage based off the previously determined allocated
14 annual use of the basin or subbasin. A five-year flex allocation
15 shall not require a new permit for existing permit holders. A five-
16 year flex allocation shall be available for:
17 1. Existing permit holders for groundwater use permits in
18 basins or subbasins, based upon the maximum annual yield
19 determination at the time of the original application;
20 2. New applicants for groundwater use permits in basins or
21 subbasins based upon the current maximum annual yield determination;
22 and
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1008 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 3. New and existing applicants and temporary permit holders for
2 groundwater use permits in basins or subbasins where the maximum
3 annual yield has yet to be determined.
4 C. An applicant for a five-year flex allocation shall be
5 required to:
6 1. Submit an annual usage report of the prior year’s usage from
7 a Board-approved water measurement system; and
8 2. Pay the annual groundwater permit fee.
9 D. A five-year flex allocation shall allow the permit holder to
10 exceed the determined annual allocation in any year of the five-year
11 allocation; provided, that the applicant shall adhere to the
12 cumulative annually determined allocation of the basin or subbasin
13 over the five-year period. The permit holder shall not exceed the
14 permitted annual allocation by over two hundred percent (200%) in
15 any of the five years.
16 E. Nothing in this section shall apply to domestic wells.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

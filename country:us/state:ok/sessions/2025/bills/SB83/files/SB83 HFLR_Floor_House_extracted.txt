10 An Act relating to prisons and reformatories;
amending 57 O.S. 2021, Section 549, which relates to
11 inmate trust funds; modifying elements of exemption
to certain account; updating statutory language;
12 removing obsolete language; and providing an
effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 549, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 549. A. The State Board of Corrections shall have the
19 following powers and duties with respect to the operation of prison
20 industries, the Construction Division of the Department of
21 Corrections, and administration of inmate trust funds:
22 1. The power to make leases or other contracts consistent with
23 the operation of prison industries, and to set aside land or
24 facilities for the use of such industry;
SB83 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. The power to establish conditions for expenditures by the
2 Department of Corrections from the Industries Revolving Fund;
3 3. The power to negotiate wages and working conditions on
4 behalf of prisoners working in prison industries or prisoners
5 working in the Construction Division. Pay grades for the
6 Construction Division “on-the-job training” inmate crews shall be as
7 follows:
8 a. Pay Grade “A” - Inmate Worker,
9 b. Pay Grade “B” - Inmate Worker,
10 c. Pay Grade “C” - Apprentice, and
11 d. Pay Grade “D” - Skilled Craft;
12 4. The power to collect wages and other receipted funds on
13 behalf of the inmate, to apportion inmate wages and funds in
14 accordance with the law; and the duty to preserve those wages and
15 funds reserved for the inmate in an account for his or her benefit,
16 and to establish procedures by which the inmate can draw funds from
17 this account under the conditions and limitations and for the
18 purposes allowed by law;
19 5. The duty to establish the percentages of such wages which
20 shall be available for apportionment to inmate mandatory savings; to
21 the inmate for his or her personal use; to the lawful dependents of
22 the inmate, if any; to the victim of the inmate’s crime; for payment
23 of creditors; for payment of costs and expenses for criminal actions
24 against such inmate; and to the Department of Corrections for costs
SB83 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 of i ncarceration. Provided, that not less than twenty percent (20%)
2 of such wages shall be placed in an account, and shall be payable to
3 the prisoner upon his or her discharge; however, inmates with a
4 sentence of life or life without the possibility of parole shall be
5 exempt from this provision. Funds from this account may be used by
6 the inmate for fees or costs in filing a civil or criminal action as
7 defined in Section 151 et seq. of Title 28 of the Oklahoma Statutes
8 or for federal action as defined in pursuant to Section 1911 et seq.
9 of Title 28 of the United States Code, 28 U.S.C., Section 1911 et
10 seq.;
11 6. The power to invest funds held by the Department of
12 Corrections on behalf of each inmate in an interest-bearing account
13 with the interest accruing and payable to the Crime Victims
14 Compensation Revolving Fund, as provided in Section 142.17 of Title
15 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The interest from each inmate’s
16 savings account shall be payable to the Crime Victims Compensation
17 Revolving Fund, at such intervals as may be determined by the Board,
18 in addition to any other payments to such fund required by the
19 inmate’s sentence or otherwise by law. An inmate shall not have the
20 right, use, or control of any interest derived from any funds placed
21 in a mandatory savings account;
22 7. The power to invest funds held by the Department of
23 Corrections on behalf of each inmate in a commingled offender
24 interest-bearing account held by the Office of the State Treasurer.
SB83 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 The State Treasurer shall post interest to this account monthly.
2 The Department of Corrections, at such intervals as may be
3 determined by the Board, will credit interest to the inmate based on
4 the pro rata account balance of the inmate. Deposits into the
5 inmate interest-bearing account will only be allowed when the trust
6 fund draw account of the inmate has a balance in excess of One
7 Hundred Dollars ($100.00). Inmate mandatory savings account
8 balances will not be used to determine the eligibility of the inmate
9 to participate in the interest-bearing savings account. Inmates who
10 participate in the interest-bearing account will only be allowed to
11 transfer funds from their interest-bearing account to their draw
12 account once every ninety (90) days. All inmate transfers from the
13 interest-bearing account of the inmate to the draw account of the
14 inmate must be approved by appropriate Department staff prior to
15 transfer. All transfers of funds from an inmate interest-bearing
16 account to external recipients must be reviewed and approved by
17 appropriate Department staff prior to transfer. The Department will
18 define in policy those rules and procedures that govern inmate
19 interest-bearing account deposits and funds transfers; and
20 8. The power to invest canteen system, offender restitution,
21 and other offender-related collections by the Department of
22 Corrections in a commingled interest-bearing account held by the
23 Office of the State Treasurer and invested as prescribed by Section
24 89.2 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The State Treasurer
SB83 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 shal l post interest to this account monthly. By the fifteenth day
2 of each month, the proportionate share of the interest from the
3 canteen system, offender restitution, and other offender-related
4 collections, excluding that portion of the interest payable to the
5 Crime Victims Compensation Revolving Fund and any interest payable
6 to inmates for the inmate interest-bearing account, shall be
7 remitted to the State Treasurer from the Department of Corrections
8 for deposit into the General Revenue Fund.
9 B. The State Board of Corrections shall cause to be placed in
10 an account income from the inmate’s employment and any other income
11 or benefits accruing to or payable to and for the benefit of said
12 the inmate, including any workers’ compensation or Social Security
13 benefits.
14 1. From this account the State Board of Corrections may charge
15 for costs of incarceration any inmate working in private prison
16 industries or any other inmate for costs of incarceration not to
17 exceed fifty percent (50%) of any deposits made to said such
18 account, unless said such deposits were from a workers’ compensation
19 benefit.
20 2. From this account, the State Board of Corrections may charge
21 any inmate for costs of incarceration, an amount equivalent to one
22 hundred percent (100%) of any deposits from a workers’ compensation
23 benefit to said such account.
24
SB83 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 3. The Department of Corrections shall pay into the Crime
2 Victims Compensation Revolving Fund, as provided in Section 142.17
3 of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, an amount equal to five
4 percent (5%) of the gross wages earned by inmates working in a
5 private prison industries program, said the amount to be paid from
6 the amount deducted for cost of incarceration.
7 4. Withdrawals and deposits shall be made according to rules
8 and regulations established by the State Board of Corrections.
9 C. The Department of Corrections may assess costs of
10 incarceration against all inmates beginning on September 1, 1992.
11 Such costs shall be a debt of the inmate owed to the Department of
12 Corrections and may be collected as provided by law for collection
13 of any other civil debt. In addition to the provisions of this
14 section authorizing expenditure of inmate trust funds for costs of
15 incarceration, any monies received for costs of incarceration shall
16 be deposited in the Department of Corrections Revolving Fund.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

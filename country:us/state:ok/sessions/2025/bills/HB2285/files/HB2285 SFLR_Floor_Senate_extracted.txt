15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 508 of Title 69, unless there is
18 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the Evacuation
20 Vehicle Access Corridor Act.
21 B. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
22 fund for the Oklahoma Department of Transportation to be designated
23 the "Evacuation Vehicle Access Corridor Revolving Fund" or "EVAC
24 Fund". The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2285 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 year limitations, and shall consist of all monies directed for
2 deposit to the fund by law. All monies accruing to the credit of
3 said fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and expended
4 by the Oklahoma Department of Transportation in accordance with the
5 provisions provided in Section C of this act. Expenditures from
6 said fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer
7 against claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the
8 Office of Management and Enterprise Services for approval and
9 payment.
10 C. 1. There is hereby established the Evacuation Vehicle
11 Access Corridor Grant Program, which may also be cited as the "EVAC
12 Grant Program";
13 2. The Oklahoma Department of Transportation shall establish
14 and administer the EVAC Grant Program to support the state purpose
15 of ensuring the health and safety of the residents of Oklahoma
16 living in geographically landlocked basins by creating evacuation
17 corridors. Such program shall embody the following procedures and
18 criteria:
19 a. the Department shall create and make available to
20 counties of the state a standardized Evacuation
21 Vehicle Access Corridor grant application;
22 b. to be eligible for a grant from the EVAC Grant
23 Program, a county must demonstrate a material need to
24 construct a county road in a qualified area;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2285 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 c. grant amounts awarded under the provisions of this act
2 shall not exceed the cumulative total of funds
3 directed for deposit to the EVAC Fund created in
4 subsection B of this act.
5 D. As used in the Evacuation Vehicle Access Corridor Act:
6 1. "Landlocked" means almost or entirely surrounded by land,
7 which lacks practical entry and egress by motor vehicles;
8 2. "Qualified area" means a landlocked basin in this state with
9 no fewer than two thousand (2,000) residents, and that has one or
10 fewer roads of entry or egress maintained by a state, county, town
11 or municipality road system or systems.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
13 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
14 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
15 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
16 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

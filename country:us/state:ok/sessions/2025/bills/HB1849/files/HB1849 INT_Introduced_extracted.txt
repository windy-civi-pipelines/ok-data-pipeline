Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to children; providing income
exemption for certain individuals when determining
8 eligibility for child care subsidy program; directing
provider to provide certain notice to Department of
9 Human Services; providing that all other conditions
for eligibility apply; providing for codification;
10 and providing an effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 461 of Title 10, unless there is
16 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. Any employee who is employed by a child care facility
18 licensed pursuant to the Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing
19 Act shall have household income exempted from consideration when
20 eligibility is determined for the Child Care Subsidy Program
21 administered by the Department of Human Services.
22 B. When an employee who is employed by a licensed child care
23 facility leaves employment of the licensed provider, the provider
24
Req. No. 10260 Page 1
1 shal l provide written notice to the Department of Human Services
2 that the employee is no longer employed within thirty (30) days.
3 C. Other than the income exemption pursuant to subsection A and
4 the reporting requirements pursuant to subsection B of this section,
5 all other conditions of eligibility for the Child Care Subsidy
6 Program located in the Oklahoma Administrative Code (OAC) must be
7 followed.
8 D. Subsection A of this section shall not apply to employees
9 who are employed by a child care facility licensed pursuant to the
10 Oklahoma Child Care Facilities Licensing Act and who are eligible
11 for the Child Care Subsidy Program without household income being
12 exempted.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

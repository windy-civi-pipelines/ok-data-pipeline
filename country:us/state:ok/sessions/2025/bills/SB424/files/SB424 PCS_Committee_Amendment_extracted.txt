1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
3 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
FOR
5
6
7 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to community health workers; creating
the Oklahoma Community Health Worker Act; providing
9 short title; defining terms; directing establishment
of certificate; making certification voluntary;
10 listing powers of community health worker; providing
eligibility criteria; directing promulgation of
11 certain rules; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.1 of Title 59, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma
19 Community Health Worker Act”.
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.2 of Title 59, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 As used in the Oklahoma Community Health Worker Act:
24
Req. No. 1799 Page 1
1 1. “Certified community health worker” means a community health
2 worker to whom the State Department of Health has issued a
3 certificate to practice as a certified community health worker in
4 this state;
5 2. “Community health worker” means a frontline health worker
6 who has a shared understanding of and is a trusted member of the
7 community being served; and
8 3. “Department” means the State Department of Health.
9 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.3 of Title 59, unless there
11 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 A. The State Department of Health shall establish a certificate
13 for community health workers. This certification shall be
14 voluntary.
15 B. A certified community health worker may:
16 1. Serve as a liaison between health and social services and
17 the community, including faith-based organizations, to facilitate
18 access to services and improve the quality and cultural competence
19 of service delivery;
20 2. Assist in identifying and communicating gaps in current
21 community and health care resources; and
22 3. Build individual and community capacity by increasing health
23 knowledge and self-sufficiency through a range of activities such as
24
Req. No. 1799 Page 2
1 outr each, community education, coaching, mentoring, social support,
2 and advocacy, including efforts within faith-based settings.
3 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.4 of Title 59, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. To be eligible to apply for voluntary community health
7 worker certification, an individual shall:
8 1. Be a United States citizen or lawful permanent resident;
9 2. Be a resident of this state;
10 3. Be at least eighteen (18) years of age; and
11 4. Meet the minimum qualifications for certification
12 promulgated by the State Commissioner of Health under Section 5 of
13 this act; provided, however, the applicant shall be exempt from such
14 minimum qualifications if the applicant has provided evidence to the
15 State Department of Health of work experience as a community health
16 worker demonstrating a minimum of one thousand (1,000) practical
17 hours and a recommendation from a supervising provider.
18 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.5 of Title 59, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 The State Commissioner of Health shall promulgate rules
22 necessary to implement this act including, but not limited to, rules
23 that:
24
Req. No. 1799 Page 3
1 1. Adopt a set of standards for the approval of certified
2 community health worker initial coursework or curriculum;
3 2. Establish examination administration requirements for the
4 State Department of Health or one or more designees;
5 3. Define minimum qualifications for applicants to be
6 certified;
7 4. Establish the criteria for granting, renewing, denying,
8 suspending, and revoking a certificate to uphold health standards
9 and safety; and
10 5. Prescribe fees for the certified community health worker
11 examination, initial certification, and certification renewal.
12 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

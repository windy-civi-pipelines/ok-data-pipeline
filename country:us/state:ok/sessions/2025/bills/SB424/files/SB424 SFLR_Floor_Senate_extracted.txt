7 An Act relating to community health workers; creating
the Oklahoma Community Health Worker Act; providing
8 short title; defining terms; directing establishment
of certificate; making certification voluntary;
9 listing powers of community health worker; providing
eligibility criteria; directing promulgation of
10 certain rules; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.1 of Title 59, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma
18 Community Health Worker Act”.
19 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.2 of Title 59, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 As used in the Oklahoma Community Health Worker Act:
23 1. “Certified community health worker” means a community health
24 worker to whom the State Department of Health has issued a
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB424 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 cert ificate to practice as a certified community health worker in
2 this state;
3 2. “Community health worker” means a frontline health worker
4 who has a shared understanding of and is a trusted member of the
5 community being served; and
6 3. “Department” means the State Department of Health.
7 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.3 of Title 59, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. The State Department of Health shall establish a certificate
11 for community health workers. This certification shall be
12 voluntary.
13 B. A certified community health worker may:
14 1. Serve as a liaison between health and social services and
15 the community, including faith-based organizations, to facilitate
16 access to services and improve the quality and cultural competence
17 of service delivery;
18 2. Assist in identifying and communicating gaps in current
19 community and health care resources; and
20 3. Build individual and community capacity by increasing health
21 knowledge and self-sufficiency through a range of activities such as
22 outreach, community education, coaching, mentoring, social support,
23 and advocacy, including efforts within faith-based settings.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB424 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.4 of Title 59, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 To be eligible to apply for voluntary community health worker
5 certification, an individual shall:
6 1. Be a United States citizen or lawful permanent resident;
7 2. Be a resident of this state;
8 3. Be at least eighteen (18) years of age; and
9 4. Meet the minimum qualifications for certification
10 promulgated by the State Commissioner of Health under Section 5 of
11 this act; provided, however, the applicant shall be exempt from such
12 minimum qualifications if the applicant has provided evidence to the
13 State Department of Health of work experience as a community health
14 worker demonstrating a minimum of one thousand (1,000) practical
15 hours and a recommendation from a supervising provider.
16 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1280.5 of Title 59, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 The State Commissioner of Health shall promulgate rules
20 necessary to implement this act including, but not limited to, rules
21 that:
22 1. Adopt a set of standards for the approval of certified
23 community health worker initial coursework or curriculum;
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB424 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Establish examination administration requirements for the
2 State Department of Health or one or more designees;
3 3. Define minimum qualifications for applicants to be
4 certified;
5 4. Establish the criteria for granting, renewing, denying,
6 suspending, and revoking a certificate to uphold health standards
7 and safety; and
8 5. Prescribe fees for the certified community health worker
9 examination, initial certification, and certification renewal.
10 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

Section 2:
Section 152, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 237,
11 O.S.L. 2022 (28 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 152), which
relates to flat fee schedule; increasing certain
12 assessment; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 28 O.S. 2021, Section 152, as
17 amended by Section 1, Chapter 237, O.S.L. 2022 (28 O.S. Supp. 2024,
18 Section 152), is amended to read as follows:
19 Section 152. A. In any civil case filed in a district court,
20 the court clerk shall collect, at the time of filing, the following
21 flat fees, none of which shall ever be refundable, and which shall
22 be the only charge for court costs, except as is otherwise
23 specifically provided for by law:
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 1. Actions for divorce, alimony without divorce,
2 separate maintenance, custody or support......................$183.00
3 2. Any ancillary proceeding to modify or vacate
4 a divorce decree providing for custody or support..............$43.00
5 3. Probate and guardianship..............................$135.00
6 4. Annual guardianship report.............................$33.00
7 5. Any proceeding for sale or lease of real or
8 personal property or mineral interest in probate or
9 guardianship...................................................$43.00
10 6. Any proceeding to revoke the probate of a
11 will...........................................................$43.00
12 7. Judicial determination of death........................$58.00
13 8. Adoption..............................................$105.00
14 9. Civil actions for an amount of Ten Thousand
15 Dollars ($10,000.00) or less and condemnation.................$150.00
16 10. Civil actions for an amount of Ten Thousand
17 One Dollars ($10,001.00) or more .............................$163.00
18 11. Garnishment...........................................$23.00
19 12. Continuing wage garnishment...........................$63.00
20 13. Any other proceeding after judgment...................$33.00
21 14. All others, including but not limited to
22 actions for forcible entry and detainer, judgments
23 from all other courts, including the Workers’
24 Compensation Court.............................................$85.00
SB600 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 15. Notice of renewal of judgment.........................$23.00
2 B. In addition to the amounts collected pursuant to paragraphs
3 1, 3, 7, 8, 9, 10 and 14 of subsection A of this section, the sum of
4 Six Dollars ($6.00) shall be assessed and credited to the Law
5 Library Fund.
6 C. In addition to the amounts collected pursuant to subsections
7 A and B of this section, the sum of Twenty-five Dollars ($25.00)
8 shall be assessed and credited to the Oklahoma Court Information
9 System Revolving Fund created pursuant to Section 1315 of Title 20
10 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
11 D. In addition to the amounts collected pursuant to subsection
12 A of this section, the sum of Five Dollars ($5.00) Ten Dollars
13 ($10.00) shall be assessed and credited to the Oklahoma court-
14 appointed special advocates (OCASA).
15 E. In addition to the amounts collected pursuant to subsection
16 A of this section, the sum of Two Dollars ($2.00) shall be assessed
17 and credited as follows:
18 1. One Dollar and fifty-five cents ($1.55) of such amount shall
19 be credited to the Council on Judicial Complaints Revolving Fund;
20 and
21 2. Forty-five cents ($0.45) of such amount shall be credited to
22 the Supreme Court Revolving Fund and may be budgeted and expended by
23 the Supreme Court for expenses lawfully incurred for providing
24 qualified courtroom interpreter services in the district courts, for
SB600 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 cred entialing and training Oklahoma courtroom interpreters, and for
2 any other expenditures determined by the Supreme Court to be
3 necessary to provide language access in the district courts as
4 required by state and federal law. Payments of expenses may be made
5 after the claim or expense is approved by the Chief Justice of the
6 Supreme Court or another justice designated by the Chief Justice.
7 F. In addition to the amounts collected pursuant to paragraphs
8 1, 3, 8, 9, 10 and 14 of subsection A of this section, each county
9 may assess, upon approval by the board of county commissioners, a
10 sum not to exceed Ten Dollars ($10.00) per case to be credited to
11 the Sheriff’s Service Fee Account in the county in which the action
12 arose for the purpose of enhancing existing or providing additional
13 courthouse security.
14 G. Until November 1, 2027, in addition to the amounts collected
15 pursuant to subsection A of this section, the sum of Ten Dollars
16 ($10.00) shall be assessed and credited to the Court Clerk’s Records
17 Management and Preservation Fund created in Section 31.3 of this
18 title.
19 H. In any case in which a litigant claims to have a just cause
20 of action and that, by reason of poverty, the litigant is unable to
21 pay the fees and costs provided for in this section and is
22 financially unable to employ counsel, upon the filing of an
23 affidavit in forma pauperis executed before any officer authorized
24 by law to administer oaths to that effect and upon satisfactory
SB600 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 show ing to the court that the litigant has no means and is,
2 therefore, unable to pay the applicable fees and costs and to employ
3 counsel, no fees or costs shall be required. The opposing party or
4 parties may file with the court clerk of the court having
5 jurisdiction of the cause an affidavit similarly executed
6 contradicting the allegation of poverty. In all such cases, the
7 court shall promptly set for hearing the determination of
8 eligibility to litigate without payment of fees or costs. Until a
9 final order is entered determining that the affiant is ineligible,
10 the clerk shall permit the affiant to litigate without payment of
11 fees or costs. Any litigant executing a false affidavit or counter
12 affidavit pursuant to the provisions of this section shall be guilty
13 of perjury.
14 I. Payments to the court clerk for fees and costs assessed
15 pursuant to this section may be made by a nationally recognized
16 credit or debit card or other electronic payment method as provided
17 in paragraph 1 of subsection B of Section 151 of this title.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
19
20 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY AND PUBLIC SAFETY, dated
04/17/2025 – DO PASS.

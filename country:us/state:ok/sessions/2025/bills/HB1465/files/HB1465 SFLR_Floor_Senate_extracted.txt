9 An Act relating to schools; amending 70 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 17-113 and Section 1, Chapter 289, O.S.L.
10 2023 (70 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 18-114.15), which
relate to teacher pay computations; adding definition
11 for military service; clarifying which branches of
military service count for credit in determining
12 teacher salaries; providing an effective date; and
declaring an emergency.

15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 17-113, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 17-113. A. In determining annual increments for
19 teacher salaries and in computing Foundation Aid, time spent in
20 military service during a period of national emergency shall be
21 counted, not to exceed five (5) years prior to the current year. No
22 more than five (5) years of such military service shall be used in
23 determining eligibility or benefits under the Teachers' Retirement
24 System.

4 services, as well as the federal and state counterparts of both the
5 Air National Guard and Army National Guard.

1 B. 1. When determining the Minimum Salary Schedule, "fringe
2 benefits" shall mean all or part of retirement benefits, excluding
3 the contributions made pursuant to subsection A of Section 17-108.1
4 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title and the flexible
5 benefit allowance pursuant to Section 26-105 of Title 70 of the
6 Oklahoma Statutes this title from the flexible benefit allowance
7 funds disbursed by the State Board of Education and the State Board
8 of Career and Technology Education pursuant to Section 26-104 of
9 Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title.
10 2. If a school district intends to provide retirement benefits
11 to a teacher such that the teacher's salary would be less than the
12 amounts set forth in the minimum salary schedule specified in
13 subsection A of this section, the district shall be required to
14 provide written notification to the teacher prior to his or her
15 employment or, if already employed by the district, no later than
16 thirty (30) days prior to the date the district elects to provide
17 retirement benefits such that the teacher's salary would be less
18 than the minimum salary schedule.
19 C. Any of the degrees referred to in this section shall be from
20 a college recognized by the State Board of Education. The Board
21 shall accept teaching experience from out-of-state school districts
22 that are accredited by the State Board of Education or appropriate
23 state accrediting agency for the districts. The Board shall accept
24 teaching experience from out-of-country schools that are accredited
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1465 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 or o therwise endorsed by the appropriate national or regional
2 accrediting or endorsement authority. Out-of-country certification
3 documentation in a language other than English shall be analyzed by
4 an educational credential evaluation service in accordance with
5 industry standards and guidelines and approved by the State
6 Department of Education. The person seeking to have credit granted
7 for out-of-country teaching experience shall be responsible for all
8 costs of the analysis by a credential evaluation service. The Board
9 shall accept teaching experience from primary and secondary schools
10 that are operated by the United States Department of Defense or are
11 affiliated with the United States Department of State.
12 D. For the purpose of state salary increments and retirement,
13 no teacher shall be granted credit for more than five (5) years of
14 active duty in the military service, as defined in Section 17-113 of
15 this title, or out-of-state or out-of-country teaching experience as
16 a certified teacher or its equivalent. Nothing in this section
17 shall prohibit boards of education from crediting more years of
18 experience on district salary schedules than those allowed for state
19 purposes.
20 E. The State Board of Education shall recognize, for purposes
21 of certification and salary increments, all the years of experience
22 of a:
23 1. Certified teacher who teaches in the educational program of
24 the Department of Corrections, beginning with fiscal year 1981;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1465 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Vocational rehabilitation counselor under the Department of
2 Human Services if the counselor was employed as a certified teacher
3 by the State Department of Education when the Division of Vocational
4 Rehabilitation was transferred from the State Board of Career and
5 Technology Education or the State Board of Education to the Oklahoma
6 Public Welfare Commission on July 1, 1968;
7 3. Vocational rehabilitation counselor which were completed
8 while employed by the Department of Human Services if such counselor
9 was certified as a teacher or was eligible for certification as a
10 teacher in Oklahoma;
11 4. Certified teacher which were completed while employed by the
12 Child Study Center located at University Hospital, if the teacher
13 was certified as a teacher in Oklahoma; and
14 5. Certified school psychologist or psychometrist which were
15 completed while employed as a doctoral intern, psychological
16 assistant, or psychologist with any agency of the State of Oklahoma
17 if the experience primarily involved work with persons of school- or
18 preschool-age and if the person was, at the time the experience was
19 acquired, certified as, or eligible for certification as, a school
20 psychologist or psychometrist.
21 F. The provisions of this section shall not apply to teachers
22 who have entered into postretirement employment with a public school
23 in Oklahoma and are still receiving a monthly retirement benefit.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1465 SFLR Page 7
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 G. If a person employed as certified personnel, as defined in
2 Section 26-103 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title, by a
3 school district during the 2022-2023 school year was receiving a
4 salary above the step level indicated by the State Minimum Salary
5 Schedule for the 2022-2023 school year, the person shall receive a
6 salary increase amount equal to the amount indicated in subsection A
7 of this section for the step level indicated for the person,
8 provided they remain employed by the same district, unless the hours
9 or the duties of the certified personnel are reduced
10 proportionately.
11 H. If a school district does not receive Foundation or Salary
12 Incentive Aid pursuant to Section 18-200.1 of Title 70 of the
13 Oklahoma Statutes this title, funds shall be allocated by the State
14 Board of Education to implement the salary increases indicated in
15 subsection A of this section.
16 I. Persons employed as classroom instructional employees of
17 technology center school districts supervised by the State Board of
18 Career and Technology Education shall receive a salary increase
19 amount equal to the amount indicated in subsection A of this section
20 for the step level indicated for the person, provided they remain
21 employed by the same technology center school district, unless the
22 hours or the duties of the classroom instructional employees are
23 reduced proportionately.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1465 SFLR Page 8
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 J. Persons employed as correctional teachers or vocational
2 instructors by the Department of Corrections pursuant to Section
3 510.6a of Title 57 of the Oklahoma Statutes or persons employed as
4 teachers by the Office of Juvenile Affairs shall receive a salary
5 increase amount equal to the amount indicated in subsection A of
6 this section for the step level indicated for the person, provided
7 they remain employed by the same Department of Corrections or Office
8 of Juvenile Affairs facility, unless the hours or the duties of the
9 correctional teachers, vocational instructors, or teachers are
10 reduced proportionately.
11 K. Persons employed as teachers by the State Department of
12 Rehabilitation Services shall receive a salary increase amount equal
13 to the amount indicated in subsection A of this section for the step
14 level indicated for the person, provided they remain employed by the
15 State Department of Rehabilitation Services, unless the hours or the
16 duties of the teachers are reduced proportionately.
17 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
18 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
19 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
20 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
21 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

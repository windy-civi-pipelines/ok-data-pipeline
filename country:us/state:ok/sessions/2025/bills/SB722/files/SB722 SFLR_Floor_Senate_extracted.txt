13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 1-3, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 1-3. For the purposes of the Oklahoma Agricultural
16 Code, unless the context indicates otherwise:
17 1. “Authorized agent” means a person who has been authorized by
18 the State Board of Agriculture to act on behalf of the Board in
19 making investigations, inspections, performing other services, or
20 doing any particular act or acts which have been vested by the
21 Oklahoma Agricultural Code in the Board. A written or printed
22 commission signed by the President of the Board shall be proof that
23 the holder has lawful authority to act on behalf of the Board in
24 implementing the Oklahoma Agricultural Code;

1 2. “Beef” means the flesh of a bovine animal;
2 3. “Beef product” means the edible products produced in whole
3 or in part from beef, excluding milk and milk products;
4 4. “Board” means the State Board of Agriculture;
5 5. “Code” means the Oklahoma Agricultural Code;
6 6. “Department” means the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture,
7 Food, and Forestry and its employees, officers, and divisions.
8 Whenever the name “Department of Agriculture” Department of
9 Agriculture appears in any law, contract or other document, it shall
10 be deemed to refer to the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food,
11 and Forestry;
12 7. “Director” means the Director of a division established in
13 the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry;
14 8. “License” means a written document issued by the Board
15 granting authority to a person to engage in a business, occupation,
16 or activity;
17 9. “Livestock” or “animals” means any cattle, bison, horses,
18 sheep, goats, asses, mules, swine, domesticated rabbits, and
19 chickens, turkeys, and other domesticated fowl, and any animal or
20 bird in captivity;
21 10. “Official identification” means any official method of
22 identification approved by USDA the United States Department of
23 Agriculture, as described by 9 C.F.R., § Section 86.1, or the State
24 Veterinarian; provided, that no official identification shall be

1 used to monitor electronically any animal of this state. Official
2 identification for specific species may be further defined within
3 the applicable section of the Oklahoma Administrative Code.
4 Backtags shall not be considered official identification unless the
5 animal is shipped directly to slaughter. The term “official ear
6 tag” is synonymous with “official identification” official
7 identification;
8 11. “Permit” means a written document issued by the Board
9 giving consent for a person to engage in an activity;
10 12. “Person” means the state, any municipality, political
11 subdivision, institution, individual, public or private corporation,
12 partnership, association, firm, company, public trust, joint-stock

17 14. “Pork products” means a product or byproduct produced in
18 whole or in part from pork;
19 15. “President” means the President of the State Board of
20 Agriculture. The President of the State Board of Agriculture shall
21 also be designated as the Commissioner of Agriculture;
22 16. “Producer” means any person planting, raising, growing, or
23 harvesting agricultural products;
24

1 17. “Quarantine” means a written document issued by the Board
2 to restrict the movement of animals, birds, plants, or agricultural
3 commodities into or out of a specified area for the control or
4 prevention of diseases or pests; and
5 18. “Stop sale order” or “stop use order” means a written or
6 printed order signed by the President or authorized agent of the
7 Board, prohibiting the sale, offering for sale, exposure for sale,
8 or use of any agricultural product, article, device, service, or
9 commodity covered by the Oklahoma Agricultural Code.
10 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 4-20, is
11 amended to read as follows:
12 Section 4-20. A. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food,
13 and Forestry shall be the official animal identification agency of
14 the state and shall be the official agency of the state in any
15 dealings between this state and the United States Department of
16 Agriculture, any other federal agency, or any agency or person of
17 this or another state on matters pertaining to animal
18 identification.
19 B. It is the intent of the Oklahoma State Legislature to direct
20 and authorize the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
21 Forestry to develop, implement, and administer an Oklahoma Animal
22 Identification Program that achieves the following goals:
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB722 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 1. Enhance disease preparedness by rapidly identifying animals
2 exposed to disease, thus allowing quick detection, containment, and
3 elimination of disease threats;
4 2. Promote continued confidence in animal products and to
5 protect the health status of Oklahoma’s herds and flocks;
6 3. Identify infected and exposed premises, animals, and groups
7 of animals;
8 4. Develop a comprehensive infrastructure that utilizes state-
9 of-the-art national and international standards with the best
10 available and practical technologies for the collection and
11 recording of livestock and food animal movements; and
12 5. Be dynamic and flexible, and incorporate new and proven
13 technologies as they become available.
14 C. The Department shall be authorized to promulgate rules for
15 the implementation and administration of the Oklahoma Animal
16 Identification Program, if one of the following occurs:
17 1. The United States Department of Agriculture issues proposed
18 or final rules or requirements for the implementation of a national
19 animal identification or premises registration program;
20 2. The United States Congress enacts requirements for a
21 national animal identification or premises registration system; or
22 3. Another state establishes requirements for animal
23 identification or premises registration affecting the importation of
24 livestock from Oklahoma.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB722 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. Any rules promulgated by the Department pursuant to this
2 section that may be reasonably necessary to implement the Oklahoma
3 Animal Identification Program may include the following:
4 1. Premises identification;
5 2. Individual animal identification;
6 3. Group and lot animal identification;
7 4. Specifications and standards for the identification
8 technologies used to track and trace animal movements; provided,
9 that the rule shall not be used to monitor electronically any cattle
10 or bison younger than eighteen (18) months of age of this state;
11 5. Record keeping; and
12 6. Protecting and improving the health of Oklahoma livestock
13 and food animals.
14 E. The Department may charge fees; however, the fees shall be
15 limited to the actual costs of the Department for the implementation
16 and administration of the Oklahoma Animal Identification Program.
17 F. Pursuant to this section and rules promulgated pursuant
18 thereto, any data or records provided to the Department by persons
19 regarding premises or animal identification, including the name and
20 address of the provider, shall be kept confidential except in the
21 following circumstances:
22 1. Federal law requires the information for a National Animal
23 Identification national animal identification program;
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB722 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. The Department deems release of confidential information to
2 other state and federal agencies is necessary for disease control
3 and disease traceback;
4 3. The information is needed to assist law enforcement
5 officials in livestock recovery and theft investigations; and
6 4. The Department may release information as needed to assist
7 in criminal investigations or tracebacks concerning violations of
8 state or federal pharmacy acts and drug residues.
9 G. In the event that information regarding premises or animal
10 identification is provided to other individuals or entities, the
11 information shall be specific and not cumulative.
12 H. A court shall quash any subpoena commanding the disclosure
13 of confidential information or records of the Department.
14 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
15 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON AGRICULTURE AND WILDLIFE

FOR
5
6
7 An Act relating to state government; creating the
State-Sponsored Persecution Prohibition Act;
8 providing short title; prohibiting certain actions by
state agencies; providing for civil action and
9 penalties; providing for noncodification; providing
for codification; and providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
14 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
15 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “State-Sponsored
16 Persecution Prohibition Act”.
17 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 13001 of Title 74, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. No state agency shall for personal, political, or
21 ideological reasons:
22 1. Maliciously investigate a law-abiding private business,
23 farmer, rancher, or taxpayer with the intent to intimidate or
24 harass;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1015 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Threaten or attempt to threaten any law-abiding private
2 business, farmer, rancher, or taxpayer with punitive regulation or
3 retaliation for questioning the agency or reporting misconduct of
4 the agency; or
5 3. Excessively and repetitively harass through communication a
6 law-abiding private business, farmer, rancher, or taxpayer.
7 B. Any agency found to have knowingly and willingly violated
8 this section of law shall be subject to civil action by the person
9 or persons directly impacted by the willful violation of the agency
10 and shall be entitled to seek damages and attorney fees.
11 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
12 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON RETIREMENT AND GOVERNMENT

12 An Act relating to crimes and punishments; amending
21 O.S. 2021, Sections 1902 and 1903, which relate to
13 the Bus Passenger Safety Act; modifying scope of
certain definitions; adding definition; expanding
14 scope of unlawful acts; updating statutory language
and references; and declaring an emergency.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1902, is
19 amended to read as follows:
20 Section 1902. As used in the Bus Passenger Safety Act:
21 1. “Bus” means a vehicle designed to carry passengers that is
22 part of a network of passenger vehicles for use by the public,
23 running on a regular schedule of routes, times, and fares;
24
SB562 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. “Bus transportation company” or “company” means any person
2 or governmental entity providing for-hire transport to passengers or
3 cargo by bus rolling stock transit vehicles upon the roads, streets,
4 highways, public or dedicated rights-of-way, and turnpikes of this
5 state;
6 3. “Deadly or dangerous weapon” includes all weapons listed in
7 Section 1287 of this title, and any other weapon capable of
8 inflicting serious bodily injury, except for a weapon carried for
9 lawful self-defense in compliance under the Oklahoma Self Defense
10 Self-Defense Act;
11 4. “Passenger” means any person served by the bus
12 transportation company; and
13 5. “Rolling stock transit vehicles” include buses, vans, cars,
14 railcars, locomotives, trolley cars, ferry boats, and vehicles used
15 for transporting the public; and
16 6. “Terminal” means a bus station, stop, platform, or depot or
17 any facility operated or leased by or operated on behalf of a bus
18 transportation company. This term shall include a reasonable area
19 immediately adjacent to any designated stop along the route traveled
20 by any bus rolling stock transit vehicle operated by a bus
21 transportation company and parking lots or parking areas adjacent to
22 a terminal.
23 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1903, is
24 amended to read as follows:
SB562 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Section 1903. A. No person shall by force or violence, or
2 threat of force or violence, seize or exercise control of any bus
3 rolling stock transit vehicle. Any person violating this subsection
4 shall be guilty of a felony and shall, upon conviction, be punished
5 by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of Corrections for
6 not more than twenty (20) years, or by a fine of not more than
7 Twenty Thousand Dollars ($20,000.00), or by both such fine and
8 imprisonment.
9 B. In addition, no No person shall intimidate, threaten,
10 assault, or batter any driver, attendant, guard, or passenger of any
11 bus with intent to violate subsection A of this section rolling
12 stock transit vehicle or cause disruption to the provided service or
13 cause harm to any person. Any person violating this subsection
14 shall be guilty of a felony and shall, upon conviction, be punished
15 by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of Corrections for
16 not more than ten (10) years, or by a fine of not more than Five
17 Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00), or by both such fine and imprisonment.
18 C. In addition, any Any person violating subsection A or B of
19 this section using a dangerous or deadly weapon shall be guilty of a
20 felony, and shall, upon conviction, be punished by imprisonment in
21 the custody of the Department of Corrections for not more than
22 twenty (20) years, or by a fine of not more than Twenty Thousand
23 Dollars ($20,000.00), or by both such fine and imprisonment.
24
SB562 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 D. It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge any firearm
2 into or within any bus rolling stock transit vehicle, terminal, or
3 other transportation facility, unless such action is determined to
4 have been in defensive force resulting from reasonable fear of
5 imminent peril of death or great bodily harm to himself or herself
6 or another. Such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a
7 felony punishable by a fine of not more than Five Thousand Dollars
8 ($5,000.00) or, by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of
9 Corrections for not more than five (5) years, or by both such fine
10 and imprisonment.
11 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
12 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
13 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
14 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
15
16 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY AND PUBLIC SAFETY
OVERSIGHT, dated 04/17/2025 - DO PASS, As Amended.

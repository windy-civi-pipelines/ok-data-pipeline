10 An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending Section 3, Chapter 269, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S.
11 Supp. 2024, Section 1688.1), which relates to
continuing education requirements; modifying license
12 renewal requirements; and providing an effective
date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 3, Chapter 269, O.S.L.
17 2024 (59 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 1688.1), is amended to read as
18 follows:
19 Section 1688.1. A. 1. Effective January 1, 2026, no
20 contractor or journeyman license shall be renewed unless the
21 licensee has completed twelve (12) hours of continuing education
22 every three (3) years or thirty-six (36) months preceding the
23 expiration date of the license or registration certificate license
24 renewal. If a license expires before the licensee completes the

5 education requirement for the expired license is met, along with any
6 other requirements, the expired license may be renewed. Twelve (12)
7 hours of continuing education will have to be completed in order to
8 meet the continuing education requirement for the subsequent thirty-
9 six-month period. The continuing education course and instructor
10 shall be approved in advance by the Committee of Electrical
11 Examiners. Exceptions to advance approval, or post-course approval,

16 courses shall be at the discretion of the course provider and may be
17 scheduled during the workday at the discretion of the course

19 hours of codes and revisions adopted by the Oklahoma Uniform

21 include the study of: electrical circuit theory and calculations,

23 electrical circuits and devices, control systems, alternative energy
24 systems including energy storage, safety related to the electrical

7 to reregister unless the apprentice registrant has completed three

16 the apprentice is allowed to reregister after having an expired
17 registration. Three (3) hours of continuing education will still

19 requirement for the subsequent registration year. The continuing

1 seve n (7) days of the course. The location of continuing education
2 courses shall be at the discretion of the course provider and may be
3 scheduled during the workday at the discretion of the course
4 provider. The continuing education material shall cover a
5 combination of the codes and revisions adopted by the OUBCC,
6 Oklahoma electrical industry regulations as well as the rules of the
7 Construction Industries Board, and "electrical safety" as defined by
8 NFPA 70E, other trade subject matters approved by the Committee, or
9 approved continuing education for the contractor or journeyman.
10 Notwithstanding the provisions of this subsection, if an apprentice
11 is a student apprentice or is enrolled and attending an approved
12 course, the continuing education shall not be required.
13 B. The Construction Industries Board shall amend its rules,
14 wherever applicable, to conform to the provisions of the Electrical
15 License Act.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

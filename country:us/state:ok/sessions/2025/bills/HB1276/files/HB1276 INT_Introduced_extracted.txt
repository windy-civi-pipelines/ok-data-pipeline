1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6
7 AS INTRODUCED
8 An Act relating to schools; requiring district boards
of education to adopt a policy prohibiting student
9 cell phone and smartwatch use under certain
circumstances; requiring exceptions for emergencies;
10 allowing board approval to opt out; requiring annual
approval of opt out policies; defining terms;
11 providing for codification; providing an effective
date; and declaring an emergency.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 24-162 of Title 70, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. Before the start of the 2025-2026 school year, each school
19 district board of education shall adopt a policy to prohibit the use
20 of cell phones and smartwatches by students enrolled in the district
21 during the entirety of the school day and while present on school
22 grounds.
23 B. District policies described in subsection A of this section
24 shall incorporate provisions for emergency use including, but not
Req. No. 10157 Page 1
1 limi ted to, use to support medical issues documented by a licensed
2 medical professional.
3 C. Notwithstanding subsection A of this section, a school
4 district board of education may vote to adopt a policy allowing
5 student cell phone or smartwatch usage during the school day. Any
6 such policy shall be reviewed and approved by the district board on
7 an annual basis.
8 D. For purposes of this section:
9 1. "Cell phone" means a portable wireless telephone device that
10 is designed to send or receive transmissions through a cellular
11 radiotelephone service;
12 2. "School day" means the entirety of the day from the first
13 bell indicating the beginning of classroom instruction through the
14 final bell indicating the end of classroom instruction; and
15 3. "Smartwatch" means a wearable electronic device, designed to
16 be worn on the wrist, featuring functionalities such as
17 notifications and app support.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
19 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
20 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
21 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
22 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

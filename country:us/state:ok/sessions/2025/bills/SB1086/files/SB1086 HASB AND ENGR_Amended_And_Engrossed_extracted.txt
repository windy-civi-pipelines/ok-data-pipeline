17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 4-120, as
19 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 357, O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp.
20 2024, Section 4-120), is amended to read as follows:
21 Section 4-120. A. The registration of any registered voter
22 shall be canceled only for one of the following reasons:
23 1. Written notice from the voter pursuant to Section 4-120.1 of
24 this title;
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1086 Page 2
1 2. Death;
2 3. Conviction of a felony;
3 4. Judicial determination of mental incapacitation under Title
4 30 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
5 5. Registration in another county or state;
6 6. Failure to respond to a confirmation of address mailing and
7 failure to vote as prescribed in Section 4-120.2 of this title;
8 7. The surrendering of the voter’s Oklahoma driver license to
9 Service Oklahoma upon being issued a driver license in another
10 state; or
11 8. Being excused from jury duty for not being a citizen of the
12 United States; or
13 9. Determination that the voter is not a citizen of the United
14 States.
15 B. For purposes of this section, paragraphs 5 and 7 of
16 subsection A of this section shall constitute confirmation from the
17 voter to cancel his or her voter registration due to a change of
18 residence.
19 C. A list of voter registrations that were canceled during the
20 previous twenty-four (24) months, and the reason for the
21 cancellation, shall be made available to the public.
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4-122 of Title 26, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1086 Page 3
1 A. The Secretary of the State Election Board may compare the
2 list of registered voters in this state with the database of any
3 federal, state, or local agency, board, or commission that maintains
4 information regarding the citizenship of persons residing in this
5 state.
6 B. If a registered voter definitively matches a person who is
7 recorded in a database described in subsection A of this section as
8 not being a citizen of the United States, the Secretary or his or
9 her designee shall send a notice of such finding to the voter. The
10 voter shall, within thirty (30) days from the date the notice is
11 sent to the voter, present proof of citizenship to the secretary of
12 the county election board in the county where the voter is
13 registered. Voters may present legible copies of qualifying
14 documents in person or by mail, electronic mail, or any other secure
15 method approved by the Secretary.
16 C. If a voter fails to present proof of citizenship within the
17 prescribed time to the county election board or otherwise provides
18 notice that the voter is not a citizen, then the voter’s
19 registration shall be canceled, and the voter shall be reported to
20 the appropriate district attorney and United States attorney.
21 D. For purposes of this section, “proof of citizenship” shall
22 mean any citizenship-confirming document that meets the minimum
23 standards of the REAL ID Act of 2005, Public Law No. 109-13, Section
24 202(c).
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1086 Page 4
1 E. The Secretary shall promulgate rules and procedures as
2 necessary to effectuate the provisions of this section.
3 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 7-103.2, as
4 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 264, O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp.
5 2024, Section 7-103.2), is amended to read as follows:
6 Section 7-103.2. A. 1. County election boards shall maintain
7 a current list of all registered voters in each precinct, which will
8 reflect the address and party affiliation of each voter. The list
9 shall be public information subject to the requirements described in
10 subsection B of this section.
11 2. The list shall only be shared with a court system if that
12 court system agrees to regularly provide the names of persons
13 convicted of a felony in that court system to the Secretary of the
14 State Election Board and the appropriate secretary of a county
15 election board, or if the court system is required by state law to
16 provide such information.
17 3. The list shall note any voter for whom a first-class mailing
18 from the county election board or the State Election Board was
19 returned undeliverable.
20 B. 1. The Secretary of the State Election Board shall ensure
21 that an electronic copy of the voter registration list described in
22 subsection A of this section is made available to the public, free
23 of charge, to the following:
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1086 Page 5
1 a. bona fide residents of this state who are citizens of
2 the United States,
3 b. official representatives of political parties
4 recognized by the laws of this state,
5 c. candidates for office in this state and their official
6 representatives, or
7 d. other persons authorized by state law.
8 2. Any person accessing the voter registration list shall be
9 required to complete an application form approved by the Secretary
10 that shall include:
11 a. the legal name, address of residence in this state,
12 telephone number, and electronic mail address of the
13 person,
14 b. an attestation under penalty of perjury that the
15 person meets the legal requirements to access the
16 list, and
17 c. any additional information deemed necessary by the
18 Secretary.
19 3. It shall be unlawful for the recipient of a voter
20 registration list to use such list for any commercial marketing or
21 advertising purposes. Provided, for the purposes of this section, a
22 commercial purpose shall not include the use of the voter list by
23 campaigns, political parties, government officials, or public
24 opinion pollsters to contact or interact with voters.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1086 Page 6
1 4. It shall be unlawful for the recipient of a voter
2 registration list to knowingly make the list available or provide
3 such list to a foreign national, foreign commercial interest, or
4 foreign government entity.
5 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025."
6 Passed the House of Representatives the 6th day of May, 2025.

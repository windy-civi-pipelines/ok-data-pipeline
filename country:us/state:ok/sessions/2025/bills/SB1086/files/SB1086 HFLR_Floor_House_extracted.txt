1 BE I T ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
2 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 4-120, as
3 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 357, O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp.
4 2024, Section 4-120), is amended to read as follows:
5 Section 4-120. A. The registration of any registered voter
6 shall be canceled only for one of the following reasons:
7 1. Written notice from the voter pursuant to Section 4-120.1 of
8 this title;
9 2. Death;
10 3. Conviction of a felony;
11 4. Judicial determination of mental incapacitation under Title
12 30 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
13 5. Registration in another county or state;
14 6. Failure to respond to a confirmation of address mailing and
15 failure to vote as prescribed in Section 4-120.2 of this title;
16 7. The surrendering of the voter’s Oklahoma driver license to
17 Service Oklahoma upon being issued a driver license in another
18 state; or
19 8. Being excused from jury duty for not being a citizen of the
20 United States; or
21 9. Determination that the voter is not a citizen of the United
22 States.
23 B. For purposes of this section, paragraphs 5 and 7 of
24 subsection A of this section shall constitute confirmation from the

1 vote r to cancel his or her voter registration due to a change of
2 residence.
3 C. A list of voter registrations that were canceled during the
4 previous twenty-four (24) months, and the reason for the
5 cancellation, shall be made available to the public.
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4-122 of Title 26, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. The Secretary of the State Election Board may compare the
10 list of registered voters in this state with the database of any
11 federal, state, or local agency, board, or commission that maintains
12 information regarding the citizenship of persons residing in this
13 state.
14 B. If a registered voter definitively matches a person who is
15 recorded in a database described in subsection A of this section as
16 not being a citizen of the United States, the Secretary or his or
17 her designee shall send a notice of such finding to the voter. The
18 voter shall, within thirty (30) days from the date the notice is
19 sent to the voter, present proof of citizenship to the secretary of
20 the county election board in the county where the voter is
21 registered. Voters may present legible copies of qualifying
22 documents in person or by mail, electronic mail, facsimile, or any
23 other secure method approved by the Secretary.

1 C. If a voter fails to present proof of citizenship within the
2 prescribed time to the county election board or otherwise provides
3 notice that the voter is not a citizen, then the voter’s
4 registration shall be canceled, and the voter shall be reported to
5 the appropriate district attorney and United States attorney.
6 D. For purposes of this section, “proof of citizenship” shall
7 mean any citizenship-confirming document that meets the minimum
8 standards of the REAL ID Act of 2005, Public Law No. 109-13, Section
9 202(c).
10 E. The Secretary shall promulgate rules and procedures as
11 necessary to effectuate the provisions of this section.
12 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 7-103.2, as
13 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 264, O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp.
14 2024, Section 7-103.2), is amended to read as follows:
15 Section 7-103.2. A. 1. County election boards shall maintain
16 a current list of all registered voters in each precinct, which will
17 reflect the address and party affiliation of each voter. The list
18 shall be public information.
19 2. The list shall only be shared with a court system if that
20 court system agrees to regularly provide the names of persons
21 convicted of a felony in that court system to the Secretary of the
22 State Election Board and the appropriate secretary of a county
23 election board, or if the court system is required by state law to
24 provide such information.

3 3. It shall be unlawful for the recipient of a voter
4 registration list to use such list for any commercial marketing or
5 advertising purposes. Provided, for the purposes of this section, a
6 commercial purpose shall not include the use of the voter list by
7 campaigns, political parties, government officials, or public
8 opinion pollsters to contact or interact with voters. Under no

10 Internet.

13 national, foreign commercial interest, or foreign government entity.

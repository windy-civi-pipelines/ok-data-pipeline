9 An Act relating to wildlife; amending 29 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 7-304, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 19,
10 O.S.L. 2022 (29 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 7-304),
which relates to wildlife refuges and Wildlife
11 Management Areas; requiring nonresident hunters to
receive permission from the Oklahoma Wildlife
12 Conservation Commission to use certain areas;
authorizing the Commission to create certain system
13 for permitting; requiring permit fee; providing for
promulgation of rules; updating statutory language;
14 and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 29 O.S. 2021, Section 7-304, as
19 amended by Section 1, Chapter 19, O.S.L. 2022 (29 O.S. Supp. 2024,
20 Section 7-304), is amended to read as follows:
21 Section 7-304. A. Except as otherwise provided by law, no
22 person may enter upon any state or federal wildlife refuge or
23 Wildlife Management Area with a dog, a gun, or a bow.
24
SB448 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. Exceptions to the above provisions are when the Oklahoma
2 Wildlife Conservation Commission and/or the Oklahoma Tourism and
3 Recreation Commission may by resolution permit:
4 1. The holding of field trials by duly authorized sportsmen’s
5 clubs; or
6 2. Such hunting, killing, or trapping of wildlife from such
7 refuge or Wildlife Management Area; provided, however, that Lake
8 Murray State Park shall not be utilized for the killing or trapping
9 of natural wildlife and shall remain a wildlife preserve.
10 C. 1. Upon authorization for the hunting, killing, or trapping
11 of wildlife from any such refuge or Wildlife Management Area by the
12 Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Commission pursuant to subsection B
13 of this section, the Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Commission shall
14 require nonresidents to schedule with the Department of Wildlife
15 Conservation in order to receive written authorization to use such
16 refuge or Wildlife Management Area.
17 2. The Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Commission shall create a
18 lottery system as needed for the distribution of permits authorizing
19 nonresidents to hunt, kill, or trap wildlife from such refuge or
20 Wildlife Management Area and shall charge a permit fee not to exceed
21 One Hundred Dollars ($100.00).
22 3. The Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Commission shall
23 promulgate any rules necessary to implement the provisions of this
24 section.
SB448 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 D. Any person convicted of violating the provisions of this
2 section shall be punished by a fine of not less than One Hundred
3 Dollars ($100.00) nor more than One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00), or
4 by imprisonment in the county jail for not less than ten (10) days
5 nor more than thirty (30) days, or by both such fine and
6 imprisonment for the first violation. A subsequent violation shall
7 result in the suspension of privileges to hunt, kill, or trap within
8 such refuge or Wildlife Management Area.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
10

8 An Act relating to civil procedure; enacting the
Uniform Collaborative Law Act; defining terms;
9 providing for applicability of act; specifying
requirements of collaborative law participation
10 agreements; providing for beginning and ending of
collaborative law process; requiring notice;
11 providing for termination with or without cause;
providing for continuation of collaborative law
12 process under certain conditions; providing for
procedures before certain tribunals; prescribing
13 procedures; providing for status report; providing
for issuance of emergency orders; authorizing
14 approval of certain agreements; providing procedures
related to disqualification of attorneys; providing
15 for representation without fees; providing standard
for income qualifications; providing for
16 disqualification of attorneys representing
governmental entities; providing for disclosure of
17 information related to collaborative matters;
providing for inapplicability of act to certain
18 professional standards; imposing duties on attorney
with respect to collaborative law participation
19 agreements; requiring certain inquiry related to
coercive or violent relationships; prescribing
20 procedures; providing for confidentiality; providing
for privilege with respect to collaborative law
21 communications; providing for waiver of privilege;
limiting scope of privilege; providing for authority
22 of tribunal in case of noncompliance; providing for
uniform application; providing for effect of act with
23 respect to other laws; providing for codification;
and providing an effective date.

8 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3302 of Title 12, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 DEFINITIONS.
12 As used in this act:
13 1. “Collaborative law communication” means a statement, whether
14 oral or in a record, or verbal or nonverbal, that:
15 a. is made to conduct, participate in, continue, or
16 reconvene a collaborative law process, and
17 b. occurs after the parties sign a collaborative law
18 participation agreement and before the collaborative
19 law process is concluded;
20 2. “Collaborative law participation agreement” means an
21 agreement by persons to participate in a collaborative law process;
22 3. “Collaborative law process” means a procedure intended to
23 resolve a collaborative matter without intervention by a tribunal in
24 which persons:

1 a. sign a collaborative law participation agreement, and
2 b. are represented by collaborative lawyers;
3 4. “Collaborative lawyer” means a lawyer who represents a party
4 in a collaborative law process;
5 5. “Collaborative matter” means a dispute, transaction, claim,
6 problem, or issue for resolution, including a dispute, claim, or
7 issue in a proceeding, which is described in a collaborative law
8 participation agreement;
9 6. “Law firm” means:
10 a. lawyers who practice law together in a partnership,
11 professional corporation, sole proprietorship, limited
12 liability company, or association, and
13 b. lawyers employed in a legal services organization, or
14 the legal department of a corporation or other
15 organization, or the legal department of a government
16 or governmental subdivision, agency, or
17 instrumentality;
18 7. “Nonparty participant” means a person, other than a party
19 and the party’s collaborative lawyer, that participates in a
20 collaborative law process;
21 8. “Party” means a person that signs a collaborative law
22 participation agreement and whose consent is necessary to resolve a
23 collaborative matter;

21 COLLABORATIVE LAW PARTICIPATION AGREEMENT; REQUIREMENTS.

23 1. Be in a record;
24 2. Be signed by the parties;

1 3. State the parties’ intention to resolve a collaborative

3 4. Describe the nature and scope of the matter;

12 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3305 of Title 12, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 BEGINNING AND CONCLUDING COLLABORATIVE LAW PROCESS.
16 A. A collaborative law process begins when the parties sign a
17 collaborative law participation agreement.
18 B. A tribunal may not order a party to participate in a
19 collaborative law process over that party’s objection.
20 C. A collaborative law process is concluded by a:
21 1. Resolution of a collaborative matter as evidenced by a
22 signed record;
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2117 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Resolution of a part of the collaborative matter, evidenced
2 by a signed record, in which the parties agree that the remaining
3 parts of the matter will not be resolved in the process; or
4 3. Termination of the process.
5 D. A collaborative law process terminates:
6 1. When a party gives notice to other parties in a record that
7 the process is ended;
8 2. When a party:
9 a. begins a proceeding related to a collaborative matter
10 without the agreement of all parties, or
11 b. in a pending proceeding related to the matter:
12 (1) initiates a pleading, motion, order to show
13 cause, or request for a conference with the
14 tribunal,
15 (2) requests that the proceeding be put on the
16 tribunal’s active calendar, or
17 (3) takes similar action requiring notice to be sent
18 to the parties; or
19 3. Except as otherwise provided by subsection G of this
20 section, when a party discharges a collaborative lawyer or a
21 collaborative lawyer withdraws from further representation of a
22 party.
23 E. A party’s collaborative lawyer shall give prompt notice to
24 all other parties in a record of a discharge or withdrawal.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2117 SFLR Page 7
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 F. A party may terminate a collaborative law process with or
2 without cause.
3 G. Notwithstanding the discharge or withdrawal of a
4 collaborative lawyer, a collaborative law process continues, if not
5 later than thirty (30) days after the date that the notice of the
6 discharge or withdrawal of a collaborative lawyer required by
7 subsection E of this section is sent to the parties:
8 1. The unrepresented party engages a successor collaborative
9 lawyer; and
10 2. In a signed record:
11 a. the parties consent to continue the process by
12 reaffirming the collaborative law participation
13 agreement,
14 b. the agreement is amended to identify the successor
15 collaborative lawyer, and
16 c. the successor collaborative lawyer confirms the
17 lawyer’s representation of a party in the
18 collaborative process.
19 H. A collaborative law process does not conclude if, with the
20 consent of the parties, a party requests a tribunal to approve a
21 resolution of the collaborative matter or any part thereof as
22 evidenced by a signed record.
23 I. A collaborative law participation agreement may provide
24 additional methods of concluding a collaborative law process.

2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3306 of Title 12, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 PROCEEDINGS PENDING BEFORE TRIBUNAL; STATUS REPORT.
5 A. Persons in a proceeding pending before a tribunal may sign a
6 collaborative law participation agreement to seek to resolve a
7 collaborative matter related to the proceeding. The parties shall
8 file promptly with the tribunal a notice of the agreement after it
9 is signed. Subject to subsection C of this section and Sections 7
10 and 8 of this act, the filing operates as an application for a stay
11 of the proceeding.
12 B. The parties shall file promptly with the tribunal notice in
13 a record when a collaborative law process concludes. The stay of
14 the proceeding under subsection A of this section is lifted when the
15 notice is filed. The notice may not specify any reason for
16 termination of the process.
17 C. A tribunal in which a proceeding is stayed under subsection
18 A of this section may require the parties and collaborative lawyers
19 to provide a status report on the collaborative law process and the
20 proceeding. A status report may include only information on whether
21 the process is ongoing or concluded. It may not include a report,
22 assessment, evaluation, recommendation, finding, or other
23 communication regarding a collaborative law process or collaborative
24 law matter.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2117 SFLR Page 9
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. A tribunal may not consider a communication made in
2 violation of subsection C of this section.
3 E. A tribunal shall provide parties notice and an opportunity
4 to be heard before dismissing a proceeding in which a notice of
5 collaborative process is filed based on delay or failure to
6 prosecute.

4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3310 of Title 12, unless there

6 LOW INCOME PARTIES.
7 A. The disqualification of subsection A of Section 9 of this
8 act applies to a collaborative lawyer representing a party with or
9 without fee.
10 B. After a collaborative law process concludes, another lawyer
11 in a law firm with which a collaborative lawyer disqualified under
12 subsection A of Section 9 of this act is associated may represent a
13 party without fee in the collaborative matter or a matter related to
14 the collaborative matter if:
15 1. The party has an annual income that qualifies the party for
16 free legal representation under the criteria established by the law
17 firm for free legal representation;
18 2. The collaborative law participation agreement so provides;
19 and
20 3. The collaborative lawyer is isolated from any participation
21 in the collaborative matter or a matter related to the collaborative
22 matter through procedures within the law firm which are reasonably
23 calculated to isolate the collaborative lawyer from such
24 participation.

3 SECTION 15. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3315 of Title 12, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 COERCIVE OR VIOLENT RELATIONSHIP.
7 A. Before a prospective party signs a collaborative law
8 participation agreement, a prospective collaborative lawyer shall
9 make reasonable inquiry whether the prospective party has a history
10 of a coercive or violent relationship with another prospective
11 party.
12 B. Throughout a collaborative law process, a collaborative
13 lawyer reasonably and continuously shall assess whether the party
14 the collaborative lawyer represents has a history of a coercive or
15 violent relationship with another party.
16 C. If a collaborative lawyer reasonably believes that the party
17 the lawyer represents or the prospective party who consults the
18 lawyer has a history of a coercive or violent relationship with
19 another party or prospective party, the lawyer may not begin or
20 continue a collaborative law process unless:
21 1. The party or the prospective party requests beginning or
22 continuing a process; and

1 2. The collaborative lawyer reasonably believes that the safety

11 SECTION 17. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3317 of Title 12, unless there
13 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 PRIVILEGE AGAINST DISCLOSURE FOR COLLABORATIVE LAW
15 COMMUNICATION; ADMISSIBILITY; DISCOVERY.
16 A. Subject to Sections 18 and 19 of this act, a collaborative
17 law communication is privileged under subsection B of this section,
18 is not subject to discovery, and is not admissible in evidence.
19 B. In a proceeding, the following privileges apply:
20 1. A party may refuse to disclose, and may prevent any other
21 person from disclosing, a collaborative law communication; and
22 2. A nonparty participant may refuse to disclose, and may
23 prevent any other person from disclosing, a collaborative law
24 communication of the nonparty participant.

(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 C. Evidence or information that is otherwise admissible or
2 subject to discovery does not become inadmissible or protected from
3 discovery solely because of its disclosure or use in a collaborative
4 law process.
5 SECTION 18. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3318 of Title 12, unless there
7 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 WAIVER AND PRECLUSION OF PRIVILEGE.
9 A. A privilege under Section 17 of this act may be waived in a
10 record or orally during a proceeding if it is expressly waived by
11 all parties and, in the case of the privilege of a nonparty
12 participant, it is also expressly waived by the nonparty
13 participant.
14 B. A person that makes a disclosure or representation about a
15 collaborative law communication which prejudices another person in a
16 proceeding may not assert a privilege under Section 17 of this act,
17 but this preclusion applies only to the extent necessary for the
18 person prejudiced to respond to the disclosure or representation.
19 SECTION 19. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3319 of Title 12, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 LIMITS OF PRIVILEGE.
23 A. There is no privilege under Section 17 of this act for a
24 collaborative law communication that is:
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2117 SFLR Page 18
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 1. Available to the public under the Oklahoma Open Records Act
2 or made during a session of a collaborative law process that is
3 open, or is required by law to be open, to the public;
4 2. A threat or statement of a plan to inflict bodily injury or
5 commit a crime of violence;
6 3. Intentionally used to plan a crime, commit or attempt to
7 commit a crime, or conceal an ongoing crime or ongoing criminal
8 activity; or
9 4. In an agreement resulting from the collaborative law
10 process, evidenced by a record signed by all parties to the
11 agreement.
12 B. The privileges under Section 17 of this act for a
13 collaborative law communication do not apply to the extent that a
14 communication is:
15 1. Sought or offered to prove or disprove a claim or complaint
16 of professional misconduct or malpractice arising from or related to
17 a collaborative law process; or
18 2. Sought or offered to prove or disprove abuse, neglect,
19 abandonment, or exploitation of a child or adult, unless the
20 Department of Human Services is a party to or otherwise participates
21 in the process.
22 C. There is no privilege under Section 17 of this act if a
23 tribunal finds, after a hearing in camera, that the party seeking
24 discovery or the proponent of the evidence has shown the evidence is
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2117 SFLR Page 19

1 not otherwise available, the need for the evidence substantially
2 outweighs the interest in protecting confidentiality, and the
3 collaborative law communication is sought or offered in:
4 1. A court proceeding involving a felony or misdemeanor; or
5 2. A proceeding seeking rescission or reformation of a contract
6 arising out of the collaborative law process or in which a defense
7 to avoid liability on the contract is asserted.
8 D. If a collaborative law communication is subject to an
9 exception under subsection B or C of this section, only the part of

16 F. The privileges under Section 17 of this act do not apply if

18 proceeding reflects agreement by the parties, that all or part of a
19 collaborative law process is not privileged. This subsection does
20 not apply to a collaborative law communication made by a person that
21 did not receive actual notice of the agreement before the
22 communication was made.

4 AUTHORITY OF TRIBUNAL IN CASE OF NONCOMPLIANCE.
5 A. If an agreement fails to meet the requirements of Section 4
6 of this act, or a lawyer fails to comply with Section 14 or 15 of
7 this act, a tribunal may nonetheless find that the parties intended
8 to enter into a collaborative law participation agreement if they:
9 1. Signed a record indicating an intention to enter into a
10 collaborative law participation agreement; and
11 2. Reasonably believed they were participating in a
12 collaborative law process.
13 B. If a tribunal makes the findings specified in subsection A
14 of this section, and the interests of justice require, the tribunal
15 may:
16 1. Enforce an agreement evidenced by a record resulting from
17 the process in which the parties participated;
18 2. Apply the disqualification provisions of Sections 5, 6, 9,
19 10, and 11 of this act; and
20 3. Apply a privilege under Section 17 of this act.

15 BEGINNING AND CONCLUDING COLLABORATIVE LAW PROCESS.
16 A. A collaborative law process begins when the parties sign a
17 collaborative law participation agreement.
18 B. A tribunal may not order a party to participate in a
19 collaborative law process over that party’s objection.
20 C. A collaborative law process is concluded by a:
21 1. Resolution of a collaborative matter as evidenced by a
22 signed record;
23
24

1 2. Resolution of a part of the collaborative matter, evidenced
2 by a signed record, in which the parties agree that the remaining
3 parts of the matter will not be resolved in the process; or
4 3. Termination of the process.
5 D. A collaborative law process terminates:
6 1. When a party gives notice to other parties in a record that
7 the process is ended;
8 2. When a party:
9 a. begins a proceeding related to a collaborative matter
10 without the agreement of all parties, or
11 b. in a pending proceeding related to the matter:
12 (1) initiates a pleading, motion, order to show
13 cause, or request for a conference with the
14 tribunal,
15 (2) requests that the proceeding be put on the
16 tribunal’s active calendar, or
17 (3) takes similar action requiring notice to be sent
18 to the parties; or
19 3. Except as otherwise provided by subsection G of this
20 section, when a party discharges a collaborative lawyer or a
21 collaborative lawyer withdraws from further representation of a
22 party.
23 E. A party’s collaborative lawyer shall give prompt notice to
24 all other parties in a record of a discharge or withdrawal.

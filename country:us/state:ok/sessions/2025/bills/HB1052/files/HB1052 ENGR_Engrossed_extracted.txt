6 An Act relating to fire departments; amending 11 O.S.
2021, Section 29-102, which relates to activity
7 reports from city fire departments; directing that
reports utilize the National Emergency Response
8 Information System; directing reports to be completed
within forty-eight hours of incident; directing
9 certain charitable corporations to submit reports
utilizing the National Emergency Response Information
10 System; providing report contents; requiring reports
to be submitted within forty-eight hours of incident;
11 amending 19 O.S. 2021, Section 901.7a, which relates
to activity reports from fire protection districts;
12 directing fire chief to utilize the National
Emergency Response Information System; requiring
13 reports be completed within forty-eight hours of
incident; providing for codification; and providing
14 an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 29-102, is
19 amended to read as follows:
20 Section 29-102. All cities having a paid fire department shall
21 have one full-time fire chief who shall supervise and administer the
22 fire department in accordance with the policies and procedures
23 prescribed by the governing body or by the city manager. The fire
24 department shall be under the direction and control of the fire
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1052 Page 1
1 chie f who shall not serve as fire chief and also as police chief,
2 city manager, mayor or any other position that impairs the ability
3 to perform the duties of a fire chief. The fire chief, whether
4 permanent or interim, of any paid municipal fire department shall
5 have had at least three (3) years' actual experience as a paid fire
6 fighter before assuming the position of fire chief. It shall be the
7 duty of the fire chief to file the appropriate activity report forms
8 reports utilizing the National Emergency Response Information System
9 (NERIS) with the Office of the State Fire Marshal in Oklahoma City
10 on an annual basis. The activity report forms shall be designed by
11 the State Fire Marshal and shall include, but not be limited to, the
12 amount of property and vehicle fire loss, types of fires,
13 inspections and investigations that occurred. The report shall
14 include notification of all fire-related civilian deaths and
15 injuries in the respective jurisdiction and of fire fighter deaths
16 in the line of duty and of fire fighter injuries in the line of duty
17 requiring the services of a hospital or physician or both, and these
18 fire-related burn injuries and death reports shall be completed
19 within forty-eight (48) hours of the incident.
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 595 of Title 18, unless there is
22 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 Any charitable corporation formed in an unincorporated area for
24 the purpose of providing either a volunteer or a full-time fire
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1052 Page 2
1 depa rtment, such as mentioned in Section 592 of Title 18 of the
2 Oklahoma Statutes, shall be required to file the appropriate
3 activity reports utilizing the National Emergency Response
4 Information System (NERIS) with the Office of the State Fire Marshal
5 in Oklahoma City. The activity report forms shall include, but not
6 be limited to, the amount of property and vehicle fire loss, types
7 of fires, inspections, and investigations. The report shall include
8 notification of all fire-related civilian deaths and injuries in the
9 respective jurisdiction and of firefighter deaths in the line of
10 duty and of firefighter injuries in the line of duty requiring the
11 services of a hospital or physician or both, and these fire-related
12 burn injuries and death reports shall be completed within forty-
13 eight (48) hours of the incident.
14 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 19 O.S. 2021, Section 901.7a, is
15 amended to read as follows:
16 Section 901.7a. All Fire Protection Districts having more than
17 two full-time paid firefighters shall have one full-time fire chief
18 who shall supervise and administer the fire protection district in
19 accordance with the policies and procedures prescribed by the board
20 of directors. The fire protection district shall be under the
21 direction and control of the fire chief. The fire chief, whether
22 permanent or interim, of any paid or combination fire protection
23 district shall have had at least three (3) years actual experience
24 as a paid firefighter before assuming the position of fire chief.
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1052 Page 3
1 Duri ng the initial transition from a volunteer fire protection
2 district to one having more than two full-time firefighters, the
3 paid fire chief shall have had at least three (3) years of
4 experience as a paid firefighter or at least five (5) years of
5 experience as a volunteer fire chief officer with that department
6 before assuming the position of paid fire chief. The board may add
7 additional requirements to the position as necessary.
8 1. The fire chief:
9 a. shall be at the head of the department, subject to the
10 laws of the State of Oklahoma, rules of the board of
11 directors and the rules and regulations herein
12 adopted,
13 b. shall be held responsible for the general condition
14 and efficient operation of the department, the
15 training of members and the performance of all other
16 duties imposed upon him or her by law or the board of
17 directors,
18 c. may inspect or cause to be inspected by members of the
19 department, the fire hydrants, cisterns and other
20 sources of water supply at least twice a year,
21 d. shall maintain a library or file of publications on
22 fire prevention and fire protection and shall make use
23 of the library or file to the best advantage of all
24 members,
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1052 Page 4
1 e. shall make every effort to attend all fires and direct
2 the officers and members of the fire department in the
3 performance of their duties,
4 f. shall see that the citizens are kept informed on fire
5 hazards within the boundaries of the department and on
6 the activities of the department,
7 g. shall see that each fire is carefully investigated to
8 determine its cause, and in the case of suspicion of
9 incendiarism shall notify proper authorities. The
10 fire chief shall secure and preserve all possible
11 evidence for future use in the case of a suspicious
12 incendiarism, and
13 h. shall file the appropriate activity report forms
14 reports utilizing the National Emergency Response
15 Information System (NERIS) with the Office of the
16 State Fire Marshal in Oklahoma City on an annual
17 basis. The activity report forms shall be designed by
18 the State Fire Marshal and shall include, but not be
19 limited to, the amount of property and vehicle fire
20 loss, types of fires, inspections and investigations.
21 The report shall include notification of all fire-
22 related civilian deaths and injuries in the respective
23 jurisdiction and of firefighter deaths in the line of
24 duty and of firefighter injuries in the line of duty
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1052 Page 5
1 requiring the services of a hospital or physician or
2 both, and these fire-related burn injuries and death
3 reports shall be completed within forty-eight (48)
4 hours of the incident.
5 2. Assistant fire chief. In the absence of the fire chief, the
6 assistant fire chief on duty shall command the department and be
7 held responsible therefore in all respects with the full powers and
8 responsibilities of the fire chief.
9 3. Company officers. The company officers shall be selected
10 upon their ability to meet the following requirements:
11 a. knowledge of firefighting,
12 b. leadership ability, and
13 c. knowledge of firefighting equipment.
14 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
15 Passed the House of Representatives the 10th day of March, 2025.

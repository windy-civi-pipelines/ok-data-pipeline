6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to public grounds; amending 25 O.S.
7 2021, Section 2101, which relates to display of
8 replicas of historical documents on public grounds;
8 authorizing state to display certain items on public
9 grounds; requiring display of certain monument;
9 requiring certain determinations by specified
10 officials; authorizing consultation with third
10 parties; specifying requirements for certain funding;
11 requiring approval for certain relocation; creating
11 felony offense; updating statutory language; and
12 declaring an emergency.
12
13
13
14
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 25 O.S. 2021, Section 2101, is
16
17 amended to read as follows:
17
18 Section 2101. A. Every The state and any county, municipality,
18
19 city, town, school or any other political subdivision is authorized
19
20 to display, in its public buildings and on its grounds, replicas of
20
21 historical documents including, but not limited to, the Ten
21
22 Commandments, Magna Carta, Mayflower Compact, Declaration of
22
23 Independence, United States Constitution, Bill of Rights, Oklahoma
23
24 Constitution and other historically significant documents in the
24
Req. No. 464 Page 1
1 form of statues, monuments, memorials, tablets or any other display
1
2 that respects the dignity and solemnity of such documents. Such
2
3 documents shall be displayed in a manner consistent with the context
3
4 of other documents contained in such display.
4
5 B. A monument of the Ten Commandments shall be prominently
5
6 displayed and protected on the inside and outside of the Oklahoma
6
7 State Capitol as a symbol of its historic significance for Oklahoman
7
8 and American history. The Governor, the President Pro Tempore of
8
9 the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall
9
10 meet within one (1) year after the effective date of this act to
10
11 determine the design, placement, security, construction, and funding
11
12 of the monuments. Such officials may consult with third-party
12
13 groups to assist in making such determinations and with the
13
14 implementation of such determinations. Only donated funds may be
14
15 used for the design, placement, security, and construction of the
15
16 monuments. Monuments shall not be relocated once they are in place
16
17 without approval by three-fourths (3/4) of both houses of the
17
18 Legislature and approval of the Governor. Any person who knowingly
18
19 causes damage or destruction to the monuments shall be, upon
19
20 conviction, guilty of a felony.
20
21 C. In the event that the legality or constitutionality of any
21
22 such display is challenged in a court of law, the Oklahoma Attorney
22
23 General is authorized to prepare and present a legal defense of the
23
24 display.
24

1 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
1
2 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
2
3 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
3
4 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

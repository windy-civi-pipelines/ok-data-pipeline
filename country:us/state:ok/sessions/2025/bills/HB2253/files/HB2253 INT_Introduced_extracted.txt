Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to teacher certification; requiring
the State Department of Education to code initial
8 teacher certification; creating the Data Governance
Council; prescribing duties of Council; providing for
9 membership of Council; establishing deadlines for
initial appointment and meeting of Council;
10 prescribing frequency of meetings; setting meeting
quorum requirement; providing staffing and support
11 for Council; prohibiting compensation for members of
Council; subjecting Council to the Oklahoma Open
12 Meeting Act and Oklahoma Open Records Act; providing
exemption from dual office-holding prohibitions;
13 providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.
14
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-188.1 of Title 70, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 The State Department of Education shall develop a system to code
22 initial teacher certification in order to report data on the
23 pathways for initial teacher certification, including emergency
24 certification and full-time adjunct certification.
Req. No. 11714 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-188.2 of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created the Data Governance Council.
5 B. The Council shall:
6 1. Monitor and evaluate the impact of each teacher
7 certification pathway, including emergency certification and full-
8 time adjunct certification, as reported by the State Department of
9 Education pursuant to Section 1 of this act, on education outcomes
10 in the state;
11 2. Identify essential data elements indicative of teacher
12 workforce quality and ensure these elements are appropriately
13 defined and standardized for acceptable uses across certification
14 pathways; and
15 3. Establish best practices in data management and storage
16 processes to ensure security, privacy, and appropriate integration.
17 C. The Council shall be comprised of thirteen (13) members as
18 follows:
19 1. The Chancellor of the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher
20 Education, or designee;
21 2. The State Superintendent of Public Instruction, or designee;
22 3. The Executive Director of the Office of Educational Quality
23 and Accountability, or designee;
24
Req. No. 11714 Page 2
1 4. One member who is an employee of the State Department of
2 Education, designated by the State Superintendent of Public
3 Instruction;
4 5. One member who is part of an organization or association
5 representing school district boards of education in the state,
6 appointed by the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives;
7 6. One member who is part of an organization or association
8 representing school administrators in the state, appointed by the
9 President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate;
10 7. One member who is an elementary or secondary public school
11 teacher in the state, appointed by the Governor;
12 8. One member who is a principal from a public prekindergarten
13 through twelfth-grade school, appointed by the Speaker of the
14 Oklahoma House of Representatives; and
15 9. Five members representing deans, directors, or coordinators
16 from accredited, university-based educator preparation programs in
17 the state, appointed as follows:
18 a. two members appointed by the Speaker of the Oklahoma
19 House of Representatives,
20 b. two members appointed by the President Pro Tempore of
21 the Oklahoma State Senate, and
22 c. one member appointed by the Governor.
23 D. Appointments to the Council shall be made by no later than
24 December 1, 2025. The first meeting of the Council shall be called
Req. No. 11714 Page 3
1 by t he State Superintendent of Public Instruction no later than
2 February 1, 2026. At the first meeting, the members of the Council
3 shall elect a chair and vice-chair from among the members. Meetings
4 of the Council shall be held at least quarterly at the call of the
5 chair. Members shall serve at the pleasure of their appointing
6 authorities. A majority of the members of the Council shall
7 constitute a quorum to transact business, but no vacancy shall
8 impair the right of the remaining members to exercise all of the
9 powers of the Council. A vacancy on the Council shall be filled by
10 the original appointing authority. The State Department of
11 Education, Office of Educational Quality and Accountability, and the
12 Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education shall provide staff,
13 support, and information as requested by the Council.
14 E. Members of the Council shall receive no compensation for
15 serving on the Council.
16 F. The Council shall be subject to the Oklahoma Open Meeting
17 Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act.
18 G. Members who serve on the Council shall be exempt from the
19 dual-office-holding prohibitions of Section 6 of Title 51 of the
20 Oklahoma Statutes.
21 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

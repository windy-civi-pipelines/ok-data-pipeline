12 An Act relating to schools; amending 70 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 8-103.1 and Section 1, Chapter 368, O.S.L.
13 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 8-114), which
relate to school transfers; removing reference to
14 home of record for military dependent transfers;
removing reference to requirement that parents
15 provide certain evidence of active duty status for
certain transfer approvals; prohibiting schools from
16 precluding certain transfer students from enrollment
prior to establishing residency; mandating that
17 intra-district transfer students stay in the
transferred district continuously; clarifying and
18 limiting exceptions for continual intra-district
transfers; providing capacity exception for certain
19 military children's intra-district transfers;
prescribing number of certain post-capacity
20 transfers; defining terms; providing an effective
date; and declaring an emergency.

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 8-103.1, is
2 amended to read as follows:
3 Section 8-103.1. A. A local school district board of education
4 which receives a request for a transfer for a student who does not
5 reside in the school district may refuse the transfer in accordance
6 with the provisions of the open transfer policy adopted by the local
7 school district board of education and subject to the provisions of
8 subsections B and C of this section. Each local board of education
9 shall adopt an open transfer policy for the school district which
10 specifies its criteria and standards for approval of transfers of
11 students who do not reside in the district. The policy shall
12 include, but shall not be limited to, provisions relating to the
13 availability of programs, staff, or space as criteria for approval
14 or denial of transfers. A school district may include in the policy
15 as the basis for denial of a transfer, the reasons outlined in
16 Section 24-101.3 of this title.
17 In considering requests for students to transfer into a school
18 district, the board of education shall consider the requests on a
19 first-come, first-serve basis. A school district shall not accept
20 or deny a transfer based on ethnicity, national origin, gender,
21 income level, disabling condition, proficiency in the English
22 language, measure of achievement, aptitude, or athletic ability.

1 Notwithstanding the provisions of the Education Open Transfer
2 Act, transfers of children with disabilities shall be granted as
3 authorized in Section 13-103 of this title.
4 B. A local school district board of education shall adopt a
5 policy for the school district regarding the transfer of students
6 who are the dependent children of a member of the active uniformed
7 military services of the United States on full-time active duty
8 status and for whom Oklahoma is the home of record and students who
9 are the dependent children of a member of the military reserve on
10 active duty orders and for whom Oklahoma is the home of record. The
11 policy shall provide for the approval of the transfer if:
12 1. At least one parent of the student has a Department of
13 Defense-issued identification card; and
14 2. At least one parent can provide evidence that he or she will
15 be on active duty status or active duty orders, meaning the parent
16 will be temporarily transferred in compliance with official orders
17 to another location in support of combat, contingency operation or a
18 natural disaster requiring the use of orders for more than thirty
19 (30) consecutive days; and
20 3. The student will be residing with a relative of the student
21 who lives in the receiving school district or who will be living in
22 the receiving school district within six (6) months of the filing of
23 the application for transfer.

20 orders; and

22 yard, center, homeport facility for any ship or other installation
23 under the jurisdiction of the Department of Defense or the United
24 States Coast Guard.

1 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 368, O.S.L.
2 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 8-114), is amended to read as
3 follows:
4 Section 8-114. A. Except as provided in subsection B of this
5 section, beginning July 1, 2024, the transfer of a student from one
6 school site to another school site within the school district where
7 the student resides shall be approved at any time in the year,
8 unless the grade level of the receiving school site has reached
9 capacity. If the capacity of a grade level is insufficient to
10 enroll all eligible students, the school district shall select
11 intra-district transfer students based on the preferences outlined
12 in paragraph 1 of subsection B of this section and then in the order
13 in which the intra-district transfer applications were received.
14 The school district board of education shall determine the capacity
15 of a school site based on its policy adopted pursuant to subsection
16 B of this section. A student may be granted a one-year intra-
17 district transfer and may shall automatically continue to attend the
18 school site where the student transferred each school year with the
19 approval of the school district, unless the school district denies
20 the continued intra-district transfer for the reasons outlined in
21 paragraphs 2 and 3 of subsection B of this section. At the end of
22 each school year, a school district may only deny continued intra-
23 district transfer of the student for the reasons outlined in
24 paragraphs 2 and 3 of subsection B of this section.

11 preference to the transfer of children of school district employees

16 district transfer if the school district policy gives preference to
17 such transfers and the student does not meet a basis for denial as
18 outlined in paragraphs 2 and 3 of subsection B of this section.
19 4. Any child in the custody of the Department of Human Services
20 and living in foster care who resides in the home of another student
21 who transfers intra-district may attend the school site to which the
22 student transferred.

3 E. 1. Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, school
4 districts shall allow students who are the dependent children of a
5 member of the active uniformed military services of the United
6 States on full-time active duty status, and students who are the

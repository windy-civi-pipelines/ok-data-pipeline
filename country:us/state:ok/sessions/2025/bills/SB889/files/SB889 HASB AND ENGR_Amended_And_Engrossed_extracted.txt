5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

9 2. Ensure the list required under paragraph 1 of this
10 subsection is available at all times to the public, including by
11 posting the list electronically in the manner provided by this
12 section.

14 maintained by a hospital under subsection A of this section shall
15 reflect the standard charges applicable to that location of the
16 hospital, regardless of whether the hospital operates in more than
17 one location or operates under the same license as another hospital.
18 C. The list required under subsection A of this section shall
19 include the following items, as applicable:
20 1. A description of each hospital item or service provided by
21 the hospital;
22 2. The following charges for each individual hospital item or
23 service when provided in either an inpatient setting or an
24 outpatient department setting, as applicable:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 889 Page 6
1 a. the gross charge,
2 b. the de-identified minimum negotiated charge,
3 c. the de-identified maximum negotiated charge,
4 d. the discounted cash price, and
5 e. the payor-specific negotiated charge, listed by the
6 name of the third-party payor and plan associated with
7 the charge and displayed in a manner that clearly
8 associates the charge with each third-party payor and
9 plan; and
10 3. Any code used by the hospital for purposes of accounting or
11 billing for the hospital item or service, including the Current
12 Procedural Terminology (CPT) code, the Healthcare Common Procedure
13 Coding System (HCPCS) code, the Diagnosis Related Group (DRG) code,
14 the National Drug Code (NDC), or other common identifier.
15 D. The information contained in the list required under
16 subsection A of this section shall be published in a single digital
17 file that is in a machine-readable format.
18 E. The list required under subsection A of this section shall
19 be displayed in a prominent location on the hospital’s publicly
20 accessible Internet website. If the hospital operates multiple
21 locations and maintains a single Internet website, the list required
22 under subsection A of this section shall be posted for each location
23 the hospital operates in a manner that clearly associates the list
24 with the applicable location of the hospital.

8 2. Be digitally searchable; and

22 compliance with the requirements of this act using any of the

6 with this act.

8 compliance with a provision of this act, the Department may take any
9 of the following actions:
10 1. Provide a written notice to the hospital that clearly

12 this act;
13 2. Request a corrective action plan from the hospital if the
14 hospital has materially violated a provision of this act, as
15 determined under Section 6 of this act; and
16 3. Impose an administrative penalty on the hospital and

18 hospital fails to:
19 a. respond to the Department’s request to submit a

22 plan submitted to the Department.

1 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-725.16 of Title 63, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. A hospital materially violates this act if the hospital
5 fails to publicize:
6 1. Pricing information as required by Section 2 of this act; or
7 2. The hospital’s standard charges in the form and manner
8 required by Sections 3 and 4 of this act.
9 B. If the State Department of Health determines that a hospital
10 has materially violated this act, the Department may issue a notice
11 of material violation to the hospital and request that the hospital
12 submit a corrective action plan. The notice shall indicate the form
13 and manner in which the corrective action plan shall be submitted to
14 the Department, and clearly state the date by which the hospital
15 shall submit the plan.
16 C. A hospital that receives a notice under subsection B of this
17 section shall:
18 1. Submit a corrective action plan in the form and manner, and
19 by the specified date, prescribed by the notice of violation; and
20 2. As soon as practicable after submission of a corrective
21 action plan to the Department, act to comply with the plan.
22 D. A corrective action plan submitted to the Department shall:
23
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 889 Page 14
1 1. Describe in detail the corrective action the hospital will
2 take to address any violation identified by the Department in the
3 notice provided under subsection B of this section; and
4 2. Provide a date by which the hospital will complete the
5 corrective action described by paragraph 1 of this subsection.
6 E. A corrective action plan is subject to review and approval
7 by the Department. After the Department reviews and approves a
8 hospital’s corrective action plan, the Department shall monitor and
9 evaluate the hospital’s compliance with the plan.
10 F. A hospital is considered to have failed to respond to the
11 Department’s request to submit a corrective action plan if the
12 hospital fails to submit a corrective action plan:
13 1. In the form and manner specified in the notice provided
14 under subsection B of this section; or
15 2. By the date specified in the notice provided under
16 subsection B of this section.
17 G. A hospital is considered to have failed to comply with a
18 corrective action plan if the hospital fails to address a violation
19 within the specified period of time contained in the plan.

3 the items or services.
4 B. If a patient believes that a hospital was not in material
5 compliance with this act on a date on or after the effective date of
6 this act that items or services were purchased by or provided to the
7 patient, and the hospital takes a collection action against the

10 compliance with this act on the date of service and if the
11 noncompliance is related to the items or services. The hospital

13 guarantor while the lawsuit is pending.
14 C. A hospital that has been found by a judge or jury to be
15 materially out of compliance with this act:
16 1. Shall refund the payor any amount of the debt the payor has
17 paid and shall pay a penalty to the patient or patient guarantor in
18 an amount equal to the total amount of the debt;
19 2. Shall dismiss or cause to be dismissed any court action with
20 prejudice and pay any reasonable attorney fees and costs incurred by
21 the patient or patient guarantor relating to the action; and
22 3. Shall remove or cause to be removed from the patient’s or
23 patient guarantor’s credit report any report made to a consumer
24 reporting agency relating to the debt.

6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to medical records; amending 76 O.S.
7 2021, Section 19, which relates to access to medical
8 records; modifying applicability of certain
8 provisions; eliminating certain duties of health care
9 providers; authorizing imposition of certain fee;
9 making certain acts unlawful; creating felony
10 offense; providing administrative and criminal
10 penalties; providing for civil relief; and providing
11 an effective date.
11
12
12
13
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 76 O.S. 2021, Section 19, is
15
16 amended to read as follows:
16
17 Section 19. A. 1. Any person who is or has been a patient of
17
18 a doctor, hospital or other medical institution any health care
18
19 provider or health care facility, or such person’s personal
19
20 representative, shall be entitled, upon request, to obtain access to
20
21 the information contained in the patient’s medical records including
21
22 any x-ray or other photograph or image, pathology slide or the
22
23 patient’s medical bills.
23
24
24
Req. No. 603 Page 1
1 2. Disclosure of the information described in paragraph 1 of
1
2 this subsection regarding a deceased patient shall require either a
2
3 court order or a written release of an executor, administrator or
3
4 personal representative appointed by the court, or if there is no
4
5 such appointment, by the spouse of the patient or, if none, by any
5
6 responsible member of the family of the patient. As used in this
6
7 paragraph, “responsible family member” shall mean the parent, adult
7
8 child, adult sibling or other adult relative who was actively
8
9 involved in providing care to or monitoring the care of the patient
9
10 as verified by the doctor, hospital or other medical institution
10
11 health care provider or health care facility responsible for the
11
12 care and treatment of such person.
12
13 2. Any person who is or has been a patient of a doctor,
13
14 hospital or other medical institution shall be furnished copies of
14
15 all medical records including any x-ray, other photograph or image,
15
16 pathology slide or all medical bills pertaining to that person’s
16
17 case upon request and upon the tender of the expenses enumerated in
17
18 this paragraph. The cost of each copy to such person or to the
18
19 personal representative, spouse or responsible family member of such
19
20 person, not including any x-ray or other photograph or image or
20
21 pathology slide, shall be fifty cents ($0.50) for each page.
21
22 3. Health care providers and health care facilities may impose
22
23 a reasonable, cost-based fee when patients or their personal
23
24 representatives request copies of medical records; provided, that
24
Req. No. 603 Page 2
1 the fee includes only the cost of applicable expenses listed in 42
1
2 C.F.R., Section 164.524(c)(4).
2
3 4. Requests for medical records and medical bills from
3
4 attorneys, insurance companies and by way of subpoena shall be
4
5 charged a base fee of Twenty Dollars ($20.00) in addition to the per
5
6 page charges required pursuant to this section, plus postage or
6
7 delivery fee. The base fee shall be charged regardless of whether
7
8 any records related to the request are located. A fee of Fifteen
8
9 Dollars ($15.00) shall be charged if a certification or an affidavit
9
10 by the provider regarding the authenticity of the medical records or
10
11 bills is requested. The physician, hospital or other medical
11
12 professionals and institutions, or their business associates as the
12
13 term is defined in Section 160.103 of Title 45 of the United States
13
14 Code of Federal Regulations shall produce the records in digital
14
15 form at the rate of thirty cents ($0.30) per page if:
15
16 a. the entire request can be reproduced from an
16
17 electronic health record system,
17
18 b. the records are specifically requested to be delivered
18
19 in electronic format, and
19
20 c. the records can be delivered electronically.
20
21 If a provider or business associate transmits the records
21
22 electronically, no postage shall be charged but a delivery charge
22
23 shall apply. In no event shall a charge for the reproduction of
23
24 electronically stored and delivered medical records pursuant to this
24
Req. No. 603 Page 3
1 para graph exceed Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00) plus postage or
1
2 delivery fee. The cost of each printed x-ray, other photograph or
2
3 image to such person or to the legal representative of such person
3
4 shall be Fifteen Dollars ($15.00). If the x-ray, other photograph
4
5 or image is provided on a CD/DVD or other electronic media, the fee
5
6 shall be Twenty Dollars ($20.00) per CD/DVD or other electronic
6
7 media. The physician, hospital, or other medical professionals and
7
8 institutions, or their business associates as the term is defined in
8
9 Section 160.103 of Title 45 of the United States Code of Federal
9
10 Regulations, shall not charge a person who requests their own record
10
11 a fee for searching, retrieving, reviewing, and preparing medical
11
12 records of the person. No mailing fee shall be charged for copies
12
13 provided by facsimile. All requests for medical records made
13
14 pursuant to this subsection shall be subject to the fees described
14
15 in this section regardless of where the copies or electronic
15
16 versions of such records are actually produced.
16
17 3. 5. The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of this subsection
17
18 shall not apply to:
18
19 a. psychological, psychiatric, mental health or substance
19
20 abuse treatment records. In the case of
20
21 psychological, psychiatric, mental health or substance
21
22 abuse treatment records, access to information
22
23 contained in the records shall be obtained pursuant to
23
24 Section 1-109 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma Statutes.
24
Req. No. 603 Page 4
1 4. The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of this subsection
1
2 shall not apply to, and
2
3 b. requests for medical records made by the Disability
3
4 Determination Division of the State Department of
4
5 Rehabilitation Services. The fee for such requests
5
6 shall be at a rate allowed by the Social Security
6
7 Administration.
7
8 B. 1. In cases involving a claim for personal injury or death
8
9 against any practitioner of the healing arts or a licensed hospital,
9
10 or a nursing facility or nursing home licensed pursuant to Section
10
11 1-1903 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes arising out of patient
11
12 care, where any person has placed the physical or mental condition
12
13 of that person in issue by the commencement of any action,
13
14 proceeding, or suit for damages, or where any person has placed in
14
15 issue the physical or mental condition of any other person or
15
16 deceased person by or through whom the person rightfully claims,
16
17 that person shall be deemed to waive any privilege granted by law
17
18 concerning any communication made to a physician or health care
18
19 provider with reference to any physical or mental condition or any
19
20 knowledge obtained by the physician or health care provider by
20
21 personal examination of the patient; provided that, before any
21
22 communication, medical or hospital record, or testimony is admitted
22
23 in evidence in any proceeding, it must be material and relevant to
23
24 an issue therein, according to existing rules of evidence.
24
Req. No. 603 Page 5
1 Psyc hological, psychiatric, mental health and substance abuse
1
2 treatment records and information from psychological, psychiatric,
2
3 mental health and substance abuse treatment practitioners may only
3
4 be obtained provided the requirements of Section 1-109 of Title 43A
4
5 of the Oklahoma Statutes are met.
5
6 2. Any person who obtains any document pursuant to the
6
7 provisions of this section shall provide copies of the document to
7
8 any opposing party in the proceeding upon payment of the expense of
8
9 copying the document pursuant to the provisions of this section.
9
10 C. This section shall not apply to the records of an inmate in
10
11 a correctional institution when the correctional institution
11
12 believes the release of such information to be a threat to the
12
13 safety or security of the inmate or the institution.
13
14 D. 1. It shall be unlawful for any person to transmit,
14
15 disclose, seek, use, or obtain any person’s health information or
15
16 records related to any medical service, procedure, or treatment that
16
17 is deemed medically necessary by a physician, nurse, or other health
17
18 care provider licensed in this state for any purpose other than
18
19 treatment, payment, or health care operations as those terms are
19
20 defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
20
21 of 1996 or as authorized by the person whose information is being
21
22 sought, used, or disclosed.
22
23
23
24
24
Req. No. 603 Page 6
1 2. Violation of paragraph 1 of this subsection shall be deemed
1
2 unprofessional conduct by the person’s licensure board if applicable
2
3 and subject to mandatory license revocation.
3
4 3. A person found in violation of paragraph 1 of this
4
5 subsection shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a felony punishable
5
6 by a fine not to exceed Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00), or by
6
7 imprisonment in the custody of the Department of Corrections for a
7
8 term not to exceed one (1) year, or by both such fine and
8
9 imprisonment.
9
10 4. If a health care provider believes a violation of paragraph
10
11 1 of this subsection has occurred or will occur in relation to a
11
12 request for medical records, the health care provider may apply to a
12
13 court of competent jurisdiction for relief and the court shall award
13
14 appropriate relief including, but not limited to, temporary,
14
15 preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief.
15
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
16

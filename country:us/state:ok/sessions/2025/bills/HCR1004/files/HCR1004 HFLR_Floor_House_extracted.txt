16 Abuse Services is obligated to provide competency evaluations and
17 restoration treatment for persons found incompetent to stand trial
18 in Oklahoma state court criminal proceedings; and
19 WHEREAS, when an Oklahoma state court determines that a person
20 is incompetent to stand trial because he or she is a "person
21 requiring treatment", but capable of achieving competency with
22 treatment within a reasonable period of time, the state court must
23 suspend the criminal proceedings and order the Department, or its

2 allow the person to achieve competency; and
3 WHEREAS, the Oklahoma Forensic Center is currently the only
4 Department-operated hospital that provides secure, in-patient
5 competency restoration treatment in Oklahoma; and
6 WHEREAS, on March 1, 2023, the putative class filed a class
7 action lawsuit pursuant to 42 U.S.C., Section 1983 against the
8 Commissioner of the Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
9 Service and the Executive Director of the Oklahoma Forensic Center;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, the parties have entered into a consent decree pursuant
12 to case number 23-cv-81-GKF-JFJ filed in the United States District
13 Court for the Northern District of Oklahoma; and
14 WHEREAS, the parties acknowledge that plaintiffs' claims and
15 allegations are serious and credible and the consent decree resolves
16 plaintiffs' claims while avoiding the costs, uncertainties, and
17 risks of protracted litigation, likely saving the Department
18 millions of dollars in legal fees and expenses if the case were
19 litigated to a conclusion.

Section 6-101.23, which relates to the Teachers Due
10 Process Act of 1990; authorizing certain temporary
contracts for teachers with emergency or provisional
11 certificates; updating citations; providing an
effective date; and declaring an emergency.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 6-101.23, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 6-101.23. A. The dismissal, suspension and
18 nonreemployment provisions of the Teacher Due Process Act of 1990
19 shall not apply to:
20 1. Substitute teachers;
21 2. Adult education teachers; and
22 3. Teachers who are employed on temporary contracts.
23 B. The dismissal and suspension provisions of the Teacher Due
24 Process Act of 1990 shall apply to teachers who are employed on

1 temp orary contracts for a complete school year and to teachers who
2 are employed in positions fully funded by federal or private
3 categorical grants, except that such teachers shall be employed only
4 for the duration of the temporary contract or the grant.
5 C. The evaluation provisions in Sections 6-101.10 and 6-101.11
6 of this title and in the Teacher Due Process Act of 1990 shall apply
7 to teachers who are employed on temporary contracts for a complete
8 school year and to teachers who are employed in positions fully
9 funded by federal or private categorical grants, except that such
10 teachers shall be employed only for the duration of the temporary
11 contract or the grant.
12 D. Teachers other than those specifically excepted in
13 subsection A of this section who are employed on contracts shall be
14 afforded all substantive and procedural rights set forth in the
15 Teacher Due Process Act of 1990 including the dismissal, suspension,
16 and nonreemployment provisions applicable to probationary or career
17 teachers as defined in Section 6-101.3 of this title.
18 E. On and after the effective date of this act July 1, 1999,
19 any teacher who has worked a complete school year under a temporary
20 contract in a school district shall be granted a year of service
21 credit toward career status in that district.
22 F. No teacher shall be hired on a temporary contract by a
23 school district for more than four semesters or on multiple

6 2. Teacher who is a retired member of the Teachers' Retirement

8 3. Teacher who is employed by a school district on an emergency

10 G. No teacher shall be offered a temporary contract with a

15 disclosure, the teacher shall be considered as employed on a
16 continuing contract basis.
17 H. On and after the effective date of this act July 1, 1999, no
18 teacher who is employed on a continuing contract basis by a school
19 district shall be reemployed on a temporary contract in that school
20 district.

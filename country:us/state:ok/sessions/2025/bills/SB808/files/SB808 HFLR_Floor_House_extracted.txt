9 An Act relating to the practice of physical therapy;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 887.17, which relates
10 to referrals; clarifying certain exemption from
referral; updating statutory language; and providing
11 an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 887.17, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 887.17. A. 1. Except for workers’ compensation
18 claims, any person licensed under the Physical Therapy Practice Act
19 as a physical therapist shall be able to including, but not limited
20 to, a licensed physical therapist operating under a direct-to-
21 employer contract, may evaluate and treat human ailments by physical
22 therapy on a patient without a referral from a licensed health care
23 practitioner for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days. Treatment
24 may be provided by a physical therapist assistant under the
SB808 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 supe rvision of a physical therapist. Any treatment provided beyond
2 the thirty-day period shall be only under the referral of a person
3 licensed as a physician or surgeon with unlimited license, or the
4 physician assistant of the person so licensed, and Doctors of
5 Dentistry, Chiropractic and Podiatry doctors of dentistry,
6 chiropractic, and podiatry and an Advanced Practice Registered
7 Nurse, with those referrals being limited to their respective areas
8 of training and practice.
9 2. A physical therapist may provide services within the scope
10 of physical therapy practice without a physician referral to
11 children who receive physical therapy services pursuant to the
12 Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, as
13 may be amended, and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973,
14 as may be amended. Provided further, a plan of care developed by a
15 person authorized to provide services within the scope of the
16 Physical Therapy Practice Act shall be deemed to be a prescription
17 for purposes of providing services pursuant to the provisions of the
18 Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, as
19 may be amended, and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973,
20 as may be amended.
21 3. Nothing in the Physical Therapy Practice Act shall prevent a
22 physical therapist from performing screening and educational
23 procedures within the scope of physical therapy practice without a
24 physician referral.
SB808 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 4. Nothing in the Physical Therapy Practice Act shall prevent a
2 physical therapist from performing services that are provided for
3 the purpose of fitness, wellness, or prevention that is not related
4 to the treatment of an injury or ailment.
5 5. Nothing in the Physical Therapy Practice Act shall be
6 construed as authorization for a physical therapist or physical
7 therapist assistant to practice any branch of the healing art.
8 6. Any person violating the provisions of the Physical Therapy
9 Practice Act shall be guilty of a misdemeanor as per Section 887.16
10 of this title.
11 B. 1. The provisions of the Physical Therapy Practice Act are
12 not intended to limit the activities of persons legitimately engaged
13 in the nontherapeutic administration of baths, massage, and normal
14 exercise.
15 2. The Physical Therapy Practice Act shall not prohibit
16 students who are enrolled in schools of physical therapy approved by
17 the State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision from performing
18 such work as is incidental to their course of study; nor shall it
19 prevent any student in any recognized school of the healing art in
20 carrying out prescribed courses of study;, provided such school is a
21 recognized institution by the statutes of Oklahoma Statutes, and its
22 practitioners are duly licensed as prescribed by law.
23 3. Nothing in the Physical Therapy Practice Act shall apply to
24 any person employed by an agency, bureau, or division of the federal

1 gove rnment while in the discharge of official duties; however, if
2 such individual engages in the practice of physical therapy outside
3 the line of official duty, the individual must be licensed as herein
4 provided.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

22 Section 121. A. In all cases where publication of legal

1 1. For all matters other than tabular matter, fourteen cents
2 ($0.14) per word for first insertion, and thirteen cents ($0.13) per
3 word for each subsequent insertion, with each separate group of
4 numerals included in the matter to be counted as one word,
5 regardless of the number of digits involved;
6 2. Effective November 1, 2005, for all matters other than
7 tabular matter, fifteen cents ($0.15) twenty-two cents ($0.22) per
8 word for first insertion, and fourteen cents ($0.14) twenty cents
9 ($0.20) per word for each subsequent insertion, with each separate
10 group of letters or numerals included in the matter to be counted as
11 one word, regardless of the number of digits involved. Elimination
12 of space between words or numeric descriptions, or the use of

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 3. A newspaper qualified to publish notices under the laws of
2 this state has the authority to publish all notices in a format
3 readable to a reasonable person. Type size used for notices shall
4 be no less than eight-point noncondensed type, with a maximum of
5 ten-point noncondensed type, unless a larger type size is requested
6 in writing by the person desiring the notice. Any notice submitted
7 to a newspaper which is unreadable or intended to be unreadable may
8 be rejected or typeset by the newspaper in a readable format;
9 4. Newspapers providing affidavits or proof of publication are
10 permitted to charge and collect a fee for a notarial act as defined
11 in Section 5 of Title 49 of the Oklahoma Statutes or Section 209 of
12 Title 49 of the Oklahoma Statutes as applicable;
13 5. For any notice placed in a newspaper, there shall be a
14 minimum charge of Twenty-five Dollars ($25.00) for such notice if
15 the total calculated cost of words and lines described in paragraphs
16 1 and 2 of this subsection is less than Twenty-five Dollars
17 ($25.00);
18 6. When notice by publication in a newspaper is required by
19 law, order of court, or other means, the newspaper publishing the
20 notice shall, at no additional cost to the person or official
21 desiring the notice, cause the notice to be placed on the newspaper
22 website if the newspaper operates a website, in front of any paywall
23 or paid access to the website. Additionally, the newspaper shall
24 upload notices to a statewide website established and maintained as

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 a jo int venture of a majority of Oklahoma newspapers qualified to
2 publish notices by the laws of this state. Posting of notices on
3 the newspaper website and statewide website shall be performed in a
4 timely manner but shall not invalidate the publication of a notice
5 otherwise valid when published in the newspaper. Temporary outages
6 or service interruptions prohibiting the posting or display of such
7 notice shall be considered harmless error and the proper legal
8 notice requirements shall be considered met if the notice published
9 in the newspaper otherwise complies with the requirements for
10 publication. Access to the statewide website shall be free to the
11 public; and
12 7. Any person or official required to publish a notice shall
13 submit the notice in writing or by electronic means by the deadline
14 established by the newspaper for such notices. No deadline shall be
15 set by the newspaper that is more than five (5) business days prior
16 to the date of publication, excluding the date of publication,
17 Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays in the State of Oklahoma.
18 Upon request, the newspaper shall confirm receipt of the notice and
19 notify the person or official in writing or by electronic means of
20 the dates of the issue or issues in which the notice shall appear.
21 If the newspaper fails to publish the notice in the issue
22 specified in the confirmation, the newspaper shall publish the
23 notice in a future issue specified by the person or official
24 desiring the notice at no charge. If the notice has multiple

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 inse rtions, only the first notice omitted by the newspaper shall be
2 required to run at no charge.
3 B. The county treasurer shall collect the cost of publication
4 of lists of lands and town lots sold for delinquent taxes at
5 original sale or resale from the individuals purchasing the lands
6 and town lots at the sale or resale involved, and shall deposit all
7 monies collected to the credit of the fund of the county which paid
8 the cost of publication. The publisher of the notice shall be paid
9 for the publication of the notice from the general fund of the
10 county or, if monies are not available in the general fund of the
11 county at the time of publication, from the "Resale-Property Fund"
12 of the county.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

11 An Act relating to title theft; authorizing filing of
certain notice; establishing requirements for filing
12 of certain notice; specifying required contents of
certain notice; authorizing charging of fees for
13 recording certain notice; requiring county clerk to
provide copy of certain notice to district attorney;
14 authorizing refusal to file notice under certain
circumstances; authorizing certain petition;
15 establishing felony offenses for title theft;
establishing elements of certain offenses;
16 establishing punishments for certain offenses;
requiring order of restitution for certain offenses;
17 requiring county clerk to post certain signage;
providing for codification; and providing an
18 effective date.

Req. No. 1851 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 311 of Title 16, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. A person who is the victim of title theft as defined in
5 Section 2 of this act may file of record a notice of fraudulent
6 conveyance, duly verified by oath, setting forth the nature of the
7 fraudulent conveyance. Such notice may be filed of record by the
8 victim or by any other person acting on behalf of the victim who is
9 under a disability or otherwise unable to assert the claim on his or
10 her own behalf. Such notice shall serve as constructive notice that
11 the conveyance is alleged to be fraudulent.
12 B. To be effective and entitled to be recorded, notice of a
13 fraudulent conveyance shall contain an accurate and full description
14 of the real property affected by the recorded fraudulent conveyance.
15 Such notice of fraudulent conveyance shall be filed for record in
16 the county clerk’s office of the county or counties where the real
17 property is situated. Except as provided in subsection D of this
18 section, the county clerk shall accept any notice presented that
19 describes real property located in the county and shall enter,
20 record, and index such notice in the same manner that a deed is
21 recorded. A county clerk may charge fees for the recording of the
22 notice in accordance with the county clerk fee schedule set forth in
23 Section 32 of Title 28 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The name or names
24
Req. No. 1851 Page 2
1 of a ny victim appearing in such notice shall be entered as a grantee
2 or grantees in such indexes.
3 C. Upon the filing of a notice of fraudulent conveyance, the
4 county clerk shall deliver a copy of the notice to the district
5 attorney for investigation and potential prosecution.
6 D. The county clerk may refuse to file a notice of fraudulent
7 conveyance provided for in subsection B of this section if the clerk
8 has a reasonable belief that the notice constitutes sham legal
9 process as defined in subsection H of Section 1533 of Title 21 of
10 the Oklahoma Statutes, or if the clerk has a reasonable belief that
11 the notice is being presented as a slander of title to the real
12 property. If the county clerk refuses to file a notice of
13 fraudulent conveyance, the aggrieved party may petition the district
14 court for a writ of mandamus to compel the county clerk to record
15 the notice pursuant to the provisions of Section 75 of Title 16 of
16 the Oklahoma Statutes.
17 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1534 of Title 21, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. A person commits title theft when he or she:
21 1. Intentionally alters, falsifies, forges, or misrepresents a
22 document pertaining to real property, with the intent to deceive,
23 defraud, or unlawfully transfer or encumber the ownership rights of
24 the owner of the real property;
Req. No. 1851 Page 3
1 2. With intent to defraud, misrepresents himself or herself as
2 the owner or authorized representative of the owner of real property
3 to induce another person to rely on such false information to obtain
4 ownership or possession of such real property; or
5 3. With intent to defraud, takes, obtains, steals, encumbers,
6 or transfers title or an interest in real property by fraud,
7 forgery, larceny, or any other fraudulent or deceptive practice.
8 B. A person who procures, files, or causes to be filed of
9 public record any document pertaining to an interest in real
10 property with intent to deceive another person as to the veracity of
11 the document recorded shall be, upon conviction, guilty of a felony
12 punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of
13 Corrections for a term not to exceed three (3) years, a fine not to
14 exceed Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00), or by both such
15 imprisonment and fine. Additionally, the court shall order
16 restitution to be paid to the aggrieved party as authorized by
17 Section 991f of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
18 C. A person who knowingly and willfully procures, files, or
19 causes to be filed of public record a document pertaining to real
20 property in this state with intent to defraud the owner of the real
21 property or the owner of an interest in the real property shall be,
22 upon conviction, guilty of a felony punishable by imprisonment in
23 the custody of the Department of Corrections for a term not to
24 exceed ten (10) years, a fine not to exceed Five Thousand Dollars
Req. No. 1851 Page 4
1 ($5, 000.00), or by both such imprisonment and fine. Additionally,
2 the court shall order restitution to be paid to the aggrieved party
3 as authorized by Section 991f of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
4 D. A county clerk shall post a sign, in letters at least one
5 (1) inch in height, that is clearly visible to the general public in
6 or near the county clerk’s office stating that it is a crime to
7 knowingly file a fraudulent document pertaining to real property
8 with the county clerk.
9 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

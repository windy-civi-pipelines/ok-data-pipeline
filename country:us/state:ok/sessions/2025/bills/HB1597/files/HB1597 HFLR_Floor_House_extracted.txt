11 An Act relating to crimes and punishments; defining

12 penalties; providing for codification; and providing
an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 540D of Title 21, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. As used in this section:
20 1. "First responder" shall include a peace officer as defined
21 in Section 99 of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, a correctional
22 or probation and parole officer, and an emergency medical technician
23 or emergency medical care provider; and
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. "Harass" means to willfully engage in a course of conduct
2 directed at a first responder which intentionally distracts the
3 first responder from performing his or her official duties and
4 serves no legitimate purpose.
5 B. It shall be unlawful for a person, after receiving a verbal
6 warning not to approach from a person he or she knows or reasonably
7 should know is a first responder, who is engaged in the lawful
8 performance of a legal duty, to knowingly and willfully violate such
9 warning and approach or remain within twenty-five (25) feet of the
10 first responder with the intent to:
11 1. Impede or interfere with the ability of the first responder
12 to perform such duty;
13 2. Threaten the first responder with physical harm; or
14 3. Harass the first responder.
15 C. Any person who violates the provisions of this section
16 shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by
17 imprisonment in the county jail for a term not to exceed one (1)
18 year, or by a fine not to exceed One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00),
19 or by both such fine and imprisonment.
20 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

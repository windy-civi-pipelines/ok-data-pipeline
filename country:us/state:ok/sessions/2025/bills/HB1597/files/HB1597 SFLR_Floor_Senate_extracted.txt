8 An Act relating to crimes and punishments; defining
terms; making certain acts unlawful; providing
9 penalties; providing for codification; and providing
an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 540D of Title 21, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. As used in this section:
17 1. "First responder" shall include a peace officer as defined
18 in Section 99 of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, a correctional
19 or probation and parole officer, and an emergency medical technician
20 or emergency medical care provider; and
21 2. "Harass" means to willfully engage in a course of conduct
22 directed at a first responder which intentionally distracts the
23 first responder from performing his or her official duties and
24 serves no legitimate purpose.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1597 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 B. It shall be unlawful for a person, after receiving a verbal
2 warning not to approach from a person he or she knows or reasonably
3 should know is a first responder, who is engaged in the lawful
4 performance of a legal duty, to knowingly and willfully violate such
5 warning and approach or remain within twenty-five (25) feet of the
6 first responder with the intent to:
7 1. Impede or interfere with the ability of the first responder
8 to perform such duty;
9 2. Threaten the first responder with physical harm; or
10 3. Harass the first responder.
11 C. Any person who violates the provisions of this section
12 shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by
13 imprisonment in the county jail for a term not to exceed one (1)
14 year, or by a fine not to exceed One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00),
15 or by both such fine and imprisonment.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
17 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY

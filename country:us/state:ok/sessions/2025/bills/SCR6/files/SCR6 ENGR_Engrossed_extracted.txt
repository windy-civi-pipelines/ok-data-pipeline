14 WHEREAS, the Month of the Military Child was established in 1986

16 honor the sacrifices made by military families and to acknowledge
17 the vital role military children play within the armed forces
18 community; and

1 WHEREAS, these children exemplify resilience, courage, and
2 strength, navigating the hardships of military life with admirable
3 perseverance and adaptability; and
4 WHEREAS, the tradition of wearing purple, known as the “purple
5 suit,” symbolizes unity across all branches of the military—
6 combining Air Force blue, Army green, Navy blue, Marine red, and
7 Coast Guard blue to represent a shared commitment to service and
8 sacrifice; and
9 WHEREAS, on April 15, wearing purple serves as a visible tribute
10 to military children, celebrating their important contributions.

12 OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
13 CONCURRING THEREIN:
14 THAT the Oklahoma State Legislature designates April 15 as
15 Purple Up! For Military Kids Day in Oklahoma.
16 THAT the Oklahoma State Legislature expresses gratitude to
17 military families and their children for their sacrifice and
18 dedication to our state and nation.

Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to marriage; enacting the Child
Protection Act of 2025; amending 43 O.S. 2021,
8 Section 3, which relates to marriage; modifying
exception; providing for noncodification; and
9 providing an effective date.
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
13 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
14 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Child
15 Protection Act of 2025".
16 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 43 O.S. 2021, Section 3, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 3. A. Any unmarried person who is at least eighteen
19 (18) years of age and not otherwise disqualified is capable of
20 contracting and consenting to marriage with a person of the opposite
21 sex.
22 B. 1. Except as otherwise provided by this subsection, no No
23 person under the age of eighteen (18) years shall enter into the
24 marriage relation, nor shall any license issue therefor, except:
Req. No. 10740 Page 1
1 a. upon the consent and authority expressly given by the
2 parent or guardian of such underage applicant in the
3 presence of the authority issuing such license,
4 b. upon the written consent of the parent or guardian of
5 such underage applicant executed and acknowledged in
6 person before a judge of the district court or the
7 court clerk of any county within the State of
8 Oklahoma,
9 c. if the parent or guardian resides outside of the State
10 of Oklahoma, upon the written consent of the parent or
11 guardian executed before a judge or clerk of a court
12 of record. The executed foreign consent shall be duly
13 authenticated in the same manner as proof of documents
14 from foreign jurisdictions,
15 d. if the certificate of a duly licensed medical doctor
16 or osteopath, acknowledged in the manner provided by
17 law for the acknowledgment of deeds, and stating that
18 such parent or guardian is unable by reason of health
19 or incapacity to be present in person, is presented to
20 such licensing authority, upon the written consent of
21 the parent or guardian, acknowledged in the same
22 manner as the accompanying medical certificate,
23 e. if the parent or guardian is on active duty with the
24 Armed Forces of the United States, upon the written
Req. No. 10740 Page 2
1 permission of the parent or guardian, acknowledged in
2 the manner provided by law for acknowledgment of deeds
3 by military personnel authorized to administer oaths.
4 Such permission shall be presented to the licensing
5 authority, accompanied by a certificate executed by a
6 commissioned officer in command of the applicant, to
7 the effect that the parent or guardian is on active
8 duty in the Armed Forces of the United States, or
9 f. upon affidavit of three (3) reputable persons stating
10 that both parents of the minor are deceased, or
11 mentally incompetent, or their whereabouts are unknown
12 to the minor, and that no guardian has theretofore
13 been appointed for the minor. The judge of the
14 district court issuing the license may in his or her
15 discretion consent to the marriage in the same manner
16 as in all cases in which consent may be given by a
17 parent or guardian
18 if a court has conferred upon the minor the rights of majority
19 pursuant to Section 91 et seq. of Title 10 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
20 2. Every person under the age of sixteen (16) years is
21 expressly forbidden and prohibited from entering into the marriage
22 relation except when authorized by the court:
23 a. in settlement of a suit for seduction or paternity, or
24
Req. No. 10740 Page 3
1 b. if the unmarried female is pregnant, or has given
2 birth to an illegitimate child and at least one parent
3 of each minor, or the guardian or custodian of such
4 child, is present before the court and has an
5 opportunity to present evidence in the event such
6 parent, guardian, or custodian objects to the issuance
7 of a marriage license. If they are not present the
8 parent, guardian, or custodian may be given notice of
9 the hearing at the discretion of the court.
10 3. A parent or a guardian of any child under the age of
11 eighteen (18) years who is in the custody of the Department of Human
12 Services or the Department of Juvenile Justice shall not be eligible
13 to consent to the marriage of such minor child as required by the
14 provisions of this subsection.
15 4. Any certificate or written permission required by this
16 subsection shall be retained by the official issuing the marriage
17 license.
18 C. No marriage may be authorized when such marriage would be
19 incestuous under this chapter.
20 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

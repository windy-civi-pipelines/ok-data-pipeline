7 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Firefighters Pension
and Retirement System; amending 11 O.S. 2021, Section
9 49-101, as amended by Section 3, Chapter 232, O.S.L.
2022 (11 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 49-101), which
10 relates to volunteer firefighter pension; modifying
pension amount; modifying compensation determination;
11 and updating statutory language.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 49-101, as
16 amended by Section 3, Chapter 232, O.S.L. 2022 (11 O.S. Supp. 2024,
17 Section 49-101), is amended to read as follows:
18 Section 49-101. A. All retired volunteer firefighters who
19 qualify for retirement shall be entitled to a monthly pension. The
20 monthly pension of a volunteer firefighter who retires prior to the
21 effective date of this act shall be in the amount retired volunteer
22 firefighters are receiving at the time the volunteer firefighter
23 begins to receive a pension for each year of credited service not to
24 exceed thirty (30) years. A volunteer firefighter who retires on
Req. No. 1763 Page 1
RBS No. 1763
1 and after the effective date of this act shall receive a monthly
2 pension in the amount of Ten Dollars ($10.00) for each year of
3 credited service not to exceed thirty (30) years. In determining
4 the number of years of credited service, a fractional year of six
5 (6) months or more shall be counted as one (1) full year and a
6 fractional year of less than six (6) months shall not be counted in
7 such determination. Retired volunteer firefighters of a
8 municipality shall receive a pension of not less than that which
9 retired volunteer firefighters of such municipality were receiving
10 on June 30, 1985.
11 B. A volunteer firefighter who terminates service after
12 completing ten (10) years of credited service, but less than twenty
13 (20) years of credited service, shall be entitled to receive a
14 vested benefit commencing on the date the firefighter reaches fifty
15 (50) years of age or the date the firefighter would have had twenty
16 (20) years of credited service had the firefighter’s service
17 continued uninterrupted, whichever is later. The monthly amount of
18 such retirement benefit shall be the amount being paid to volunteer
19 firefighters at the time the member vests multiplied by the number
20 of years of credited service. Credited service must be established
21 at the time of the volunteer firefighter’s termination. If a
22 volunteer firefighter who terminated employment and elected, or was
23 eligible to elect, a vested benefit dies prior to being eligible to
24 receive benefits, the volunteer firefighter’s beneficiary, as
Req. No. 1763 Page 2
RBS No. 1763
1 defi ned in paragraph 16 of Section 49-100.1 of this title, shall be
2 entitled to the volunteer firefighter’s normal monthly retirement
3 benefit on the date the deceased volunteer firefighter would have
4 been eligible to receive the benefit.

16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 15.8, as
18 last amended by Section 1, Chapter 25, O.S.L. 2023 (59 O.S. Supp.
19 2024, Section 15.8), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 15.8. A. A qualification applicant to qualify as a
21 candidate for examination shall file an application for
22 qualification in a format approved by the Oklahoma Accountancy
23 Board. The fee for the qualification application shall be
24 determined by the Board and shall not exceed Three Hundred Dollars
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 1
1 ($30 0.00). Every qualification applicant to qualify as a candidate
2 for the certificate of certified public accountant shall submit to a
3 national criminal history record search, must be a resident of this
4 state immediately prior to making application and, except as
5 otherwise provided in this section, shall meet the education and
6 experience requirements, except as otherwise provided in this
7 section. The costs associated with the national criminal history
8 records search shall be paid by the applicant.
9 B. On or after the effective date of this act November 1, 2025,
10 every qualification applicant to qualify as a candidate for
11 examination for the certificate of certified public accountant shall
12 have at least one hundred twenty (120) semester hours, or the
13 equivalent thereof, of college education including a baccalaureate
14 or higher degree, or the equivalent thereof, conferred by a college
15 or university acceptable to the Board from an accredited four-year
16 college or university in this state or any other accredited four-
17 year college or university recognized by the Board. The total
18 educational program of the applicant for examination shall include
19 an accounting concentration or its equivalent as determined
20 acceptable by the Board which shall include not less than twenty-
21 four (24) semester hours, or the equivalent thereof, in accounting
22 courses above principles of accounting or introductory accounting,
23 with at least one course in auditing or assurance; the remaining
24 accounting courses shall be selected from financial accounting,
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 2
1 acco unting theory, cost/managerial accounting, federal income tax,
2 governmental, not-for-profit accounting, accounting information
3 systems, accounting history and other accounting electives; at least
4 nine (9) semester hours shall be from any or all of the subjects of
5 economics, statistics, business law, finance, business management,
6 marketing, business communication, risk management, insurance,
7 management information systems, or computer science at the upper-
8 division level of college or above or the equivalent of such
9 subjects as determined by the Board; all the remaining semester
10 hours, if any, shall be elective.
11 C. The costs associated with the national criminal history
12 record check shall be paid by the applicant.
13 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 15.9, as
14 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 25, O.S.L. 2023 (59 O.S. Supp.
15 2024, Section 15.9), is amended to read as follows:
16 Section 15.9. A. Upon payment of appropriate fees, the
17 Oklahoma Accountancy Board shall grant a certificate to any
18 individual of good character who meets the applicable education,
19 experience and testing requirements provided for in this section and
20 in Sections 15.8 and 15.10 of this title. For purposes of this
21 subsection, good character means an individual who does not have a
22 history of dishonest acts as demonstrated by documented evidence and
23 has not been convicted, pled guilty, or pled nolo contendere to a
24 felony charge. The Board may refuse to grant a certificate to an
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 3
1 appl icant for failure to satisfy the requirement of good character.
2 The Board shall provide to the denied applicant written notification
3 specifying grounds for denial of a certificate including failure to
4 meet the good character criterion. Appeal of the action of the
5 Board may be made in accordance with the provisions of the
6 Administrative Procedures Act.
7 B. The Board shall issue certificates as certified public
8 accountants to those applicants who have met the qualifications
9 required by the provisions of the Oklahoma Accountancy Act and the
10 applicable rules of the Board, and have passed an examination in
11 accounting, auditing and related subjects as the Board determines
12 appropriate with such grades that satisfy the Board that each
13 applicant is competent to practice as a certified public accountant.
14 C. The Board may make use of all or any part of the Uniform
15 Certified Public Accountant Examination and any organization that
16 assists in providing the examination.
17 D. An applicant for initial issuance of a certificate pursuant
18 to this section shall show that the applicant has at least one
19 hundred fifty (150) semester hours, or the equivalent thereof, of
20 college education including a baccalaureate or higher degree, or the
21 equivalent thereof, conferred by a college or university acceptable
22 to the Board from an accredited four-year college or university in
23 this state or any other accredited four-year college or university
24 recognized by the Board. Of the one hundred fifty (150) semester
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 4
1 hour s, not less than thirty (30) semester hours, or the equivalent
2 thereof, shall be in accounting courses above principles of
3 accounting or introductory accounting. These education requirements
4 shall have been completed prior to submitting an application to the
5 Board and within the time frame outlined in this section. A
6 qualified applicant may take the certified public accountant
7 examination as allowed in Section 15.8 of this title but shall not
8 be issued a certificate until the education requirements pursuant to
9 this section and Section 15.8 of this title have been met.
10 E. An applicant for initial issuance of a certificate under
11 this section shall show that the applicant has had one (1) year of
12 experience. Experience shall be defined by the Board by rule and
13 shall include providing a type of service or advice involving the
14 use of accounting, attest, compilation, management advisory,
15 financial advisory, tax or consulting skills, and be satisfied
16 through work experience in government, industry, academia or public
17 practice, all of which shall be verified by a certificate or license
18 holder or an individual approved by the Board. Upon completion of
19 the requirements of Section 15.8 of this title, a qualified
20 applicant for the examination may take the certified public
21 accountant or public accountant examination prior to earning the
22 experience required in this subsection, but shall not be issued a
23 certificate until the experience requirement has been met.
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 5
1 F. Every applicant for the certificate of certified public
2 accountant shall provide evidence of successful completion of an
3 ethics examination prescribed by the Board.
4 G. Every applicant for the certificate of certified public
5 accountant shall submit to a national criminal history record check
6 in accordance with Section 150.9 of Title 74 of the Oklahoma
7 Statutes. Results of national criminal history record checks shall
8 be provided to the Oklahoma Accountancy Board by the Oklahoma State
9 Bureau of Investigation. Criminal history record check results are
10 confidential and shall not be disseminated outside the Oklahoma
11 Accountancy Board. The costs associated with the national criminal
12 history record check shall be paid by the applicant.
13 H. An individual applying for a certificate as a certified
14 public accountant must make an application for the certificate
15 within five (5) years of the date the Board notifies the candidate
16 that the candidate has successfully passed all sections of the
17 C.P.A. Examination. If the candidate fails to make an application
18 for the certificate within five (5) years, the candidate must
19 provide documentation showing he or she has completed at least one
20 hundred twenty (120) hours of qualifying continuing public
21 accountancy education completed within the three-year period
22 immediately preceding the date the individual applies for
23 certification. The Board shall establish rules whereby time limits
24 set for application pursuant to this provision may, upon written
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 6
1 appl ication to the Board, be waived or reduced if the candidate is
2 called to active military service or becomes incapacitated as a
3 result of illness or injury or for such other good causes as
4 determined by the Board on a case-by-case basis.
5 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 15.13, as
6 amended by Section 6, Chapter 26, O.S.L. 2022 (59 O.S. Supp. 2024,
7 Section 15.13), is amended to read as follows:
8 Section 15.13. A. The Oklahoma Accountancy Board may issue a
9 certificate to an applicant who has been authorized to practice
10 public accounting as a certified public accountant pursuant to the
11 laws of any jurisdiction if the applicant passed a test administered
12 for the purpose of authorizing an individual to practice as a
13 certified public accountant with grades which were equivalent to
14 passing a test for the same purpose in this state as of the date the
15 applicant originally passed the examination, and the applicant:
16 1. Meets the requirements for issuance of a certificate in this
17 state on the date of making application;
18 2. Met, on the date the certificate was issued by the other
19 jurisdiction, the requirements in effect on that date for issuance
20 of a certificate in this state; or
21 3. Met, on the date of becoming a candidate in another
22 jurisdiction, the requirements of becoming a candidate in this
23 state, except for residency.
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 7
1 B. In the event an applicant does not meet the requirements of
2 subsection A of this section, but has passed a test administered for
3 the purpose of authorizing an individual to practice as a certified
4 public accountant with grades which were equivalent to passing a
5 test for the same purpose in this state on the date the applicant
6 passed the examination, the Board may issue a certificate to an
7 applicant if such applicant has four (4) years of experience
8 practicing public accounting as a certified public accountant
9 pursuant to the laws of any jurisdiction. Such experience must have
10 occurred within the ten (10) years immediately preceding the
11 application. Experience acceptable to satisfy the requirements of
12 this subsection shall be determined by standards established by the
13 Board.
14 C. An applicant who is seeking a permit to practice under this
15 section must also provide satisfactory documentation to the Board
16 that such applicant has met the continuing professional education
17 requirements, as provided in Section 15.35 of this title, in effect
18 on the date of the application.
19 D. The Board may issue a certificate by reciprocity to the
20 extent required by treaties entered into by the government of the
21 United States.
22 E. A fee in the amount equal to the registration fee and permit
23 fee, if applicable, plus an administrative fee, the total of which
24 shall not exceed Three Hundred Dollars ($300.00), shall be paid by
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 8
1 an a pplicant seeking a certificate pursuant to the provisions of
2 this section. The total amount shall be established by Board rule.
3 F. On or after July 1, 2005, an applicant for the certificate
4 of certified public accountant under this section shall provide
5 evidence of successful completion of an ethics examination
6 prescribed by the Board.
7 G. As an alternative to the requirements of subsection A, B or
8 C of this section, a certificate holder licensed by another
9 jurisdiction who establishes the certificate holder's principal
10 place of business in this state shall request the issuance of a
11 certificate from the Board prior to establishing such principal
12 place of business. The Board shall issue a certificate to such
13 person who obtains from the NASBA National Qualification Appraisal
14 Service verification that such individual's CPA qualifications are
15 substantially equivalent to the CPA licensure requirements of the
16 AICPA/NASBA Uniform Accountancy Act.
17 H. An applicant for the certificate of certified public
18 accountant under this section shall submit to a national criminal
19 history record check in accordance with Section 150.9 of Title 74 of
20 the Oklahoma Statutes. Results of national criminal history record
21 checks shall be provided to the Oklahoma Accountancy Board by the
22 Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation. Criminal history record
23 check results are confidential and shall not be disseminated outside
24 the Oklahoma Accountancy Board. The costs associated with the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2736 Page 9
1 nati onal criminal history record check shall be paid by the
2 applicant.
3 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

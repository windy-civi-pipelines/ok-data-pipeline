7 An Act relating to elections; amending 26 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 3-101, as amended by Section 2, Chapter 329,
8 O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 3-101),
which relates to dates of elections held by political
9 subdivisions; modifying dates upon which certain
elections may be held; updating statutory reference;
10 updating statutory language; and providing an
effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 3-101, as
15 amended by Section 2, Chapter 329, O.S.L. 2023 (26 O.S. Supp. 2024,
16 Section 3-101), is amended to read as follows:
17 Section 3-101. A. No election required to be conducted by any
18 county election board shall be scheduled for a day other than
19 Tuesday.
20 B. Except as otherwise provided by law, no regular or special
21 election to fill an elective office for any purpose shall be held by
22 any county, school district, technology center school district,
23 municipality, fire protection district or other political
24 subdivision authorized to call elections except as follows:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2106 Page 1
1 1. The second Tuesday of February in any year;
2 2. The first Tuesday of April in any year;
3 3. The date of any regularly scheduled statewide state or
4 federal election in an even-numbered year;
5 4. The second third Tuesday of September June in an odd-
6 numbered any year;
7 5. 4. The second fourth Tuesday of November August in an odd-
8 numbered any year; and
9 6. 5. The second first Tuesday after the first Monday of June
10 of an odd-numbered year for a special election to fill a vacancy
11 November in any year.
12 C. Except as otherwise provided by law or by Section 1 of this
13 act, no election for any purpose other than to fill an elective
14 office shall be held by any county, school district, technology
15 center school district, municipality, fire protection district or
16 other political subdivision authorized to call elections except on:
17 1. The second Tuesday of January, February, May, June, July,
18 August, September, October and November and the first Tuesday in
19 March and April in odd-numbered years; provided, a municipality with
20 a population in excess of two hundred fifty thousand (250,000)
21 persons, according to the most recent Federal Decennial Census, may
22 also hold an election on the second Tuesday of December in odd-
23 numbered years; and
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2106 Page 2
1 2. The second Tuesday of January and February, the first
2 Tuesday in March and April, the third Tuesday in June, the fourth
3 Tuesday in August, and the first Tuesday after the first Monday in
4 November of any even-numbered year.
5 D. In the event that a regular or special election date occurs
6 on an official state holiday, the election shall be scheduled for
7 the next following Tuesday. In the event that any day of a
8 candidate filing period occurs on a Saturday, Sunday or any official
9 state holiday, that day of the filing period shall be scheduled for
10 the next business day.
11 D. For a special election called by the Governor pursuant to
12 law, the Governor shall be limited to the dates stated in subsection
13 B of this section. Provided, if a vacancy has occurred in the
14 office of United States Representative, State Senator, or State
15 Representative, or in the event of a declared national or state
16 emergency that requires a special election, the Governor may also
17 use the following dates for a special election called pursuant to
18 law:
19 1. The second Tuesday of January of any year;
20 2. The first Tuesday of March of any year;
21 3. The first Tuesday of May of an odd-numbered year;
22 4. The second Tuesday of July of any year;
23 5. The second Tuesday of September of an odd-numbered year;
24 6. The first Tuesday of October of an odd-numbered year; and
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2106 Page 3
1 7. The first Tuesday of December of any year.
2 E. Notwithstanding any other provision of law or any provision
3 of a municipal charter, any municipality, school district,
4 technology center district, county, rural fire protection district,
5 or any other entity seeking to hold a regular or special election to
6 be conducted by a county election board on the same date as a
7 regular or special federal or state election, shall file the
8 resolution calling for the election with the county election board
9 secretary no later than seventy-five (75) days prior to the election
10 date. A candidate filing period of three (3) days, if so required
11 by the resolution, shall begin no later than ten (10) days following
12 the deadline to file the resolution with the secretary of the county
13 election board; provided, the filing period for such municipal
14 office may be scheduled on the same dates as the filing period for
15 state or federal office to be filled at such election.
16 F. Any school district, technology center district,
17 municipality, including any municipality governed by charter, rural
18 fire protection district or any other entity seeking to hold a
19 special election for the purpose of filling a vacancy shall schedule
20 a candidate filing period of three (3) days to begin not more than
21 twenty (20) days following the date the resolution calling the
22 election is required to be filed with the secretary of the county
23 election board.
24 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

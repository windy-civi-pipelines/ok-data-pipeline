11 An Act relating to public finance; enacting the
Foreign Adversary Divestment Act of 2025; making
12 findings; defining terms; prohibiting certain
entities from owning certain investments; prescribing
13 procedures for divestment; prescribing duties for the
State Treasurer; providing for construction of act;
14 providing immunity from certain liability for
specific pension systems; providing for affirmative
15 defense; providing for indemnification; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11001 of Title 62, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Foreign
23 Adversary Divestment Act of 2025”.
24
HB1561 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11002 of Title 62, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 The Foreign Adversary Divestment Act of 2025 will ensure that
5 Oklahoma public investment dollars are not put at risk in countries
6 of concern and do not fund the development of the military
7 technologies and surveillance tools of foreign adversaries that
8 impede the furthering of Oklahoma’s interests and the interests of
9 the United States of America, by requiring the divestment of public
10 pension dollars and university endowment funds from countries of
11 concern.
12 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11003 of Title 62, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 As used in this act:
16 1. “Company” shall mean any sole proprietorship, organization,
17 association, corporation, partnership, joint venture, limited
18 partnership, limited liability partnership, limited liability
19 company, or other entity or business association, including wholly
20 owned subsidiaries, majority-owned subsidiaries, parent companies,
21 or affiliates of those entities or business associations, that exist
22 for the purpose of making profit;
23 2. “Divestment” shall mean the sale or otherwise contractual
24 end of any current or planned investments;
HB1561 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 3. “Domicile” shall mean either the country in which a company
2 is registered, where the company’s affairs are primarily completed,
3 or where the majority of ownership share is held;
4 4. “Foreign adversary” shall mean any country designated by the
5 United States Secretary of State as hostile or a Country of
6 Particular Concern (CPC);
7 5. “Investment” shall mean any transfer of funds in any active
8 or passive, direct or indirect structure which seeks to generate
9 revenue or accomplish any other gain, including nonmonetary;
10 6. “Official government capacity” shall mean any role, elected
11 or appointed, in a government or its subsidiaries;
12 7. “State-managed fund” shall mean any short- or long-term
13 investment structure which is state-managed, state-run, state-
14 controlled, or otherwise overseen by Oklahoma, a state agency, or
15 any political subdivision within Oklahoma or agency controlled by
16 the same, in which the state or subdivision has primary discretion
17 over or vested interest in. Nothing in this definition shall be
18 construed so as to not apply to any fund which is subject to the
19 purview or direction of Oklahoma or applicable subdivision, and
20 populated, wholly or in part, with state funds, but managed by a
21 third-party entity, such as a fiduciary. This shall include, but is
22 not limited to:
23 a. public pension funds, public retirement funds, or
24 other state-sponsored funds, that is sponsored,
HB1561 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 maintained, or contributed to or required to be
2 contributed to by Oklahoma or any locality within
3 Oklahoma, or any subsidiaries of such,
4 b. city, county, or other local or municipal entity
5 investment funds, “rainy day funds”, or other
6 political-subdivision-controlled investment
7 structures, and
8 c. university or college endowments, trusts, or other
9 structure which constitutes, wholly or in part, a
10 public institution of higher education’s investable
11 assets; and
12 8. “State-owned enterprise” shall mean any company owned or
13 controlled, in whole or in part, by a government or by individuals
14 acting in official government capacities in any form.
15 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11004 of Title 62, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. All state and local managed funds are prohibited from
19 holding investments in any:
20 1. Foreign adversary;
21 2. Company owned or controlled by a foreign adversary;
22 3. State-owned enterprise of a foreign adversary;
23 4. Company domiciled within a foreign adversary; or
24 5. Other entity within a foreign adversary.
HB1561 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. All state and local managed funds are prohibited from
2 investing or depositing public funds in any bank that is domiciled
3 or has its principal place of business in a foreign adversary.
4 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11005 of Title 62, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 A. All state-managed funds are required to immediately in good
8 faith begin divestment of any holdings prohibited in this act, with
9 total divestment achieved by January 1, 2028, or two (2) years after
10 the effective date of this act, whichever is earliest.
11 B. For purposes of this section, “total divestment” shall mean
12 reducing the value of prohibited investments to no more than five-
13 hundredths of one percent (0.0005) of the market value of all assets
14 under management by a state or locally managed fund.
15 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11006 of Title 62, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The State Treasurer shall develop a notification system to
19 be able to identify foreign adversaries and countries of particular
20 concern. Such information shall be available to the entities
21 subject to the provisions of this act.
22 B. No later than six (6) months after the effective date of
23 this act, the entities subject to the provisions of this act shall
24 identify all companies that are state-owned enterprises of, are
HB1561 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 domi ciled within, whose primary affairs are conducted within, or
2 whose majority ownership is held within a foreign adversary, and
3 companies owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. The
4 identification process shall include, but are not limited to the
5 following:
6 1. Reviewing and relying on publicly available information
7 regarding companies that are state-owned enterprises of, are
8 domiciled within, whose primary affairs are conducted within, or
9 whose majority ownership share is held within a foreign adversary,
10 including information provided by nonprofit organizations, research
11 firms, and government entities;
12 2. Contacting asset managers and fund managers contracted by a
13 state-managed fund that invest in companies and in funds that are
14 state-owned enterprises of, are domiciled within, whose primary
15 affairs are conducted within, or whose majority or plurality
16 ownership share is held within a foreign adversary;
17 3. Contacting other institutional investors that have divested
18 from or engaged with companies that are state-owned enterprises of,
19 are domiciled within, whose primary affairs are conducted within, or
20 whose majority ownership share is held within a foreign adversary;
21 and
22 4. Retaining an independent research firm to identify companies
23 that are directly or indirectly investment holdings of a state or
24 local fund that are state-owned enterprises of, are domiciled
HB1561 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 with in, whose primary affairs are conducted within, or whose
2 majority or plurality ownership share is held within a foreign
3 adversary.
4 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11007 of Title 62, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 Nothing in this act shall be construed so as to inhibit,
8 conflict, impede, or otherwise interfere with any required financial
9 safeguards, fiduciary requirements, and other sound investment
10 criteria which any state-managed fund is subject to, nor any
11 applicable state or federal law.
12 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11008 of Title 62, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. As used in this section, "public pension system" means the:
16 1. Oklahoma Firefighters Pension and Retirement System;
17 2. Oklahoma Police Pension and Retirement System;
18 3. Uniform Retirement System for Justices and Judges;
19 4. Oklahoma Law Enforcement Retirement System;
20 5. Teachers' Retirement System of Oklahoma; and
21 6. Oklahoma Public Employees Retirement System.
22 B. No public pension system shall be liable in any action for
23 damages based upon the loss or alleged loss of value in pension
24 system assets based upon the provisions of this act. The provisions
HB1561 HFLR Page 7
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 of t his subsection shall also be applicable to any action filed
2 naming the governing board of a public pension system, one or more
3 members of the governing board of a public pension system, or any of
4 its employees as defendants.
5 C. In any action filed naming a public pension system or any
6 other person described in subsection B of this section as
7 defendants, it shall be a defense to the action that the value of
8 pension system assets increased at any time after the date of the
9 alleged decrease in the value of pension system assets resulting
10 from failure to comply with the provisions of this act. The
11 provisions of this subsection shall be applicable with regard to an
12 increase in the value of pension system assets occurring at any time
13 after such date including the date as of which the action is filed.
14 For purposes of this section, any increase in the value of pension
15 system assets occurring during the pendency of an appeal from a
16 final judgment or other order establishing liability for damages may
17 also be used in defense of the claim for damages and the time period
18 shall be inclusive of any period during which a case is remanded
19 from an appellate court to a district court.
20 SECTION 9. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11009 of Title 62, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. The State of Oklahoma, public pension system plans as
24 defined in this act, as well as officers, board members, and
HB1561 HFLR Page 8
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 empl oyees of the state or the public pension systems are immune from
2 civil liability for any act or omission related to any provision
3 under this act.
4 B. In addition to the immunity provided under subsection A of
5 this section, officers, board members, and employees of the state or
6 the public pension system plans are entitled to indemnification from
7 the public pension system for all losses, costs and expenses,
8 including reasonable attorney fees, associated with defending
9 against any claim or suit related to any provision of this act.
10 SECTION 10. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

6 An Act relating to schools; amending 70 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 16-106, which relates to contracts for school
7 materials; requiring stipulation that electronic
textbook material will be ready for use; requiring
8 contracts between vendors and schools for certain
electronic materials to include certain provisions;
9 allowing contract termination and disqualification
for violations; clarifying violations are subject to
10 certain penalties and damages; defining terms;
providing for codification; providing an effective
11 date; and declaring an emergency.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 16-106, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 16-106. A. All contracts with publishers shall be
18 signed by the chairperson and secretary of the State Textbook
19 Committee on behalf of the state. Each contract shall stipulate the
20 price at which each textbook will be sold to the State of Oklahoma,
21 and that all copies supplied to the State of Oklahoma will be equal
22 to or better than the official copy filed with the secretary of the
23 Committee. The State Textbook Committee may approve the

24 are provided to the Committee shall be in a format prescribed by

1 fede ral law or regulations promulgated by the United States
2 Department of Education which requires national standards for
3 electronic files to be used for production of accessible
4 instructional materials. In the absence of a federal law or
5 regulation establishing a national standard, a file format standard
6 shall be prescribed by the State Department of Rehabilitation
7 Services.
8 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 16-201 of Title 70, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. Any contract, licensing agreement, or other agreement
12 between a vendor and a school or school district for electronic
13 educational materials, including, but not limited to, digital
14 textbooks, supplemental textbook materials, software, online access,
15 and other means of digital distribution, shall include a stipulation
16 or addendum ensuring:
17 1. The materials purchased are ready for use or will be ready
18 for use by the date of delivery;
19 2. A replacement for any defective or nonworking materials will
20 be sent at no cost to the school, in a timely manner, but no later
21 than seven (7) calendar days from notification by the school of such
22 defect or nonworking material; and
23 3. Any updates, maintenance, or support services needed for the
24 proper function or applicability of the materials will be supplied
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1982 Page 4
1 in a timely manner, but no later than seven (7) calendar days from
2 notification by the school of an issue.
3 B. A vendor who violates any provision of this section may, as
4 determined by the school district, be subject to immediate contract
5 termination and disqualification from future contracts and shall be
6 subject to damages and penalties as provided by law.
7 C. For purposes of this section:
8 1. "Electronic educational materials" means materials that are
9 selected and placed on the list of textbooks by the State Textbook
10 Committee, optional supplements created for the approved list of
11 textbooks, materials pertaining to subjects that are not reviewed by
12 the State Textbook Committee, or materials that support instruction
13 in the subject matter standards as adopted by the State Board of
14 Education in Section 11-103.6 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes
15 that were not reviewed by the State Textbook Committee; and
16 2. "Vendor" means any person, firm, company, corporation, or
17 organization making sales of tangible personal property,
18 intellectual property, or services in this state.
19 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
20 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
21 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
23 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

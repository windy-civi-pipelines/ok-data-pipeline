9 FLOOR SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to special education; defining term;
requiring certain qualifications for special
11 education administrators; requiring State Board of
Education to issue certain acknowledgement of
12 competency; requiring training cover certain topics;
requiring State Board of Education to offer certain
13 options for completing competency requirements;
requiring competency exam; exempting certain special
14 education administrators from requirement; providing
for codification; providing an effective date; and
15 declaring an emergency.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-189.3 of Title 70, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. For this section, "special education administrator" means
23 the director and assistant director of a special education program
24 for a school district.
Req. No. 13142 Page 1
1 B. Beginning August 1, 2026, no school district shall employ a
2 special education administrator unless the individual:
3 1. Holds a valid standard teaching certificate issued by the
4 State Board of Education; and
5 2. Except as provided for in subsection D of this section,
6 obtains an acknowledgment of competency as provided for in
7 subsection C within one (1) year of being employed as a special
8 education administrator.
9 C. 1. The State Board of Education shall issue an
10 acknowledgement of competency in the area of special education
11 federal and state laws, regulations, policies, and procedures to a
12 special education administrator. By January 1, 2026, the State
13 Board shall develop two (2) tracks for completing a one-time
14 acknowledgement of competency:
15 a. a micro-credential track for individuals with five (5)
16 or less years of experience previously or currently
17 serving in the role of a special education
18 administrator, and
19 b. a standard track for individuals who are not required
20 to obtain the micro-credential and have six (6) or
21 more years of experience currently serving in the role
22 of a special education administrator.
23 2. The content of the 150-clock hour special education program
24 shall be the same for the standard track and the micro-credential
Req. No. 13142 Page 2
1 trac k, except the micro-credential track shall require mastery of
2 competency at or above seventy percent (70%) on coursework and
3 examinations.
4 3. Beginning August 1, 2026, an individual must complete either
5 the standard or micro-credential track within one (1) calendar year
6 from the date of hire.
7 4. At the successful completion of either the standard or
8 micro-credential track, the individual shall receive a certificate
9 of completion which will be maintained as part of the employee's
10 record with the local education agency and stored in the Oklahoma
11 Educator Credentialing System.
12 D. 1. Except as provided in paragraph 2 of this subsection, a
13 special education administrator shall successfully complete the
14 subject area competency examination in either mild-moderate
15 disabilities, severe-profound disabilities, or comprehensive special
16 education as prescribed by the State Department of Education.
17 2. An individual who has a valid Special Education Teaching
18 Certificate as provided for in Sections 6-122.7, 6-122.8, or 6-122.9
19 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes or an individual who has a
20 master's degree in school administration, special education, or a
21 related field shall be exempt from the subject area competency
22 examination requirement in paragraph 1 of this subsection.
23 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
24
Req. No. 13142 Page 3
1 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
2 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
3 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
4 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

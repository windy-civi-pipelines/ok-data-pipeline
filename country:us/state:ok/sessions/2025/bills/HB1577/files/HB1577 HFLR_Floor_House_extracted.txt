9 AS INTRODUCED
10 An Act relating to state Medicaid program; requiring
coverage of medically necessary donor human milk-
11 derived products under certain conditions; requiring
certain reimbursement; directing promulgation of
12 rules; authorizing adoption of certain policy or
procedure; directing the Oklahoma Health Care
13 Authority to seek certain federal approval; providing
for codification; and providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5026.2 of Title 63, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority shall, to the extent
21 permissible under federal law and subject to the federal approval
22 described in subsection D of this section, provide Medicaid coverage
23 for medically necessary donor human milk-derived products in an
24
HB1577 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 inpa tient or outpatient setting for an infant whose gestationally
2 corrected age is under twelve (12) months; provided:
3 1. A physician, physician assistant, or Advanced Practice
4 Registered Nurse licensed in this state signs an order stating that
5 donor human milk-derived products are medically necessary for the
6 infant Medicaid beneficiary due to any of the following:
7 a. the infant has a birth weight of less than one
8 thousand five hundred (1,500) grams or an infant body
9 weight below healthy levels, as determined by the
10 ordering provider,
11 b. the infant's gestational age at birth was thirty-four
12 (34) weeks or less, or
13 c. the infant has any congenital or acquired condition
14 for which the ordering provider determines that the
15 use of donor human milk-derived products is medically
16 necessary and will support the treatment and recovery
17 of the infant; and
18 2. The donor human milk-derived products adhere to such quality
19 standards as may be required by the Oklahoma Health Care Authority
20 Board.
21 B. Reimbursement under subsection A of this section for donor
22 human milk-derived products for an infant in an inpatient setting
23 shall be provided separately from the existing hospital payment for
24 inpatient services.
HB1577 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 C. The Board shall promulgate rules to implement the provisions
2 of this section, and may adopt a policy or procedure to implement
3 the provisions of this section while in the process of promulgating
4 rules; provided, such policy or procedure shall be posted on the
5 Internet website of the Authority prior to adoption of the policy.
6 D. The Authority shall seek any federal approval necessary to
7 implement this section.
8 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

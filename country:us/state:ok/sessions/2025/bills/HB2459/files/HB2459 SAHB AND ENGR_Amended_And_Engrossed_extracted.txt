7 An Act relating to fire suppression; amending Section
1, Chapter 106, O.S.L. 2024 (74 O.S. Supp. 2024,
8 Section 325.20), which relates to mobile food
establishments, fire extinguishers, and inspections;
9 directing compliance with current adopted codes;
providing exception for certain extinguishing
10 systems; deleting certain annual inspection
requirement; providing for inspections; providing for
11 the expiration of certain provisions; requiring
compliance with certain codes; providing sole
12 authority to the Fire Marshal; providing for the
issuance of operational permit decals; declaring
13 authority of the State Fire Marshal to enforce
minimum code requirements and revoke operations of
14 mobile food preparation vehicles; declaring authority
of political subdivisions to enforce permits, fees,
15 regulations, and ordinances; prohibiting the
enactment or enforcement of any fire or life safety
16 code-regulated ordinances or rules; and providing an
effective date.

6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
7 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 106, O.S.L.
8 2024 (74 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 325.20), is amended to read as
9 follows:
10 Section 325.20. A. In lieu of an automatic fire extinguishing
11 system, portable fire extinguishers shall be installed in mobile
12 food establishments that produce All mobile food preparation
13 vehicles operating within this state prior to July 1, 2024, that are
14 capable of producing smoke or grease-laden vapors shall be in
15 compliance with the most current adopted fire code. With the
16 exception of an automatic fire extinguishing system, such
17 requirement shall apply to vehicles including, but not limited to,
18 vehicles utilizing electric energy, liquefied petroleum gas,
19 compressed natural gas, or a combination thereof for cooking,
20 provided that portable fire extinguishers are installed and
21 maintained in accordance with the most current version of NFPA 10:
22 Standard for Portable Fire Extinguishers. All fire extinguishers
23 shall be inspected annually by a licensed contractor. A six-liter
24 Class K-rated, portable fire extinguisher is required for all
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2459 Page 2
1 cook ing equipment involving solid fuels or, vegetable or animal oils
2 and fats, or a combination thereof. All portable fire extinguishers
3 shall be in clear view and immediately available for use.
4 B. The provisions of this section shall cease to have the force
5 and effect of law on November 1, 2025 All mobile food preparation
6 vehicles utilizing, handling, or transporting liquefied petroleum
7 gas or any liquefied petroleum gas system, equipment, container,
8 apparatus, or appliance shall receive and maintain a permit from,
9 and be inspected annually by, the Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas
10 Administration or its employees, in conjunction with the State Fire
11 Marshal issuing an annual operation permit decal to mobile food
12 preparation vehicles.
13 C. The provisions in subsection A of this section shall expire
14 on January 1, 2026, and all mobile food preparation vehicles shall
15 comply with the most current adopted fire codes set forth by this
16 state.
17 D. Mobile food preparation vehicles shall be under the
18 jurisdiction of the State Fire Marshal for fire and life safety code
19 compliance and shall be inspected annually by the State Fire Marshal
20 to ensure compliance with the latest version of the state-adopted
21 minimum codes for fire and life safety as adopted by the Oklahoma
22 Uniform Building Code Commission. The State Fire Marshal shall
23 coordinate, at minimum, annual inspections conducted:
24 1. Within each of the four quadrants of this state; and
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2459 Page 3
1 2. Twice within each county with a population exceeding four
2 hundred thousand (400,000) according to the latest Federal Decennial
3 Census.
4 E. Upon approval, the State Fire Marshal shall issue an
5 operational permit decal indicating that the vehicle meets the
6 minimum fire and life code requirements adopted at the time of
7 inspection.
8 F. Nothing in this section shall prevent:
9 1. The State Fire Marshal from enforcing the minimum fire code
10 requirements as allowed by law and revoking the operations of a
11 mobile food preparation vehicle for cause when such vehicle is
12 determined to be inherently dangerous to the health, safety, or
13 welfare of persons within this state;
14 2. A political subdivision from enforcing the event permits,
15 operational fees, land use regulations, zoning, or other ordinances
16 in relation to the operation of a mobile food preparation vehicle
17 within the boundaries of the political subdivision; or
18 3. The State Department of Health from licensing and regulating
19 mobile food preparation vehicles as provided by the provisions of
20 Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes and rules promulgated by the State
21 Commissioner of Health.
22 G. The State Fire Marshal may promulgate rules necessary to
23 implement the provisions of this section. Such rules shall have
24 uniform force and effect throughout this state and no political
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2459 Page 4
1 subd ivision or other agency shall enact or enforce any fire or life
2 safety code-related ordinances, rules, or other requirements for
3 mobile food preparation vehicles, except in a county with a
4 population exceeding four hundred thousand (400,000) according to
5 the latest Federal Decennial Census.
6 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.”
7 Passed the Senate the 6th day of May, 2025.

11 An Act relating to schools; creating Rain's Law;
requiring certain instruction on fentanyl abuse
12 prevention and drug poisoning awareness; prescribing
frequency of and grades for instruction; listing
13 information to be included in fentanyl abuse
instruction; authorizing organizations and persons to
14 provide instruction; directing the State Department
of Education to adopt certain curriculum standards;
15 requiring the Department to provide certain resources
to schools; requiring fentanyl awareness curriculum
16 in certain units of instruction; directing Governor
to designate Fentanyl Poisoning Awareness Week in
17 schools; requiring correlation with National Red
Ribbon Week; providing for certain instruction during
18 designated week; providing for noncodification;
providing for codification; providing an effective
19 date; and declaring an emergency.
20
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
23 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
24 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
HB1484 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 This act shall be known and may be cited as "Rain's Law".
2 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
3 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.229-8 of Title 70, unless
4 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
5 A. As part of the comprehensive, integrated curriculum for
6 substance abuse programs provided in Section 1210.229-5 of Title 70
7 of the Oklahoma Statutes, each school district shall annually
8 provide research-based instruction related to fentanyl abuse
9 prevention and drug poisoning awareness to students in grades six
10 through twelve.
11 B. The instruction required by this section shall include:
12 1. Suicide prevention;
13 2. Prevention of the abuse of and addiction to fentanyl;
14 3. Awareness of local school and community resources and how to
15 access those resources; and
16 4. Health education that includes information about substance
17 use and abuse of fentanyl, including youth substance use and abuse
18 of fentanyl.
19 C. The instruction required by this section may be provided by
20 an organization or an employee or agent of an organization that is:
21 1. A public or private institution of higher education;
22 2. A library;
23 3. A community service organization;
24 4. A religious organization;
HB1484 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. A local public health agency; or
2 6. An organization employing mental health professionals.
3 D. The State Department of Education shall identify and adopt
4 curriculum standards for fentanyl awareness instruction that reflect
5 the areas of instruction listed in subsection B of this section.
6 The standards shall be incorporated into the appropriate health
7 standards for grades six through twelve.
8 E. The State Department of Education shall provide resources to
9 assist schools and teachers in providing instruction on fentanyl
10 awareness.
11 F. Any school district that offers a health education class in
12 grades six through twelve shall include fentanyl awareness
13 curriculum in the drug awareness unit.
14 G. To educate students about the dangers posed by fentanyl and
15 the risks of fentanyl poisoning, including overdose, the Governor
16 shall designate a week to be known as Fentanyl Poisoning Awareness
17 Week in public schools. Fentanyl Poisoning Awareness Week shall be
18 in correlation with National Red Ribbon Week. Fentanyl Poisoning
19 Awareness Week may include age-appropriate instruction, including
20 instruction on the prevention of the abuse of and addiction to
21 fentanyl, as determined by each school district.
22 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
23 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
24 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
HB1484 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 decl ared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
2 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

03/04/2025 - DO PASS, As Amended.

Section 2:
Section 2, Chapter 224, O.S.L. 2024 (21 O.S. Supp.
10 2024, Section 1795), which relates to unlawful
immigration; making certain acts unlawful; providing
11 penalties; providing for the posting of a cash bond;
remanding persons to the custody of the Department of
12 Corrections; directing the Department to notify
certain federal agency to arrange transportation; and
13 declaring an emergency.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 2, Chapter 224, O.S.L.
18 2024 (21 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 1795), is amended to read as
19 follows:
20 Section 1795. A. As used in this section, the term "alien"
21 means any person not a citizen or national of the United States.
22 B. A person commits an impermissible occupation if the person
23 is an alien and willfully and without permission enters and remains
24
Req. No. 12783 Page 1
1 in t he State of Oklahoma without having first obtained legal
2 authorization to enter the United States.
3 C. 1. Any person found to have committed an impermissible
4 occupation pursuant to the provisions of subsection B of this
5 section and who enters this state without having obtained the legal
6 authorization to enter the United States shall, upon conviction, be
7 guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment in the county
8 jail for a term of not more than one (1) year, or by a fine of not
9 more than Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00), or by both such fine and
10 imprisonment. In addition, the person shall be required to leave
11 the state within seventy-two (72) hours following his or her
12 conviction or release from custody, whichever comes later.
13 2. Any second or subsequent offense for an impermissible
14 occupation pursuant to the provisions of subsection B of this
15 section, or any such offense committed during the commission of any
16 other crime shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a felony punishable
17 by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of Corrections for
18 a term of not more than two (2) years, or by a fine of not more than
19 One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00), or by both such fine and
20 imprisonment. In addition, the person shall be required to leave
21 the state within seventy-two (72) hours following his or her
22 conviction or release from custody, whichever comes later Any person
23 who is apprehended and arrested for violating a criminal law of this
24 state, and after determining the citizenship status of the person is
Req. No. 12783 Page 2
1 foun d to be unlawfully present in the United States, shall be guilty
2 of a felony punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the
3 Department of Corrections for a term of not less than five (5)
4 years.
5 C. A person arrested for a violation of this section may be
6 allowed to post a cash bond in an amount of not less than Thirty-
7 five Thousand Dollars ($35,000.00).
8 D. In lieu of the sentence of imprisonment, the court shall
9 have the authority to dismiss the criminal prosecution and issue a
10 written order discharging the person and requiring said individual
11 to return to his or her country of origin if:
12 1. The person agrees to the written order;
13 2. The person has not previously been convicted of a violent
14 crime, as provided for in Section 571 of Title 57 of the Oklahoma
15 Statutes, in this state or another state; and
16 3. The person is not charged with another felony offense.
17 Upon issuance of the written order, the court shall direct the
18 law enforcement agency having custody of the person to remand the
19 person to the custody of the Department of Corrections with
20 instructions to return the person to his or her country of origin.
21 E. Once the Department of Corrections receives custody of the
22 person, the Department of Corrections shall notify the United States
23 Customs and Border Protection of the Department of Homeland Security
24
Req. No. 12783 Page 3
1 with in seven (7) days to arrange transportation for the repatriation
2 of said person back to his or her country of origin.
3 D. F. Any alien who has been denied admission, excluded,
4 deported, or removed, or has departed the United States while an
5 order of exclusion, deportation, or removal is outstanding, and
6 thereafter enters, attempts to enter, or is at any time found in
7 Oklahoma shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a felony and shall be
8 punished in accordance with the provisions set forth in paragraph 2
9 of subsection C B of this section, unless:
10 1. Prior to reembarkation of the alien at a place outside the
11 United States or application by the alien for admission from a
12 foreign contiguous territory, the United States Attorney General has
13 expressly consented to such alien's reapplying for admission; or
14 2. With respect to an alien previously denied admission and
15 removed, such alien established that he or she was not required to
16 obtain such advance consent under this section or any prior statute.
17 E. G. The arresting law enforcement agency shall collect all
18 available identifying information of the person including all
19 fingerprints and any other applicable photographic and biometric
20 data to identify the person. Once obtained, the law enforcement
21 agency shall cross-reference the collected information with:
22 1. All relevant local, state, and federal criminal databases;
23 and
24
Req. No. 12783 Page 4
1 2. Federal lists or classifications used to identify a person
2 as a threat or potential threat to national security.
3 The Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation shall have the
4 authority to collect and maintain the identifying information
5 collected by law enforcement agencies pursuant to the provisions of
6 this subsection.
7 F. H. It shall be an affirmative defense to prosecution under
8 the provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of subsection C B of this
9 section that:
10 1. The federal government has granted the defendant:
11 a. lawful presence in the United States, or
12 b. asylum under Section 1158 of Title 8 of the United
13 States Code; or
14 2. The defendant was approved for benefits under the federal
15 Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals program between June 15,
16 2012, and July 16, 2021.
17 G. I. Any person convicted of an offense pursuant to the
18 provisions of this section shall not be eligible for probation or
19 delayed sentencing.
20 H. J. The Legislature finds that the presence of persons who
21 are unauthorized to be present within the State of Oklahoma is a
22 matter of statewide concern. Therefore, the Legislature hereby
23 occupies and preempts the entire field of legislation in this state
24 regarding the presence of persons who have entered and remained in
Req. No. 12783 Page 5
1 this state without first having obtained the legal authorization to
2 do so. Any municipality or other political subdivision of this
3 state shall be prohibited from adopting any ordinance, regulation,
4 resolution, rule, or policy that conflicts with the provisions of
5 this act.
6 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
8 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

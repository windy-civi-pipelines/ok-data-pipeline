16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 3-151.1, is
18 amended to read as follows:
19 Section 3-151.1. A. For purposes of establishing a uniform and
20 accurate definition of high school graduation rate for school
21 districts and secondary schools in the state, the State Board of
22 Education shall adopt and implement a four-year adjusted cohort
23 graduation rate and an extended-year adjusted cohort graduation
24 rate. The Board shall utilize and report the four-year adjusted
Req. No. 13483 Page 1
1 coho rt graduation rate and the extended-year adjusted cohort
2 graduation rate at the secondary school site, the school district
3 and the state level, and the graduation rates shall be reported in
4 the aggregate as well as disaggregated by subgroups as required in
5 the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 2001 (ESEA), P.L. No.
6 107-110, also known as the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001.
7 B. The Board shall use the four-year adjusted cohort graduation
8 rate for purposes of determining the high school graduation rate
9 indicator for the academic performance data calculation beginning
10 with state and district report cards providing results of
11 assessments administered in the 2011-2012 school year. The Board
12 shall use the four-year adjusted cohort graduation rate for
13 establishing the high school graduation rate for measuring alternate
14 year percentage growth targets as set forth in Section 3-151.2 of
15 this title beginning with the 2012-2013 school year.
16 C. The four-year adjusted cohort graduation rate shall be
17 defined as the number of students who graduate in four (4) years
18 with a standard high school diploma divided by the number of
19 students who entered high school four (4) years earlier, adjusted
20 for transfers. Students who transfer in and out of the school,
21 émigrés emigrate to another country, and deceased students shall not
22 be included in the adjusted cohort graduation rate. Students who
23 graduate in four (4) years shall include students who earn a
24 standard high school diploma at the end of their fourth year, before
Req. No. 13483 Page 2
1 the end of their fourth year, and during a summer session
2 immediately following their fourth year. To remove a student from a
3 cohort, a school district shall confirm in writing or by electronic
4 means that a student has transferred out of the school site or
5 school district, has emigrated to another country, or is deceased.
6 For a student who transfers out of a school site or school district,
7 the written or electronic confirmation shall be official and
8 document that the student has enrolled in another school site or
9 school district or in an educational program that culminates in a
10 high school diploma recognized by a state or private higher
11 educational institution accredited or recognized by the Oklahoma
12 State Regents for Higher Education for purposes of admission to the
13 institution. Students who are not enrolled in a program which
14 culminates in the award of a credential recognized by a state or
15 private higher educational institution for purposes of admissions
16 shall not be included in the graduation rate calculation as set
17 forth in this subsection as a student who graduated in four (4)
18 years with a standard high school diploma but shall be included in
19 the number of students who entered high school four (4) years
20 earlier.
21 D. The extended-year adjusted cohort graduation rate shall be
22 defined as the number of students who graduate in four (4) years or
23 five (5) years with a high school diploma divided by the number of
24 students who form the adjusted cohort for the four-year graduation
Req. No. 13483 Page 3
1 rate as calculated as provided for in subsection C of this section,
2 provided that the adjustments add to the cohort all students who
3 transfer into the cohort by the end of the year of graduation being
4 considered and subtract students who transfer out, emigrate to
5 another country or are deceased by the end of that year. The
6 extended-year adjusted cohort graduation rate shall be reported
7 separately from the four-year adjusted cohort graduation rate.
8 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 1210.545, is
9 amended to read as follows:
10 Section 1210.545. A. Except as otherwise provided, as part of
11 the accountability system developed as provided for in Section
12 1210.541 of this title, the State Board of Education shall prepare
13 annual reports of the results of the Oklahoma School Testing Program
14 which describe student achievement in the state, and each school
15 site, pursuant to the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965
16 (ESEA) as reauthorized by P.L. No. 114-95, also known as the Every
17 Student Succeeds Act (ESSA), and any related federal regulations.
18 B. The annual report as required pursuant to subsection A of
19 this section shall identify school sites as having one of the
20 following grades for each indicator, separately, and by a single
21 overview grade of all indicators, defined according to rules of the
22 State Board of Education:
23 1. "A" means schools making excellent progress;
24 2. "B" means schools making above average progress;
Req. No. 13483 Page 4
1 3. "C" means schools making satisfactory progress;
2 4. "D" means schools making less than satisfactory progress;
3 and
4 5. "F" means schools failing to make adequate progress.
5 C. Each school that has students who are tested and included in
6 the school grading system as provided for in this section shall
7 receive a school grade, except as follows:
8 1. A school shall not receive a school grade if the number of
9 students tested and included in the school grading system is less
10 than the minimum sample size necessary for statistical reliability
11 and prevention of the unlawful release of personally identifiable
12 student data. The State Board of Education is directed to establish
13 the lowest minimum sample size necessary to meet the requirements of
14 this paragraph; and
15 2. The academic performance of students who are enrolled full-
16 time in an online program that is offered by a school district or
17 charter school that is not the district of residence or is not
18 located in the district of residence of the student shall be
19 reported separately by the school district or charter school and
20 shall not be included when determining the grade of the school site
21 or charter school.
22 D. The State Board of Education may adopt alternate systems of
23 accountability for statewide virtual charter schools, alternative
24 education programs as defined in Section 1210.568 of this title, and
Req. No. 13483 Page 5
1 scho ols serving only grades prekindergarten or kindergarten through
2 two.
3 E. The grade of a school shall be based on a multimeasures
4 approach to accountability in accordance with the Elementary and
5 Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA) as reauthorized by P.L. No.
6 114-95, also known as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) and any
7 federal regulations that include, but are not limited to, the
8 following indicators:
9 1. Statewide assessments, including the establishment of
10 student performance bands;
11 2. Graduation rates for high schools;
12 3. Statewide academic measures for elementary and middle
13 schools;
14 4. English language proficiency for English learners; and
15 5. At least one additional statewide measure of school quality
16 or student success, including, but not limited to, school climate,
17 school safety, student engagement, educator engagement, and/or
18 advanced coursework and postsecondary readiness. Chronic
19 absenteeism shall not be included as any statewide measure of school
20 quality or student success.
21 F. Of the indicators included in subsection E of this section,
22 not less than seventy percent (70%) of the overview grade must be
23 given to indicators 1, 2, 3 and 4 and, in the aggregate, with not
24 more than thirty percent (30%) of the grade given to indicator 5.
Req. No. 13483 Page 6
1 G. Further, the grade of an elementary or middle school site
2 shall include, but not be limited to, a measure of status for
3 English language arts (ELA), math and science, growth in ELA and
4 math, English Language Proficiency Assessment (ELPA) progress, and
5 chronic absenteeism instructional time as described in subsection M
6 of this section.
7 H. The grade of a high school site shall include, but not be
8 limited to, a measure of status for English language arts (ELA),
9 math and science status, English Language Proficiency Assessment
10 (ELPA) progress, graduation rate, chronic absenteeism, and
11 postsecondary opportunities, and instructional time as described in
12 subsection M of this section. For purposes of this section,
13 postsecondary opportunities shall include Advanced Placement,
14 International Baccalaureate, dual and concurrent enrollment, scoring
15 Silver Level or above on American College Testing (ACT) WorkKeys,
16 internships, mentorships and apprenticeships, military readiness as
17 evidenced by being accepted for enlistment into any branch of the
18 military within the first year after high school graduation or
19 earning a military readiness score on the Armed Services Vocational
20 Aptitude Battery (ASVAB), and industry certifications. The Board
21 shall adopt a time line for moving from completion of such
22 opportunities to crediting achievement of such opportunities.
23 I. The annual report shall identify the performance of each
24 school as having improved, remained the same, or declined. This
Req. No. 13483 Page 7
1 scho ol improvement rating shall be based on a comparison of the
2 student and school performance data of the current year to the
3 previous year data.
4 J. The State Department of Education shall annually develop a
5 school site report card to be delivered to parents throughout each
6 school district. The report card shall be in accordance with the
7 requirements of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965
8 (ESEA), as reauthorized and amended by P.L. No. 114-95, also known
9 as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) and includes the grade for
10 the school, information regarding school improvement, an explanation
11 of school performance, and indicators of return on investment. The
12 report card for each school site shall be published annually by the
13 Department on its website, and every school district shall provide
14 the school site report card to the parent or guardian of each
15 student enrolled in the school site. In order to provide
16 information regarding school performance for school report cards
17 issued during the 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 school year, the
18 Department shall include an explanation of the changes to the
19 statewide system of student assessments as required in Section
20 1210.508 of this title and how the transition in assessments may
21 impact school performance. The Department shall issue school report
22 cards using the 2016-2017 school year assessment data that is
23 available.
24
Req. No. 13483 Page 8
1 K. The Legislature may factor in the performance of schools in
2 calculating any performance-based funding policy that is provided to
3 public school districts.
4 L. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
5 implement the provisions of this section.
6 M. The State Board of Education shall amend the State ESSA Plan
7 in accordance with the requirements of the Every Student Succeeds
8 Act (ESSA) to replace the measure of chronic absenteeism with a
9 measure of instructional time as described in this subsection. The
10 Board shall submit the proposed amendment to the United States
11 Department of Education no later than August 1, 2025.
12 If the amended state plan is approved by the United States
13 Department of Education, beginning with the 2025-2026 school year,
14 the grade for an elementary, middle, and high school site shall
15 include a measure of instructional time in lieu of chronic
16 absenteeism. This measure shall account for a total of ten (10)
17 possible points out of ninety (90) points on a school’s report card.
18 The measure of instructional time shall comprise the following
19 weighted subcomponents:
20 1. Total in-person school days. A school’s total instructional
21 days shall be benchmarked against a one-hundred-eighty-day school
22 year with a weight of 0.50. A full school day shall be at least
23 five and one-half (5.5) hours. School days and hours shall be
24
Req. No. 13483 Page 9
1 calc ulated pursuant to Sections 1-109 and 1-109.1 of this title.
2 The total number of school days may not include virtual days;
3 2. Total school hours for the academic year. A school’s total
4 school hours shall be benchmarked against a one-thousand-two-
5 hundred-hour school year with a weight of 0.40. Total school hours
6 shall include all hours students are present, including lunch and
7 passing periods; and
8 3. Day length. A school’s day length shall be benchmarked
9 against a seven-hour school day for middle and high schools and a
10 six-and-one-half-hour school day for elementary schools. This
11 subcomponent shall have a weight of 0.10.
12 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
13 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
14 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
15 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
16 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

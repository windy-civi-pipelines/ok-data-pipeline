6 An Act relating to harmful woody species; directing
the Oklahoma Conservation Commission to implement
7 certain plan; requiring certain report to be compiled
and presented to Commission by certain date;
8 authorizing the Commission to implement certain plans
to assist state agencies; directing the agencies to
9 submit reports each year on progress; defining term;
providing for promulgation of rules; providing for
10 codification; providing an effective date; and
declaring an emergency.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-9-100 of Title 27A, unless
16 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The Oklahoma Conservation Commission shall implement a
18 statewide plan for the control, removal, and eradication of harmful
19 woody species on state-owned, -leased, or -operated land.
20 B. On or before January 1, 2026, a report shall be compiled and
21 presented to the Commission from the Oklahoma Department of
22 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry, the Commissioners of the Land
23 Office, the Department of Wildlife Conservation, the Department of
24 Corrections, the Oklahoma Tourism and Recreation Department, the
ENGR. S. B. NO. 940 Page 1
1 Depa rtment of Transportation, and the Oklahoma Turnpike Authority
2 with a comprehensive report to determine the affected inventory and
3 subsequent plan to control, remove, or eradicate harmful woody
4 species from state-owned, -leased, or -operated properties. The
5 Commission shall assist in the administration of the agency’s plan
6 and develop any necessary programs to expand the capabilities of
7 each agency’s approach to control, remove, or eradicate harmful
8 woody specifies.
9 C. The agencies specified in subsection B of this section shall
10 electronically submit to the Legislature an annual report by
11 November 1, 2026, and each subsequent year by November 1, detailing
12 the progress of the control, removal, and eradication of harmful
13 woody species on state-owned, -leased, or -operated land.
14 D. As used in this section, “harmful woody species” means a
15 tree or shrub species that is rapidly encroaching into adjoining
16 lands and is likely to cause harm to the environment or economy.
17 Harmful woody species include, but are not limited to, Juniperus
18 virginiana (Eastern Redcedar), Juniperus scopulorum (Rocky Mountain
19 juniper), Juniperus monosperma (oneseed juniper), and Tamarix
20 ramosissima (salt cedar).
21 E. The Oklahoma Conservation Commission shall promulgate rules
22 necessary to implement the provisions of this section.
23 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
24

1 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
2 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
3 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
4 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
5 Passed the Senate the 10th day of March, 2025.

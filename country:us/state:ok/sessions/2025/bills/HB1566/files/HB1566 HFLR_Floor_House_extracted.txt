19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
20 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
21 This act shall be known and may be cited as "Neil's Law".

3 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 14-102 of Title 43A, unless
5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. The purpose of the Oklahoma Elder Exploitation and Abuse Act
7 is to promote the general welfare of all citizens by establishing a
8 civil action for incapacitated persons and vulnerable adults which
9 provides for the protection of their rights and affords them a right
10 of civil action against those who neglect, exploit, or abuse them.
11 B. The purpose of the Oklahoma Elder Exploitation and Abuse Act
12 is to allow incapacitated persons and vulnerable adults, through
13 their legal representatives, to pursue claims against persons who
14 neglect, exploit, or abuse the incapacitated person or vulnerable
15 adult if he or she lacks the capacity to pursue such claims on his
16 or her own behalf.
17 C. The Oklahoma Elder Exploitation and Abuse Act does not
18 preclude an individual's mandatory responsibility to report to Adult
19 Protective Services or law enforcement if there is reasonable cause
20 to believe a vulnerable adult is being abused, neglected, or
21 exploited.
22 D. It is the intent of the Oklahoma Legislature:
23 1. That the district court shall exercise the authority
24 conferred by the Oklahoma Elder Exploitation and Abuse Act to
HB1566 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 enco urage the development of maximum self-reliance and independence
2 of incapacitated persons and vulnerable adults and make appointive
3 and other orders only to the extent necessitated by the mental and
4 adaptive limitations of other conditions of the incapacitated or
5 partially incapacitated person warranting the action; and
6 2. That in performing their duties and exercising their powers,
7 legal representatives bringing claims under this act for
8 incapacitated persons and vulnerable adults shall:
9 a. assure, to the extent reasonably possible, that the
10 rights of the persons for whom they are appointed are
11 protected, and
12 b. encourage, to the extent reasonably possible,
13 incapacitated persons and vulnerable adults to
14 participate to the maximum extent of their abilities
15 in all decisions which affect them concerning the
16 claims afforded them under this act.

20 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 14-104 of Title 43A, unless
22 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. A claim for elder neglect, exploitation, or abuse, as
24 prescribed in this section, may be brought in any district court
HB1566 HFLR Page 9
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 with in the county in the State of Oklahoma in which the vulnerable
2 adult lives or maintains his or her residence or was living at the
3 time of the act or omission giving rise to the claim.
4 B. A person who commits neglect or financial neglect against a
5 vulnerable adult, proven by a preponderance of the evidence, shall
6 be liable for actual damages in an amount that compensates the
7 vulnerable adult for the loss he or she has incurred as a result
8 thereof and shall be liable for punitive damages as Oklahoma law
9 otherwise allows.
10 C. A person who commits exploitation or abuse of a vulnerable
11 adult, proven by a preponderance of the evidence, shall be liable
12 for damages three times the actual damages incurred by the
13 vulnerable adult as a result thereof and shall be liable for
14 punitive damages as Oklahoma law otherwise allows.
15 D. The prevailing party in the claims prescribed in subsections
16 B and C of this section shall be entitled to recover reasonable
17 attorney fees and costs.
18 E. The claims prescribed in this section shall follow and
19 otherwise be subject to the general laws governing civil claims
20 under Oklahoma law, including, without limitation, the provisions
21 found in Titles 12 and 23 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
22 F. The claims provided for by this section may be brought by
23 the vulnerable adult or on behalf of the vulnerable adult by his or
24 her guardian, limited guardian, conservator, agent under an
HB1566 HFLR Page 10
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 appr opriate power of attorney, duly appointed representative of the
2 estate of the vulnerable adult, if deceased, or other legal
3 representative approved by the court.
4 G. In addition to any other documents or records to which the
5 vulnerable adult or his or her legal representative may be entitled
6 in pursuit of the claim or claims prescribed in this section, the
7 court may authorize or direct the vulnerable adult and his or her
8 legal representative access to and copies of financial, legal,
9 mental health, and physical health records of the vulnerable adult
10 in the possession of any physician, hospital, other health care
11 provider, firm, financial institution, lawyer, accountant,
12 counselor, broker, caregiver, corporation, other business entity, or
13 other facility or party. These records, upon order of the court,
14 shall be produced within ten (10) days, unless the court orders a
15 shorter time, and the records shall not be disclosed for any purpose
16 other than the purpose for which they have been obtained.
17 H. The claims provided for in this section survive the death of
18 the vulnerable adult.
19 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 14-105 of Title 43A, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 The district court in which a claim authorized in this act is
23 filed may, upon proper application, issue a restraining order or
24 other injunctive relief to prohibit any further violation of this
HB1566 HFLR Page 11
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 act, regardless of the existence of any other remedy at law and in
2 addition thereto.
3 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 14-106 of Title 43A, unless
5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 Subject to a specific court order, the physician-patient
7 privilege nor spousal privilege shall be grounds for excluding
8 evidence regarding the neglect, exploitation, or abuse of a
9 vulnerable adult or the cause thereof in any judicial proceeding
10 arising under this act.
11 SECTION 8. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

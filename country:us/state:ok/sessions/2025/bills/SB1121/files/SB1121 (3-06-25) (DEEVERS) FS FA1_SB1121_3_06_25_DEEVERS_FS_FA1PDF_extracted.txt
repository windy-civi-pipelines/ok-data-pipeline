14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
16 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
17 A. Upon the effective date of this act, there is hereby created
18 the Joint Interim Committee on the Elimination of Ad Valorem Taxes.
19 The Committee shall be composed of ten (10) members as follows:
20 1. Five members of the Senate designated by the President Pro
21 Tempore of the Senate; and
22 2. Five members of the House of Representatives designated by
23 the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
24
Req. No. 1795 Page 1
1 The President Pro Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the
2 House of Representatives shall each designate a co-chair from the
3 members of the Committee.
4 B. The meetings of the Committee shall be subject to the
5 Oklahoma Open Meeting Act. The Committee shall convene upon the
6 joint call of the co-chairs.
7 C. The Committee shall conduct a comprehensive study of
8 alternative methods of taxation to replace local tax revenue lost
9 from the elimination of ad valorem tax. The study shall consider
10 potential revenue sources, economic impacts, and legal frameworks
11 for implementing property tax elimination.
12 D. For each alternative method of taxation considered, the
13 Committee shall:
14 1. Consider whether political subdivisions that currently
15 impose ad valorem taxes would have the authority to impose the
16 proposed alternative tax;
17 2. Determine the average tax rate for the proposed alternative
18 tax imposed by each type of political subdivision that would be
19 necessary to generate the same amount of tax revenue as the amount
20 of tax revenue lost as a result of the elimination of ad valorem
21 taxes;
22 3. Determine, if appropriate, the effect that broadening the
23 application of the proposed alternative tax at the local level would
24
Req. No. 1795 Page 2
1 have on the tax rates identified pursuant to paragraph 2 of this
2 subsection;
3 4. Identify whether tax revenue generated by the alternative
4 method of taxation would require redistribution to offset
5 disparities in available local tax revenue as a result of the
6 elimination of ad valorem taxes and, if so, evaluate the different
7 mechanisms of redistribution available; and
8 5. Identify and examine any other issue required to be
9 addressed to implement the elimination of ad valorem taxes.
10 E. Upon a request of the Committee, a state agency or political
11 subdivision of this state shall provide information for and
12 assistance in conducting the study under this section.
13 F. The members of the Committee shall receive no compensation
14 but shall receive travel reimbursement for necessary travel expenses
15 incurred in the performance of their duties in accordance with the
16 State Travel Reimbursement Act. The Committee shall be staffed by
17 the Senate.
18 G. Not later than July 1, 2028, the Committee shall prepare and
19 electronically submit to the Legislature a written report containing
20 the results of the study and any recommendations for legislative or
21 other action. The report shall be published on the website of the
22 Senate.
23 H. The Committee created pursuant to this section shall be
24 abolished January 1, 2030.
Req. No. 1795 Page 3
1 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

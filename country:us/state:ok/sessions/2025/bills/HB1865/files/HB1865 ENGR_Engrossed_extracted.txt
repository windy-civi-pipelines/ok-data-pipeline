20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 4-120.4, is
22 amended to read as follows:
23 A. The Secretary of the State Election Board shall accept
24 written notice from the United States Attorney of persons convicted
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1865 Page 1
1 of f elonies in a district court of the United States. The Secretary
2 of the State Election Board shall cause the voter registrations of
3 persons listed in the written notice to be cancelled in the county
4 of the person’s residence and shall notify the secretary of the
5 appropriate county election board of the cancellation.
6 B. The Secretary of the State Election Board shall accept
7 written notice from the tribal court clerk of a federally recognized
8 tribe of persons convicted of felonies in a tribal court in the
9 United States. The Secretary of the State Election Board shall
10 cause the voter registrations of persons listed in the written
11 notice to be cancelled in the county of the person’s residence and
12 shall notify the secretary of the appropriate county election board
13 of the cancellation.
14 C. The court clerk in each county shall prepare a list monthly
15 of all persons convicted in the county of a felony and shall
16 transmit the list to the secretary of the county election board.
17 The list shall include information necessary to identify a person on
18 the list as a registered voter prescribed by the Secretary of the
19 State Election Board. The secretary shall cancel the registration
20 of registered voters in the county included on the list. The
21 secretary of the county election board shall forward the names of
22 any persons on the list who are not residents of the county to the
23 Secretary of the State Election Board. The Secretary of the State
24 Election Board shall cause the voter registrations of persons from a
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1865 Page 2
1 list who are forwarded to the Secretary to be cancelled in the
2 county of the person’s residence.
3 C. D. The Secretary of the State Election Board, secretaries of
4 county election boards, and their agents and employees shall not be
5 held civilly liable for any action taken based upon information
6 concerning felony convictions received from a United States Attorney
7 or a county court clerk pursuant to subsections A and B of this
8 section if a reasonable effort was made to make an accurate match of
9 the information provided with voter registration records before
10 canceling any voter’s registration.
11 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 291, O.S.L.
12 2022 (26 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 6-104.1), is amended to read as
13 follows:
14 Section 6-104.1. A. All ballots used for any election shall be
15 printed on paper to ensure a fair and accurate count; provided, each
16 precinct polling place and in-person absentee voting location shall
17 provide a means for any voter who requires assistance by reason of
18 blindness, disability, or inability to read or write to cast a
19 ballot privately and independently, in a manner to be determined by
20 the Secretary of the State Election Board.
21 B. 1. Ballots for each election day precinct shall be printed
22 in advance of the date of the election. Provided, the Secretary of
23 the State Election Board may authorize the printing of additional
24 ballots on election day in the event of a ballot shortage or due to
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1865 Page 3
1 an e mergency or unforeseen circumstance as described in Section 22-
2 101 of this title.
3 2. Nothing in this subsection shall prohibit the printing or
4 marking of ballots for persons with blindness, disability, or
5 inability to read or write as provided in subsection A of this
6 section.
7 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 7-104, is
8 amended to read as follows:
9 Section 7-104. A. At every Primary, Runoff Primary and General
10 Election and any other election conducted by a county election
11 board, each polling place in the state shall open at 7:00 a.m. and
12 shall remain open continuously until 7:00 p.m., and every registered
13 voter of a precinct who presents himself or herself between said
14 hours shall be entitled to vote, as provided by law, provided
15 further, all qualified voters who are in line waiting to vote at
16 7:00 p.m. shall be allowed to vote.
17 B. 1. A registered voter wishing to vote on election day shall
18 appear at the polling place for his or her assigned precinct.
19 2. Neither the State Election Board nor any county election
20 board shall utilize vote centers for voting on election day. For
21 the purposes of this section, "vote center" means a place where any
22 voter in the entire jurisdiction can vote on election day,
23 regardless of the voter's precinct assignment. Provided, an
24 exception to this requirement may be authorized for a county
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1865 Page 4
1 elec tion board by the Secretary of the State Election Board in the
2 event of an emergency as described in Section 22-101 of this title.
3 The emergency use of a countywide vote center must be limited to the
4 precincts directly impacted by the emergency.
5 3. Nothing in this subsection shall prohibit the use of
6 subprecincts as described in Section 3-119 of this title, nor shall
7 it prohibit the assignment of precinct officials to serve more than
8 one precinct, if authorized by law, if the precincts share the same
9 election day polling place.
10 C. If any provision of federal law specifies hours for voting
11 in federal elections, the Secretary of the State Election Board
12 shall direct the county election boards to allow voting in all
13 elections held on the same day as such federal elections during the
14 hours specified by federal law.
15 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
16 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
17 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
18 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
19 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

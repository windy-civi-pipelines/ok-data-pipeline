Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to public finance; creating the
Defending the Integrity of the Appropriations Process
8 and the Verbal Earmarks Transparency Act of 2025;
amending 62 O.S. 2021, Section 34.42, as amended by
9 Section 1, Chapter 113, O.S.L. 2024 (62 O.S. Supp.
2024, Section 34.42), which relates to budget work
10 programs; requiring executive officer to certify
agency compliance; requiring certain disclosure by
11 executive officer; providing requirements of
disclosure; defining term; providing for
12 noncodification; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
17 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
18 This section shall be known and may be cited as the "Defending
19 the Integrity of the Appropriations Process and the Verbal Earmarks
20 Transparency Act of 2025".
21 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 34.42, as
22 amended by Section 1, Chapter 113, O.S.L. 2024 (62 O.S. Supp. 2024,
23 Section 34.42), is amended to read as follows:
24
Req. No. 10540 Page 1
1 Section 34.42. A. On or before the first day of June in each
2 year, or as soon thereafter as possible, all agencies shall file
3 agency budgets with the Director of the Office of Management and
4 Enterprise Services. Copies of all agency budgets shall also be
5 made available electronically to the staff of the Joint Legislative
6 Committee on Budget and Program Oversight.
7 B. The required instructions, content and format of agency
8 budgets shall be developed by the staff of the Budget Division of
9 the Office of Management and Enterprise Services.
10 C. 1. The agency budget shall include a description of all
11 funds available to the agency for expenditure and set out allotments
12 requested by the agency by quarter and the entire fiscal year.
13 2. The agency budget shall be accompanied by an organizational
14 chart of the agency, a statement of agency mission and program
15 objectives.
16 3. The agency budget shall delineate agency spending by such
17 categories and with at least as much detail as is specified in the
18 legislative appropriation and as prescribed by the Director of the
19 Office of Management and Enterprise Services.
20 4. Agency budgets shall be signed by the executive officer of
21 each agency.
22 5. The executive officer shall certify that the agency is in
23 complete compliance with the requirements of Section 34.11.3 of this
24
Req. No. 10540 Page 2
1 titl e and Section 3-114 of Title 65 of the Oklahoma Statutes 3 of
2 this act.
3 D. A "request officer" shall be designated by each state agency
4 for the purpose of making program and allotment requests.
5 E. Executive officers of agencies shall cooperate with the
6 Office of Management and Enterprise Services staff and Joint
7 Committee staff in developing program budgeting categories.
8 F. All funds available or expected to be made available to any
9 agency, including nonfiscal appropriations, shall not be available
10 for expenditure until the request officer of the agency has complied
11 with the applicable provisions of the Oklahoma State Finance Act and
12 has received approval of such request for funds from the Director of
13 the Office of Management and Enterprise Services.
14 G. The provisions of this section shall not apply to CompSource
15 Oklahoma if CompSource Oklahoma is operating pursuant to a pilot
16 program authorized by Sections 3316 and 3317 of Title 74 of the
17 Oklahoma Statutes.
18 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 34.42.2 of Title 62, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. An agency executive officer shall disclose, within seventy-
22 two (72) hours, his or her awareness of any communication that
23 occurs between a member of the Legislature, or an employee of the
24 same, and the executive officer or an agency employee, during which
Req. No. 10540 Page 3
1 the legislator or employee suggests the manner in which state funds
2 should be expended. Such disclosure shall be made with the State
3 Ethics Commission and shall be posted to the Commission's website.
4 The disclosure shall include, at a minimum, but not be limited to,
5 the approximate time and date of the communication, the nature of
6 the suggested expenditure, and the identity of the legislator or
7 employee proffering the suggestion.
8 B. For purposes of this section, "executive officer" means the
9 director of any office, officer, bureau, board, counsel, court,
10 commission, institution, unit, division, body or house of the
11 executive or judicial branches of the state government, whether
12 elected or appointed, excluding political subdivisions of the state,
13 the State Legislature, and the Office of the Governor.
14 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

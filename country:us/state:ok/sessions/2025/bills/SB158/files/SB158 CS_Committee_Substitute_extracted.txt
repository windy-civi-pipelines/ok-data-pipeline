9 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to severe weather tracking; creating
the Oklahoma Emergency Weather Response and Tracking
11 Regulatory Act of 2025; providing short title;
defining terms; allowing certain entities to hire or
12 contract with certain persons for severe weather
tracking; establishing requirements for professional
13 severe weathers; establishing provisions for severe
weather tracking during certain significant weather
14 events; exempting professional severe weather
trackers from certain traffic regulations under
15 certain circumstances; providing exceptions to
certain exemptions; construing provision;
16 establishing penalty; amending 47 O.S. 2021, Section
1-103, which relates to authorized emergency
17 vehicles; designating certain vehicles as authorized
emergency vehicles in certain circumstances; amending
18 47 O.S. 2021, Section 12-218, which relates to
distinctive markings of emergency vehicles; providing
19 for distinctive markings for primary vehicles of
professional severe weather trackers; updating
20 statutory language; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
21 emergency.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
24
Req. No. 1647 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-300 of Title 47, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma
5 Emergency Weather Response and Tracking Regulatory Act of 2025”.
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-301 of Title 47, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 As used in this act:
10 1. “Professional severe weather tracker” means an individual
11 hired by or contracted with a qualified media outlet or affiliated
12 with a research program at a qualified institution of higher
13 education;
14 2. “Qualified media outlet” means a television or radio
15 broadcasting station licensed by the Federal Communications
16 Commission for the broadcast or transmission of programming via
17 broadcast, cable, streaming, or other platforms as recognized by the
18 Federal Communications Commission;
19 3. “Qualified institution of higher education” means any
20 college or university overseen by the Oklahoma State Regents for
21 Higher Education that offers a course or program in meteorology; and
22 4. “Significant weather event” means:
23 a. a day of enhanced, moderate, or high-risk conditions
24 in one or more counties in this state predicted by the
Req. No. 1647 Page 2
1 National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s
2 National Weather Service Storm Prediction Center,
3 b. any tornado or severe thunderstorm watch in one or
4 more counties in this state issued by the National
5 Weather Service or a qualified media outlet,
6 c. any storm in one or more counties in this state, or
7 anticipated to enter one or more counties in this
8 state, for which a tornado or severe thunderstorm
9 warning has been issued by the National Weather
10 Service or a qualified media outlet,
11 d. any blizzard warning, ice storm warning, winter
12 weather advisory, winter storm warning, or winter
13 storm watch issued by the National Weather Service or
14 a qualified media outlet,
15 e. a red flag warning issued by the National Weather
16 Service or the Forestry Services Division of the
17 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
18 Forestry, or
19 f. a flash flood warning or flood warning issued by the
20 National Weather Service or a qualified media outlet.
21 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-302 of Title 47, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 1647 Page 3
1 A. A qualified media outlet or qualified institution of higher
2 education may hire or contract with not more than five professional
3 severe weather trackers for the purposes provided for in this act.
4 The qualified media outlet or qualified institution of higher
5 education shall:
6 1. Issue specific identification or credentialing information
7 to each hired or contracted professional severe weather tracker; and
8 2. Maintain a list of the names of each hired or contracted
9 professional severe weather tracker on a publicly available website.
10 B. Any professional severe weather tracker employed by or
11 contracted with a qualified media outlet or qualified institution of
12 higher education shall:
13 1. Complete an emergency driving course to be administered by a
14 law enforcement entity in this state, including, but not limited to,
15 a municipal police department, a county sheriff office, the Council
16 on Law Enforcement Education and Training, or the Department of
17 Public Safety;
18 2. Maintain a liability insurance policy of not less than One
19 Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00); and
20 3. Maintain in his or her vehicle the identifying or
21 credentialing information issued by the qualified media outlet or
22 qualified higher education institution.
23
24
Req. No. 1647 Page 4
1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-304 of Title 47, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. Primary vehicles utilized by a professional severe weather
5 tracker shall:
6 1. Display markings clearly identifying such vehicle as being
7 utilized for severe weather tracking and identifying the qualified
8 media outlet or qualified institution of higher education with which
9 the tracker is affiliated; and
10 2. Be authorized to be equipped with and to use the visual
11 signals described in Section 12-218 of Title 47 of the Oklahoma
12 Statutes.
13 B. Once the conditions of a significant weather event have been
14 met, a professional severe weather tracker may:
15 1. Activate the visual signals authorized for use in this act;
16 2. Be considered an authorized emergency vehicle for the
17 purposes of this act;
18 3. Travel upon roads, highways, and county roads closed by the
19 Department of Transportation, the Oklahoma Turnpike Authority, any
20 city or county, or any law enforcement agency by reason of
21 conditions triggered by the significant weather event;
22 4. Proceed past a red or stop signal or stop sign, but only
23 after slowing down as may be necessary for safe operation;
24
Req. No. 1647 Page 5
1 5. Exceed the maximum speed limits so long as speeding does not
2 endanger life or property;
3 6. Disregard regulations governing direction of movement; and
4 7. Disregard regulations governing turning in specified
5 directions.
6 C. The exemptions granted to a professional severe weather
7 tracker in this section shall apply only when the tracker is
8 properly and lawfully making use of flashing green and white lights
9 or a combination of flashing green and white lights meeting the
10 requirements of Section 12-218 of Title 47 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
11 D. The provisions of this section shall not limit the liability
12 of a professional severe weather tracker. The provisions of this
13 section shall not relieve the professional severe weather tracker
14 from the duty to drive with due regard for the safety of all
15 persons, nor shall such provisions protect the driver from the
16 consequences of reckless disregard for the safety of others.
17 E. Nothing in this section shall be construed to exempt a
18 professional severe weather tracker from obligation to yield right-
19 of-way and stop for superseding authorized emergency vehicles, as
20 prescribed in Section 1-103 of Title 47 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
21 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-305 of Title 47, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 1647 Page 6
1 Any professional severe weather tracker found to be in violation
2 of the provisions of this act may be subject to a fine not to exceed
3 Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00). Such fine shall be in addition to
4 any criminal or civil penalties the tracker may be subject to for
5 the violation of the laws of this state.
6 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 19-306 of Title 47, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 Nothing in this act shall be construed to prohibit or limit the
10 rights of any individual to engage in recreational or commercial
11 storm tracking or chasing.

7 7. Vehicles of Oklahoma National Guard units designated by the
8 Adjutant General for support to civil authorities; or
9 8. Vehicles owned and operated by any local organization for
10 emergency management as defined by Section 683.3 of Title 63 of the
11 Oklahoma Statutes; or
12 9. Primary vehicles of professional severe weather trackers
13 hired by or contracted with a qualified media outlet or qualified

15 Section 2 of this act. Provided, however, such authorized emergency

22 B. All Except for primary vehicles of professional severe

24 section shall be equipped with sirens capable of giving audible

4 SECTION 8. AMENDATORY 47 O.S. 2021, Section 12-218, is
5 amended to read as follows:
6 Section 12-218. A. Every authorized emergency vehicle shall,
7 in addition to any other equipment and distinctive markings required
8 by this title, be equipped with flashing red or blue lights or a
9 combination of flashing red and blue lights. The lights shall be
10 visible at five hundred (500) feet in normal sunlight.
11 B. A law enforcement vehicle when used as an authorized
12 emergency vehicle may but need not be equipped with alternately-
13 flashing alternately flashing red or blue lights specified herein.
14 An unmarked vehicle used as a law enforcement vehicle for routine
15 traffic enforcement shall be equipped with the following combination
16 of lights:
17 1. Three flashing red, blue, or a combination of red and blue
18 lights emitting the flashing lights to the front of the vehicle;
19 2. Two flashing white lights emitting the flashing white lights
20 to the front of the vehicle;
21 3. Flashing red, blue, white or any combination of red, blue or
22 white lights placed at and emitting the flashing lights from the
23 four corners of the vehicle so that they are visible for three
24 hundred sixty (360) degrees; and
Req. No. 1647 Page 9
1 4. One flashing red, blue, amber, or any combination of red,
2 blue, or amber lights emitting the flashing light to the rear of the
3 vehicle.
4 C. A professional severe weather tracker hired by or contracted
5 with a qualified media outlet or qualified institution of higher
6 education, as such terms are defined in Section 2 of this act, shall
7 be equipped with a combination of green and white flashing lights.
8 The lights shall be visible at five hundred (500) feet in normal
9 sunlight.
10 D. The use of the signal equipment described herein shall
11 impose upon drivers of other vehicles the obligation to yield right-
12 of-way and stop for authorized emergency vehicles, as prescribed in
13 Section 11-405 of this title.
14 SECTION 9. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
15 SECTION 10. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

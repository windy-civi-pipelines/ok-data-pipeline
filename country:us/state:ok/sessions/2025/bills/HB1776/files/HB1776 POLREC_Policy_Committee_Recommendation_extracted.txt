8 POLICY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
9 An Act relating to definitions and general
provisions; enacting the United States and Oklahoma
10 Flag Display Rights Act of 2025; prohibiting state or
local government entities from restricting persons
11 from making proper display of the flag of the United
States of America or the flag of the State of
12 Oklahoma; providing for noncodification; providing
for codification; and declaring an emergency.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
17 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
18 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "United States
19 and Oklahoma Flag Display Rights Act of 2025".
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 155.1 of Title 25, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. No state or local government entity shall prohibit any
24 person from displaying either the flag of the United States of
Req. No. 12872 Page 1
1 Amer ica or the flag of the State of Oklahoma on property owned by or
2 lawfully occupied by such state or local government entity if the
3 display is made from a vehicle owned by or lawfully operated by such
4 person or if the display is being made by the person holding the
5 flag whether or not the display is made from a vehicle and if the
6 flag is being displayed in accordance with accepted standards for
7 the proper and respectful display of such flag and the flag has not
8 been altered or modified or defaced in any way.
9 B. The provisions of this section shall be applicable to a
10 motorcycle owned or lawfully operated by such person or any other
11 vehicle authorized pursuant to law to be operated on public roads or
12 highways.
13 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
14 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
15 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
16 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

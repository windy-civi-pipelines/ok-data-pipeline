Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to civil procedure; amending 12 O.S.
2021, Section 765, as amended by Section 2, Chapter
8 326, O.S.L. 2022 (12 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 765),
which relates to sheriff sales; prohibiting buyer's
9 premium for online auction; limiting fee for online
auction; and providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 12 O.S. 2021, Section 765, as
14 amended by Section 2, Chapter 326, O.S.L. 2022 (12 O.S. Supp. 2024,
15 Section 765), is amended to read as follows:
16 Section 765. A. Upon the return of any writ of execution for
17 the satisfaction of which any lands or tenements have been sold, the
18 party causing the execution to be issued shall:
19 1. Cause a written notice of hearing on the confirmation of the
20 sale to be mailed, by first class mail, postage prepaid, to all
21 persons to whom mailing of the notice of the execution sale was
22 required to be made pursuant to Section 764 of this title and to the
23 high bidder at such sale, at least ten (10) days before the hearing
24 on the confirmation of the sale, and if the name or address of any
Req. No. 10404 Page 1
1 such person is unknown, shall cause a notice of the hearing on the
2 confirmation of the sale to be published in a newspaper authorized
3 by law to publish legal notices in the county in which the property
4 is situated. If no newspaper authorized by law to publish legal
5 notices is published in such county, the notice shall be published
6 in some such newspaper of general circulation which is published in
7 an adjoining county. The notice shall state the name of any person
8 being so notified and shall be published once at least ten (10) days
9 prior to the date of the hearing on the confirmation of the sale;
10 and
11 2. Files in the case an affidavit of proof of mailing, and if
12 required, of publication.
13 B. Any person filing a written objection to the confirmation of
14 the sale shall cause a copy of such written objection to be mailed,
15 prior to the hearing on the confirmation of the sale, by first class
16 mail, postage prepaid, to all persons to whom mailing of the notice
17 of the hearing on the confirmation of the sale was required to be
18 made pursuant to this section. The court may continue the hearing
19 or make such other orders as are necessary to allow the interested
20 persons to adequately support or oppose any such objections to the
21 confirmation of the sale. If the court, after having carefully
22 examined the proceedings of the officer, is satisfied that the sale
23 has, in all respects, been made in conformity with the provisions of
24 this article, the court shall direct the clerk to make an entry on
Req. No. 10404 Page 2
1 the journal that the court is satisfied of the legality of such sale
2 and shall order that the officer make to the purchaser a deed for
3 such lands and tenements; and the officer, on making such sale,
4 shall deposit the purchase money with the clerk of the court from
5 which said writ of execution issued, where same shall remain until
6 the court shall have examined his or her proceedings as aforesaid,
7 when said clerk of the court shall pay the same to the person
8 entitled thereto, agreeable to the order of the court. In the case
9 of a sale by a sheriff conducted through an online auction
10 marketplace, the online auction marketplace may collect and hold
11 deposits and additional purchase money payments up to the full
12 amount of the winning bid, settle the transaction, and then remit
13 payment of the purchase money to the court clerk as directed by the
14 sheriff or the court. Any No buyer's premium shall be charged to a
15 buyer for on any sale including when a sale is conducted through an
16 online auction marketplace services rendered to the buyer shall not
17 be considered purchase price provided that the buyer's premium is
18 disclosed in advance in the listing platform. The fee charged by
19 the online auction marketplace and all costs incurred by the online
20 auction marketplace shall be assessed as costs in the case not to
21 exceed Three Hundred and Fifty Dollars ($350.00).
22 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
23

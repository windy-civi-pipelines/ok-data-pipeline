11 An Act relating to digital assets; providing
definitions; requiring a license for digital asset
12 kiosk operators to engage in transactions; providing
penalties for violation; authorizing the Attorney
13 General to promulgate rules; permitting civil suits
against digital asset kiosk operators; requiring
14 notice before location or relocation of a digital
asset kiosk; requiring digital asset kiosk operators
15 to submit a quarterly report; requiring digital asset
kiosk operators to disclose terms and conditions;
16 requiring digital asset kiosk operators to provide a
receipt upon each transaction's completion; providing
17 requirement if paper receipt is not possible;
directing all digital asset kiosk operators to use
18 blockchain technology; prohibiting a digital asset
kiosk operator to accept certain transactions
19 equaling more than Two Thousand Dollars, or the
equivalent in digital assets in a single day;
20 limiting fees and charges; requiring live customer
support during certain hours; permitting the Banking
21 Department to promulgate rules; and providing an
effective date.

1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1520 of Title 6, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 As used in this act:
5 1. “Blockchain analytics” means the analysis of data from
6 blockchains or publicly distributed ledgers including, but not
7 limited to, associated transaction information;
8 2. “Blockchain technology” means the same as defined in Section
9 1359 of Title 68 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
10 3. “Currency” means the same as defined in Section 1512 of
11 Title 6 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
12 4. “Digital assets” means the same as defined in Section 1359
13 of Title 68 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
14 5. “Digital asset address” means an alphanumeric identifier
15 associated with a digital asset wallet identifying the location to
16 which a digital asset transaction can be sent;
17 6. “Digital asset wallet” means a software application or other
18 mechanism providing a means to hold, store, or transfer digital
19 assets;
20 7. “Digital asset kiosk” means an electronic terminal acting as
21 a mechanical agent of the digital asset kiosk operator to enable the
22 digital asset kiosk operator to facilitate the exchange of digital
23 assets for money, bank credit, or other digital asset including, but
24 not limited to:
SB1083 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 a. connecting directly to a separate digital asset
2 exchange that performs the actual digital asset
3 transmission, or
4 b. drawing upon the digital asset in the possession of
5 the electronic terminal’s operator;
6 8. “Digital asset kiosk operator” means a person or entity that
7 owns, operates, or manages a digital asset kiosk located in this
8 state offering digital asset kiosk transactions;
9 9. “Digital asset kiosk transaction” means a transaction
10 conducted or performed, in whole or in part, by electronic means via
11 a digital asset kiosk. Digital asset kiosk transaction shall also
12 mean a transaction made at a digital asset kiosk to purchase digital
13 assets with currency or to sell digital assets for currency;
14 10. “New customer” means an individual who has never previously
15 transacted with a digital asset kiosk operator. The new customer
16 shall remain defined as such during the seventy-two (72) hours after
17 the first financial transaction with the digital asset kiosk
18 operator; and
19 11. “Transaction hash” means a unique identifier made up of a
20 string of characters that acts as a record of and provides proof
21 that the transaction was verified and added to the blockchain.

12 nor more than one (1) year, or by both such fine and imprisonment.

18 seized under this subsection.
19 3. The Attorney General is authorized to promulgate rules to
20 effectuate the provisions of this subsection.

11 technology to assist in the prevention of sending purchased digital
12 assets from a digital asset kiosk operator to a digital asset wallet

18 more than Two Thousand Dollars ($2,000.00), or the equivalent in
19 digital assets, in a single day in connection with digital asset
20 kiosk transactions with a new customer in this state by one or more
21 digital asset kiosks.

23 charged to a customer related to a single transaction or series of
24 related transactions involving digital assets effected through a

3 convert digital assets and the prevailing market value of such
4 digital assets at the time of such transaction, shall not exceed
5 fifteen percent (15%) of the United States dollar equivalent of the
6 digital assets involved in the transaction or transactions.
7 J. All digital asset kiosk operators performing business in
8 this state shall provide live customer service at a minimum Monday
9 through Friday from 8:00 a.m. CST to 10:00 p.m. CST. The customer
10 service toll-free number shall be displayed on the digital asset
11 kiosk or the digital asset kiosk screens.
12 K. The Banking Department is authorized to promulgate rules
13 necessary to implement the provisions of this act.

Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to public safety; directing fire
departments to designate an individual to monitor
8 fire site for flare ups and hot spots; clarifying
that designee shall be responsible for observing and
9 ensuring the safety of the affected area; clarifying
designee does not need to be a fire fighter;
10 requiring designee to monitor sight for certain
amount of time; permitting responsibility to be
11 shared; prohibiting fire department from leaving
before having a designee; providing for codification;
12 and providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 170 of Title 63, unless there is
18 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. Any fire department operating within the State of Oklahoma
20 shall, upon concluding extinguishment efforts at the scene of a
21 fire, designate an individual to monitor the site for potential
22 flare-ups or residual hot spots.
23 B. The designee shall assume responsibility for observing and
24 ensuring the safety of the affected area.
Req. No. 10366 Page 1
1 C. The designated individual is not required to be a
2 professional or volunteer firefighter; however, they must have the
3 ability to conduct site inspections at intervals not exceeding one
4 (1) hour for a minimum period of twelve (12) hours following the
5 fire's extinguishment.
6 D. The responsibility for monitoring may be shared among
7 multiple individuals, provided the requirements of subsection C are
8 met.
9 E. The fire department shall leave at least one firefighter at
10 the sight until a designee has been designated.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

6 An Act relating to parole; amending 57 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 516, which relates to parole violators;
7 authorizing parole revocation by certain entity;
clarifying authority under certain circumstance; and
8 providing an effective date.
9
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 516, is
13 amended to read as follows:
14 Section 516. A. Except as provided in subsection B of this
15 section, the probation and parole officer shall, upon information
16 sufficient to give the officer reasonable grounds to believe that
17 the parolee has violated the terms of and conditions of parole,
18 notify the Department of Corrections. If it is determined that the
19 facts justify revocation action, the Department shall issue a
20 warrant for the arrest of the parolee and the warrant shall have the
21 force and effect of any warrant of arrest issued by a district court
22 in this state. The parolee shall, after arrest, be immediately
23 incarcerated in the nearest county jail, intermediate sanctions
24 facility, or a Department of Corrections facility to await action by
ENGR. S. B. NO. 76 Page 1
1 the Governor or the Pardon and Parole Board as to whether the parole
2 will be revoked. Parole time shall cease to run after the issuance
3 of a warrant for arrest by the Department of Corrections, and earned
4 credits shall not be accrued during any period of time when the
5 parolee is incarcerated pending revocation action by the Governor or
6 the Pardon and Parole Board. Pursuant to the provisions of this
7 subsection:
8 1. The Governor shall have the power and authority to revoke
9 parole granted by the Governor and file the certificate of
10 revocation with the Secretary of State; and
11 2. The Pardon and Parole Board, by majority vote, shall have
12 the power and authority to revoke parole granted by the Pardon and
13 Parole Board and file the certificate of revocation with the
14 Secretary of State.
15 B. Any parolee determined to have violated any terms or
16 conditions of parole by the supervising parole officer may be given
17 the option, at the discretion of the Department of Corrections, to
18 be placed in an intermediate sanctions facility for disciplinary
19 sanction and programmatic services in lieu of revocation or when
20 revocation action by the Governor or the Pardon and Parole Board is
21 deemed unnecessary for the nature of the violation. Any parolee for
22 whom a warrant for arrest issues as provided in subsection A of this
23 section may, at the discretion of the Department or the Governor, be
24 placed in an intermediate sanctions facility pending or following
ENGR. S. B. NO. 76 Page 2
1 any action by the Governor or the Pardon and Parole Board as to
2 revocation of parole or required additional conditions to remain on
3 parole. A parolee may be received and processed into the custody of
4 the Department on an expedited basis through any facility serving
5 such purpose or may be processed directly by the intermediate
6 sanctions facility.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.
8 Passed the Senate the 27th day of March, 2025.

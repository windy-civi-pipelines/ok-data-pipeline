18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-180 of Title 70, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. The State Department of Education shall establish and
22 maintain the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program for grades
23 kindergarten through eight.
24 B. For the purposes of this section:
Req. No. 13623 Page 1
1 1. “Cohort” means a student or group of students of no more
2 than five who receive subject-specific high dosage tutoring in
3 mathematics or English language arts (ELA) and are at least one-half
4 grade level behind in mathematics or ELA prior to participating in
5 the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program; and
6 2. “High dosage tutoring” means in-person, subject-specific
7 tutoring conducted by an individual employed by or contracted with a
8 school district participating in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring
9 Program after school hours for a minimum of three one-hour sessions
10 per week per cohort. Tutoring shall be conducted during a minimum
11 of ten (10) weeks in the fall semester and a minimum of twelve (12)
12 weeks in the spring semester.
13 C. 1. Participating school districts shall assess kindergarten
14 through third grade students participating in high dosage ELA
15 tutoring using the screening instrument required by Section
16 1210.508C of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes to track ELA academic
17 growth. Fourth- through eighth-grade students participating in high
18 dosage ELA tutoring shall be assessed using an assessment chosen by
19 the district to determine academic readiness for the next grade
20 level.
21 2. Participating school districts shall assess students
22 participating in high dosage mathematics tutoring using an
23 assessment chosen by the district to determine academic readiness
24 for the next grade level.
Req. No. 13623 Page 2
1 3. Results of screening instruments and assessments
2 administered pursuant to this subsection shall be electronically
3 reported to the State Department of Education three times a year at
4 intervals determined by the Department.
5 D. The State Department of Education shall give priority for
6 participation in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program to school
7 districts federally designated for additional targeted support and
8 improvement or comprehensive support and improvement under the
9 Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 as reauthorized by
10 P.L. No. 114-95, also known as the Every Student Succeeds Act.
11 Participation in the program shall be approved on a first-come,
12 first-served basis without any limitation other than the amount of
13 funds available for the program and the number of school districts
14 that apply.
15 E. High dosage tutors employed by or contracted with public
16 school districts participating in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring
17 Program shall be awarded bonuses in the following amounts:
18 1. One Thousand Six Hundred Dollars ($1,600.00) for each cohort
19 educated by a high dosage tutor per semester; and
20 2. One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) for each academic grade
21 level increase in ELA or mathematics that each student within a
22 cohort experiences within one (1) academic year.
23 F. 1. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort do not
24 improve by at least half a grade level in their tutored subject
Req. No. 13623 Page 3
1 with in the first year of the program, the tutor shall be placed on a
2 conditional one-year term of participation.
3 2. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort fail to
4 achieve half a grade level of growth during the one-year conditional
5 term of participation, the tutor shall lose eligibility to
6 participate in the program. A tutor who loses eligibility pursuant
7 to this paragraph may be reconsidered as a high dosage tutor after
8 two (2) years.
9 3. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort achieve half
10 a grade level of growth during the one-year conditional term of
11 participation, the tutor’s conditional status shall be lifted.
12 G. High dosage tutors shall be provided the necessary
13 information for each student’s subject-specific academic performance
14 level, as determined by the student’s current teacher of the tutored
15 subject.
16 H. 1. School districts participating in the Oklahoma High
17 Dosage Tutoring Program shall obtain written consent from a
18 student’s parent or legal guardian prior to allowing the student to
19 participate in high dosage tutoring.
20 2. If a student participating in high dosage tutoring fails to
21 attend a tutoring session, the tutor shall notify the student’s
22 school district, and the school district shall notify the student’s
23 parent or legal guardian.

4 sessions during a semester, or a student’s behavior during tutoring
5 sessions requires disciplinary action, the student may be removed
6 from the cohort. The performance of a student subject to the
7 provisions of this paragraph shall not count against the high dosage
8 tutor.
9 I. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
10 implement the provisions of this section.

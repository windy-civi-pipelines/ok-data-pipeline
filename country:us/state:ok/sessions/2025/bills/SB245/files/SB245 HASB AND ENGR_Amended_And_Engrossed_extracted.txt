21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-180 of Title 70, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 245 Page 1
1 A. The State Department of Education shall establish and
2 maintain the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program for grades
3 kindergarten through eight.
4 B. For the purposes of this section:
5 1. “Cohort” means a student or group of students of no more
6 than five who receive subject-specific high dosage tutoring in
7 mathematics or English language arts (ELA) and are at least one-half
8 grade level behind in mathematics or ELA prior to participating in
9 the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program; and
10 2. “High dosage tutoring” means in-person, subject-specific
11 tutoring conducted by an individual employed by or contracted with a
12 school district participating in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring
13 Program after school hours for a minimum of three one-hour sessions
14 per week per cohort. Tutoring shall be conducted during a minimum
15 of ten (10) weeks in the fall semester and a minimum of twelve (12)
16 weeks in the spring semester.
17 C. 1. Participating school districts shall assess kindergarten
18 through third grade students participating in high dosage ELA
19 tutoring using the screening instrument required by Section
20 1210.508C of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes to track ELA academic
21 growth. Fourth- through eighth-grade students participating in high
22 dosage ELA tutoring shall be assessed using an assessment chosen by
23 the district to determine academic readiness for the next grade
24 level.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 245 Page 2
1 2. Participating school districts shall assess students
2 participating in high dosage mathematics tutoring using an
3 assessment chosen by the district to determine academic readiness
4 for the next grade level.
5 3. Results of screening instruments and assessments
6 administered pursuant to this subsection shall be electronically
7 reported to the State Department of Education three times a year at
8 intervals determined by the Department.
9 D. The State Department of Education shall give priority for
10 participation in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring Program to school
11 districts federally designated for additional targeted support and
12 improvement or comprehensive support and improvement under the
13 Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 as reauthorized by
14 P.L. No. 114-95, also known as the Every Student Succeeds Act.
15 Participation in the program shall be approved on a first-come,
16 first-served basis without any limitation other than the amount of
17 funds available for the program and the number of school districts
18 that apply.
19 E. High dosage tutors employed by or contracted with public
20 school districts participating in the Oklahoma High Dosage Tutoring
21 Program shall be awarded bonuses in the following amounts:
22 1. One Thousand Six Hundred Dollars ($1,600.00) for each cohort
23 educated by a high dosage tutor per semester; and
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 245 Page 3
1 2. One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) for each academic grade
2 level increase in ELA or mathematics that each student within a
3 cohort experiences within one (1) academic year.
4 F. 1. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort do not
5 improve by at least half a grade level in their tutored subject
6 within the first year of the program, the tutor shall be placed on a
7 conditional one-year term of participation.
8 2. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort fail to
9 achieve half a grade level of growth during the one-year conditional
10 term of participation, the tutor shall lose eligibility to
11 participate in the program. A tutor who loses eligibility pursuant
12 to this paragraph may be reconsidered as a high dosage tutor after
13 two (2) years.
14 3. If the students in a high dosage tutor’s cohort achieve half
15 a grade level of growth during the one-year conditional term of
16 participation, the tutor’s conditional status shall be lifted.
17 G. High dosage tutors shall be provided the necessary
18 information for each student’s subject-specific academic performance
19 level, as determined by the student’s current teacher of the tutored
20 subject.
21 H. 1. School districts participating in the Oklahoma High
22 Dosage Tutoring Program shall obtain written consent from a
23 student’s parent or legal guardian prior to allowing the student to
24 participate in high dosage tutoring.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 245 Page 4
1 2. If a student participating in high dosage tutoring fails to
2 attend a tutoring session, the tutor shall notify the student’s
3 school district, and the school district shall notify the student’s
4 parent or legal guardian.
5 3. If the parent or legal guardian of a student participating
6 in high dosage tutoring no longer wishes for the student to
7 participate, a student fails to attend nine (9) high dosage tutoring
8 sessions during a semester, or a student’s behavior during tutoring
9 sessions requires disciplinary action, the student may be removed
10 from the cohort. The performance of a student subject to the
11 provisions of this paragraph shall not count against the high dosage
12 tutor.
13 I. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
14 implement the provisions of this section.

2 1st Session of the 60th Legislature (2025)
3 PROPOSED OVERSIGHT
COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
4 FOR
HOUSE BILL NO. 1767 By: Kerbs
5
6
7
8 PROPOSED OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to counties and county officers;
directing county offices and courts to offer option
10 to pay via credit card for all transactions;
permitting county offices and courts to pass on
11 service fee; directing county offices and courts to
provide forms; prohibiting charging a fee for forms;
12 directing that certain forms be made available on
county website; mandating county offices and courts
13 have a notary during business hours; prohibiting
notaries from charging a fee; providing for
14 codification; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 272 of Title 19, unless there is
20 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 All county offices and courts shall offer the option to pay via
22 credit card for all transactions. County offices and courts shall
23 be allowed to pass the service fee onto the person requesting to pay
24 via credit card.
Req. No. 12701 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 273 of Title 19, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 All county offices, including courts, shall be responsible for
5 providing any forms necessary to citizens of the county when
6 conducting business or legal proceedings with that county office or
7 court. County offices and courts may not charge a fee for providing
8 or printing such forms. County offices shall make all forms
9 necessary for citizens of the county when conducting business or
10 legal proceedings with that county office or court available on the
11 county website.
12 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 274 of Title 19, unless there is
14 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. Every county office, including the county clerk's office,
16 the county treasurer's office, the county assessor's office, and the
17 county courthouse, shall have a notary available during business
18 hours.
19 B. The notary may not charge a fee for their services.
20 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

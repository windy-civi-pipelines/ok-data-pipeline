14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 9211 of Title 74, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The applicable powers, duties, and responsibilities
18 exercised by the Oklahoma Broadband Office shall be fully
19 transferred to the Oklahoma Department of Commerce on December 31,
20 2030. The Office shall exist as a division of the Department. All
21 records, property, equipment, assets, monies, financial interests,
22 liabilities, matters pending, and funds of the Office shall be
23 transferred to the Department.

1 B. The Department shall succeed to any contractual rights or
2 responsibilities incurred by the Office.
3 C. The administrative rules previously promulgated by the
4 Office shall be transferred to and become a part of the
5 administrative rules of Department upon the effective date of this
6 act. The Office of Administrative Rules in the Office of the
7 Secretary of State shall provide adequate notice in "The Oklahoma
8 Register" of the transferred rules and shall place the transferred
9 rules under the Administrative Code section of the Department. On
10 the effective date of this act, any amendment, repeal, or addition
11 to the transferred rules shall be under the rulemaking authority of
12 the Department.
13 D. The Department and the Office may enter into an agreement
14 for the transfer of personnel from the Office to the Department. No
15 employee shall be transferred to the Department except on the freely
16 given written consent of the employee. Any employees who are
17 transferred to the Department shall not be required to accept a
18 lesser grade or salary than presently received. All employees shall
19 retain leave, sick, and annual time earned, and any retirement and
20 longevity benefits which have accrued during their tenure with the
21 Department. The transfer of any personnel between the state
22 agencies shall be coordinated with the Office of Management and
23 Enterprise Services.

6 Section 9209. A. The Office, with the participation and advice

14 facilitate the framework of the Program. The Office shall

18 services in this state.

14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 9211 of Title 74, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The applicable powers, duties, and responsibilities
18 exercised by the Oklahoma Broadband Office shall be fully
19 transferred to the Oklahoma Department of Commerce on December 31,
20 2030. The Office shall exist as a division of the Department. All
21 records, property, equipment, assets, monies, financial interests,
22 liabilities, matters pending, and funds of the Office shall be
23 transferred to the Department.

4 Office shall be transferred to and become a part of the
5 administrative rules of Department upon the effective date of this
6 act. The Office of Administrative Rules in the Office of the
7 Secretary of State shall provide adequate notice in "The Oklahoma
8 Register" of the transferred rules and shall place the transferred
9 rules under the Administrative Code section of the Department. On
10 the effective date of this act, any amendment, repeal, or addition
11 to the transferred rules shall be under the rulemaking authority of
12 the Department.
13 D. The Department and the Office may enter into an agreement
14 for the transfer of personnel from the Office to the Department. No

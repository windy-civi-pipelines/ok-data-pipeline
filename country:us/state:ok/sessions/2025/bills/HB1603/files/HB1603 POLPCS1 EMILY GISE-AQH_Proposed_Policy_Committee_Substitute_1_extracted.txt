8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to schools; requiring teaching of
human growth and development curriculum; specifying
10 curriculum requirements; requiring school districts
to offer human growth and development curriculum in
11 an age-appropriate manner; allowing district to
choose how to incorporate certain instruction;
12 requiring certain instruction for graduation;
requiring integration of certain instruction with
13 health or science education; requiring opt-out option
be provided to parents and legal guardians; providing
14 for opt-out form; mandating the human growth and
development curriculum be incorporated in the state
15 academic content standards; directing the State
Department of Education to develop certain guidelines
16 and resources for schools and teachers; providing for
noncodification; providing for codification; and
17 providing an effective date.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
22 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
23 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Window into the
24 Womb Act".
Req. No. 12464 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11-203 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. Human growth and development curriculum shall be taught in
5 the public schools of this state. Human growth and development
6 education shall include, but is not limited to:
7 1. Human biology related to pregnancy;
8 2. Human development inside of a womb;
9 3. A high-definition ultrasound video, at least three (3)
10 minutes in duration, showing the development of the fetus in a
11 biological woman; and
12 4. A high-quality, computer-generated rendering or animation
13 showing the process of fetal development, highlighting significant
14 markers in cell growth and organ development until birth.
15 B. School districts shall provide the curriculum described in
16 subsection A of this section to students in an age-appropriate
17 manner. Schools may incorporate the curriculum into an existing
18 course or courses or develop curriculum for a separate course.
19 Schools shall provide parents and legal guardians an opportunity to
20 opt-out of their child being taught the curriculum described in
21 subsection A of this section two (2) weeks prior to the date the
22 curriculum is to be taught. The opt-out option shall be on a form
23 prescribed by the State Board of Education and retained by the
24 school district.
Req. No. 12464 Page 2
1 C. In order to graduate from a public high school accredited by
2 the State Board of Education with a standard diploma, students in
3 grades nine through twelve shall receive human growth and
4 development instruction as described in subsection A of this section
5 unless the student's parent or legal guardian completed an opt-out
6 form as provided for in subsection B of this section. Such
7 instruction shall be integrated into health or science education and
8 shall be incorporated into the state academic content standards
9 adopted by the State Board of Education pursuant to Section 11-103.6
10 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
11 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

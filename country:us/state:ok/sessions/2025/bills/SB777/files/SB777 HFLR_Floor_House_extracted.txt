9 An Act relating to wildlife; amending 2 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 6-313, which relates to commingling with wild
10 aquatic species; prohibiting certain sales; allowing
certain harvest under certain conditions; authorizing
11 the Department of Wildlife Conservation to manage
certain conditions for certain possession; allowing
12 the Department to manage certain transactions;
providing for promulgation of rules; allowing the
13 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
Forestry to assess certain import fees under certain
14 circumstances; requiring each shipment to possess
certain certificate; amending 29 O.S. 2021, Section
15 2-115, which relates to definition of game fish;
expanding definition; and providing an effective
16 date.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 6-313, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 6-313. A. All catfish, minnows, fingerlings, fish,
22 frogs, and other aquatic species which are legally produced,
23 pursuant to the provisions of this act Sections 6-311 through 6-317
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 of t his title, shall be confined to the lands and waters of the
2 licensee in such a manner so as to prohibit:
3 1. Contact with wild fish or other aquatic species in public
4 waters; and
5 2. Such wild fish or other aquatic species in from being
6 commercially harvested from public waters from and becoming a part
7 of the commercial production sold as a wild-born commodity. As used
8 in this paragraph, “wild-born” means wild fish or aquatic species
9 born in public waters and not introduced though aquaculture.
10 B. 1. Except as otherwise provided by this subsection, the
11 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry shall not
12 restrict the manner in which persons, licensed pursuant to this act
13 Sections 6-311 through 6-317 of this title, gather or impound
14 aquatic species raised within the impoundments in their commercial
15 operations.
16 2. No person shall sell or attempt to sell game fish and/or
17 paddlefish obtained from public waters that have been commingled
18 with privately produced fish subject to the provisions of this act
19 Sections 6-311 through 6-317 of this title regarding the capture of
20 wild fish or aquatic species for direct sale. A licensed producer
21 may collect both game fish and nongame fish broodstock from public
22 waters using any legal means of take for use in propagation of fish
23 only in a recirculating aquaculture system up to, but not exceeding,
24 the normal possession limit for a person holding a recreational

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 fish ing license; provided, that the licensed producer complies with
2 all applicable terms set forth by the Department of Wildlife
3 Conservation. Additionally, such individual broodstock may not be
4 sold and cannot be released back into the wild. Production of fish
5 species considered to be species of special concern, excluding
6 paddlefish (polyodon spathula), along with species prohibited under
7 agency rules and federal threatened and endangered species, is
8 prohibited.
9 C. No licensee may receive, free of charge, any catfish,
10 minnow, fingerling, fish, frog or other aquatic species from the
11 state or federal government Meat and any other finished products for
12 human consumption that result from fish spawned in a captive system
13 may be sold in this state or out of state; provided, that the
14 producer or seller obtains the necessary export permits. Live fish
15 of such propagations may also be sold subject to certain
16 restrictions as set forth by rule by the Department.
17 D. The Department may establish a process to facilitate
18 broodstock transactions between the state and licensed in-state
19 aquatic propagators. The Department is authorized to assess a fee
20 necessary to maintain such process and the oversight required for
21 broodstock transactions. Fees imposed shall be reasonable,
22 accessible, and transparent, with clearly defined application
23 requirements, publicly available guidance, and established timelines

1 for application review and approval. The Department may promulgate
2 all rules necessary for the implementation of this subsection.
3 E. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry
4 may assess import fees for any entity selling live fish within this
5 state that does not possess a valid license for commercial
6 production of aquatic species issued by the Oklahoma Department of
7 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry along with an Oklahoma address
8 subject to regular inspections. Fees shall reflect the costs in-
9 state producers are subjected to when exporting to other states.
10 Each shipment shall include a valid Animal and Plant Health
11 Inspection Service health certificate.
12 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 29 O.S. 2021, Section 2-115, is
13 amended to read as follows:
14 Section 2-115. “Game fish” is a fish normally sought after by
15 sportsmen, and includes only largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, white
16 bass, spotted bass, black crappie, white crappie, northern pike,
17 trout, sauger, saugeye, striped bass, hybrid striped bass, walleye,
18 blue catfish and channel catfish. Blue catfish and channel catfish
19 are herein defined to mean “forked tail” catfish.
20 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

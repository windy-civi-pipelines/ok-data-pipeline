15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 13-101.2, as

18 Section 13-101.2. A. There is hereby created the Lindsey
19 Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with Disabilities Program.
20 The Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with Disabilities
21 Program is established to provide a scholarship to a private school
22 of choice for students with disabilities for whom an individualized
23 education program (IEP) in accordance with the Individuals with
24 Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or an individualized service plan

1 (ISP ) pursuant to Section 1-4-704 of Title 10A of the Oklahoma

5 scholarship is received by the State Department of Education, or for
6 students who have at least one incarcerated parent at the time the
7 request for a scholarship is received by the Department.
8 Scholarships shall be awarded beginning with the 2010-2011 school
9 year.
10 B. The parent or legal guardian of a public school student with
11 a disability, on an individualized service plan, or with at least
12 one incarcerated parent may exercise their parental option and
13 request to have a Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship awarded for the
14 child to enroll in and attend a private school in accordance with
15 this section and the scholarship shall be awarded if:
16 1. The student has spent the prior school year in attendance at
17 a public school in this state. For purposes of this section, "prior
18 school year in attendance" means that the student was enrolled in
19 and reported by a school district for funding purposes during the
20 preceding school year regardless of whether or not the student had
21 an IEP at the time the student was counted for funding purposes. A
22 student who is a child of a member of the United States Armed Forces
23 who transfers to a school in this state from out of state or from a
24 foreign country pursuant to a permanent change of station orders of
Req. No. 11309 Page 2
1 the parent shall be exempt from the requirements of this paragraph
2 but shall be required to meet all other eligibility requirements to
3 participate as provided for in this section. A student who has been
4 provided services under an Individual Family Service Plan through
5 the SoonerStart program and during transition was evaluated and
6 determined to be eligible for school district services shall be
7 exempt from the requirements of this paragraph but shall be required
8 to meet all other eligibility requirements to participate as
9 provided for in this section. A student who was in out-of-home
10 placement with the Department of Human Services; who was adopted
11 while in the permanent custody of the Department of Human Services;
12 who is enrolling or is enrolled in a school that exclusively serves
13 students experiencing homelessness; or who was in out-of-home
14 placement with the Office of Juvenile Affairs; or who has at least
15 one incarcerated parent shall be exempt from the requirements of
16 this paragraph but shall be required to meet all other eligibility
17 requirements to participate as provided for in this section; and
18 2. The parent or legal guardian has obtained acceptance for
19 admission of the student to a private school that is eligible for
20 the program as provided in subsection H of this section and has
21 notified the State Department of Education of the request for a
22 scholarship. Requests to participate in the program made after
23 December 1 shall be granted, but funding for scholarships requested
24 after December 1 shall not be available until the beginning of the
Req. No. 11309 Page 3
1 next school year. The request shall be through a communication
2 directly to the Department in a manner that creates a written or
3 electronic record of the request and the date of receipt of the
4 request. The Department shall notify the school district in which
5 the student is enrolled, if applicable, upon receipt of the request.
6 For purposes of continuity of educational choice, the scholarship
7 shall remain in force until the student enrolls in a public school,
8 graduates from high school, or reaches the age of twenty-two (22),
9 whichever occurs first. At any time, the parent or legal guardian
10 of the student may remove the student from the private school and
11 place the student in another private school that is eligible for the
12 program as provided in subsection H of this section or place the
13 student in a public school.
14 C. A student shall be eligible for a scholarship if the parent
15 or legal guardian of the student made a request for a scholarship
16 for the 2010-2011 school year and the student transferred to an
17 eligible private school but was subsequently denied a scholarship
18 because the student did not have an IEP or ISP in effect on October
19 1, 2009, but did meet all other eligibility requirements as set
20 forth in the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with
21 Disabilities Program Act.
22 D. A student shall not be eligible for a Lindsey Nicole Henry
23 Scholarship if the student is not having regular and direct contact
24
Req. No. 11309 Page 4
1 with the private school teachers at the physical location of the
2 private school.
3 E. School districts shall notify the parent or legal guardian
4 of a public school student with a disability or a student who has at
5 least one incarcerated parent of all options available pursuant to
6 this section and inform the parent or legal guardian of the
7 availability of information about the program from the State
8 Department of Education through the toll-free telephone number or
9 website. The notification shall be provided with or included in the
10 copy of the "Parents Rights in Special Education: Notice of
11 Procedural Safeguards" document given to parents at least annually
12 or as otherwise required by law.
13 F. 1. Acceptance of a Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship shall
14 have the same effect as a parental revocation of consent to service
15 pursuant to 20 U.S.C., Sections 1414(a)(1)(D) and 1414(C) of the
16 IDEA.
17 2. Upon acceptance of a Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship, the
18 parent or legal guardian shall assume full financial responsibility
19 for the education of the student including, but not limited to,
20 transportation to and from the private school.
21 G. If the parent or legal guardian requests a Lindsey Nicole
22 Henry Scholarship and the student is accepted by the private school
23 pending the availability of a space for the student, the parent or
24 legal guardian of the student shall notify the State Department of
Req. No. 11309 Page 5
1 Educ ation before entering the private school and before December 1
2 in order to be eligible for the scholarship during the school year
3 when a space becomes available for the student in the private
4 school. If notification is made after December 1, payment of the
5 scholarship shall not begin until the next school year.
6 H. 1. To be eligible to participate in the Lindsey Nicole
7 Henry Scholarships for Students with Disabilities Program, a private
8 school shall notify the State Department of Education of its intent
9 to participate. The notice shall specify the grade levels and
10 services that the private school has available for students with
11 disabilities who are participating in the scholarship program. The
12 State Department of Education shall approve a private school as
13 eligible to participate in the scholarship program upon
14 determination that the private school:
15 a. meets the accreditation requirements set by the State
16 Board of Education or another accrediting association
17 approved by the State Board of Education. The State
18 Department of Education shall list on its website all
19 accrediting associations approved by the Board,
20 b. demonstrates fiscal soundness by having been in
21 operation for one (1) school year or providing the
22 State Department of Education with a statement by a
23 certified public accountant confirming that the
24 private school desiring to participate is insured and
Req. No. 11309 Page 6
1 the owner or owners have sufficient capital or credit
2 to operate the school for the upcoming year by serving
3 the number of students anticipated with expected
4 revenues from tuition and other sources that may be
5 reasonably expected. In lieu of a statement, a surety
6 bond or letter of credit for the amount equal to the
7 scholarship funds for any quarter may be filed with
8 the Department,
9 c. complies with the antidiscrimination provisions of 42
10 U.S.C., Section 2000d,
11 d. meets state and local health and safety laws and
12 codes,
13 e. will be academically accountable to the parent or
14 legal guardian for meeting the educational needs of
15 the student,
16 f. employs or contracts with teachers who hold
17 baccalaureate or higher degrees, or have at least
18 three (3) years of teaching experience in public or
19 private schools, or have special skills, knowledge, or
20 expertise that qualifies them to provide instruction
21 in subjects taught,
22 g. complies with all state laws relating to general
23 regulation of private schools, and
24
Req. No. 11309 Page 7
1 h. adheres to the tenets of its published disciplinary
2 procedures prior to the expulsion of a scholarship
3 student.
4 2. Upon approval of a private school, the State Department of
5 Education shall provide on its website all information and material
6 submitted by the private school with its application.
7 I. 1. Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship Program participants
8 shall comply with the following:
9 a. the parent or legal guardian shall select the private
10 school from the schools approved for eligibility
11 pursuant to subsection H of this section and apply for
12 the admission of the child,
13 b. the parent or legal guardian shall request the
14 scholarship no later than December 1 of the school
15 year during which the scholarship is requested,
16 c. any student participating in the scholarship program
17 shall attend throughout the school year, unless
18 excused by the school for illness or other good cause,
19 and shall comply fully with the code of conduct for
20 the school,
21 d. the parent or legal guardian shall fully comply with
22 the parental involvement requirements of the private
23 school, unless excused by the school for illness or
24 other good cause, and
Req. No. 11309 Page 8
1 e. upon issuance of a scholarship warrant, the parent or
2 legal guardian to whom the warrant is made shall
3 restrictively endorse the warrant to the private
4 school for deposit into the account of the private
5 school. The parent or legal guardian may not
6 designate any entity or individual associated with the
7 private school as the attorney in fact for the parent
8 or legal guardian to endorse a warrant. A parent or
9 legal guardian who fails to comply with this
10 subparagraph shall forfeit the scholarship.
11 2. A participant who fails to comply with this subsection
12 forfeits the scholarship.
13 J. Provisions governing payment of a Lindsey Nicole Henry
14 Scholarship shall be as follows:
15 1. The State Department of Education shall calculate the total
16 cost of all scholarships for all eligible students in the state.
17 The State Department of Education shall then reserve or retain from
18 the total amount appropriated to the State Board of Education for
19 State Aid purposes and any other revenue available for allocation
20 for State Aid purposes the total cost for all scholarship payments;
21 2. The maximum scholarship granted for an eligible student with
22 disabilities shall be a calculated amount equivalent to the total
23 State Aid factors for the applicable school year multiplied by the
24 grade and disability weights for which the student is eligible for
Req. No. 11309 Page 9
1 the applicable school year. The disability weights used in
2 calculating the scholarship amount shall include all disability
3 weights which correspond to the disabilities included in the
4 multidisciplinary evaluation and eligibility group summary for the
5 student at the time the request for a scholarship is made by the
6 parent or legal guardian. The maximum scholarship amount shall be
7 calculated by the State Board of Education for each year the student
8 is participating in the scholarship program;
9 3. The amount of the scholarship shall be the amount calculated
10 in paragraph 2 of this subsection or the amount of tuition and fees
11 for the private school, whichever is less, minus up to two and one-
12 half percent (2 1/2%) of the scholarship amount which may be
13 retained by the State Department of Education as a fee for
14 administrative services rendered. If a private school does not
15 charge tuition, the amount of the scholarship shall be the amount
16 calculated in paragraph 2 of this subsection minus up to two and
17 one-half percent (2 1/2%) of the scholarship amount which may be
18 retained by the State Department of Education as a fee for
19 administrative services rendered. The amount of any assessment fee
20 required by the private school and the amount associated with
21 providing services and therapies to address the disabilities of the
22 student may be paid from the total amount of the scholarship. The
23 amount of the scholarship shall be prorated to reflect the number of
24
Req. No. 11309 Page 10
1 days remaining in the current school year, if the scholarship
2 request is granted after the beginning of the school year;
3 4. The State Department of Education shall notify the private
4 school of the amount of the scholarship within ten (10) days after
5 receiving the request for a scholarship, when the total State Aid
6 factors have been determined for the current fiscal year. The
7 initial payment shall be made after the Department verifies
8 admission acceptance and enrollment. Quarterly payments shall be
9 made upon verification of continued enrollment and attendance at the
10 private school. Payment shall be made by the Department with an
11 individual warrant made payable to the parent or legal guardian of
12 the student and mailed by the Department to the private school that
13 the parent or legal guardian chooses. The parent or legal guardian
14 shall restrictively endorse the warrant to the private school for
15 deposit into the account of the private school;
16 5. The State Department of Education shall not be responsible
17 for any additional costs associated with special education and
18 related services incurred by the private school for the student
19 including the cost of teachers, equipment, material, and special
20 costs associated with the special education class;
21 6. The State Department of Education shall establish a toll-
22 free telephone number or website that provides parents or legal
23 guardians and private schools with information about the program;
24

1 7. The State Department of Education shall require an annual,
2 notarized, sworn compliance statement by participating private
3 schools certifying compliance with state laws and shall retain all
4 records received from a participating private school. The State
5 Department of Education shall post on its website the annual
6 compliance statement for each participating private school; and
7 8. The State Department of Education shall cross-check the list
8 of participating scholarship students with the public school
9 enrollments prior to each scholarship payment to avoid duplication.
10 K. 1. The State Superintendent of Public Instruction shall
11 deny, suspend, or revoke the participation of a private school in
12 the scholarship program if it is determined that the private school
13 has failed to comply with the provisions of this section. However,
14 in instances in which the noncompliance is correctable within a
15 reasonable amount of time and in which the health, safety, or
16 welfare of the students is not threatened, the Superintendent may
17 issue a notice of noncompliance which shall provide the private
18 school with a time frame within which to provide evidence of
19 compliance prior to taking action to suspend or revoke participation
20 in the scholarship program.
21 2. If the Superintendent intends to deny, suspend, or revoke
22 the participation of a private school in the scholarship program,
23 the Department shall notify the private school of the proposed
24 action in writing by certified mail and regular mail to the private
Req. No. 11309 Page 12
1 scho ol's address of record with the Department. The Department
2 shall also notify any parents or legal guardians of scholarship
3 students attending the private school. The notification shall
4 include the reasons for the proposed action and notice of the
5 timelines and procedures set forth in this subsection.
6 3. The private school that is adversely affected by the
7 proposed action shall have fifteen (15) days from receipt of the
8 notice of proposed action to file with the Department a request for
9 an administrative hearing proceeding pursuant to the Administrative
10 Procedures Act.
11 4. Upon receipt of a request for a hearing, the State Board of
12 Education shall commence a hearing within sixty (60) days after the
13 receipt of the formal written request and enter an order within
14 thirty (30) days after the hearing.
15 5. The Board may immediately suspend payment of scholarship
16 funds if it is determined that there is probable cause to believe
17 that there is an imminent threat to the health, safety, or welfare
18 of the students or fraudulent activity on the part of the private
19 school.
20 L. A parent or legal guardian who applies for a Lindsey Nicole
21 Henry Scholarship for a public school student who has at least one
22 incarcerated parent shall, in support of the application, provide
23 documentation verifying a parent is incarcerated and in the custody
24 of the Department of Corrections, another out-of-state prison

1 syst em, or the Federal Bureau of Prisons. The documentation shall
2 include, but not be limited to, the active Department of Corrections
3 inmate number for the incarcerated parent.
4 M. No liability shall arise on the part of the state, the State
5 Board of Education, the State Department of Education, or a school
6 district based on the award or use of any scholarship provided
7 through the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with
8 Disabilities Program.
9 M. N. The inclusion of private schools within options available
10 to public school students in Oklahoma shall not expand the
11 regulatory authority of the state or any school district to impose
12 any additional regulation of private schools beyond those reasonably
13 necessary to enforce the requirements expressly set forth in this
14 section.
15 N. O. If the State Department of Education determines that a
16 school district prior to August 26, 2011, has failed to comply with
17 the provisions of the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students
18 with Disabilities Program Act and has failed to make full or partial
19 scholarship payments for eligible students, the Department shall
20 have authority to reduce the amount of State Aid allocated to the
21 school district or require the school district to make repayment to
22 the Department of State Aid allocations in an amount equal to the
23 amount of scholarship payments the school district failed to make.
24 The Department shall make payment to the parent or legal guardian in
Req. No. 11309 Page 14
1 the amount the school district failed to make in the manner as
2 provided for in subsection J of this section.
3 O. P. The State Department of Education shall annually prepare
4 and post on its website a report of Lindsey Nicole Henry
5 Scholarships for Students with Disabilities Program. The annual
6 report shall include, but not be limited to, the following
7 information:
8 1. The total number and amount of scholarships awarded and
9 reported for each participating private school;
10 2. The total number of scholarships denied;
11 3. The total number and amount of scholarship payments
12 suspended for each participating private school; and
13 4. Data on participating students, disaggregated by years of
14 participation in the program, grade level, economically
15 disadvantaged status, racial and ethnic groups, and disability
16 category.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

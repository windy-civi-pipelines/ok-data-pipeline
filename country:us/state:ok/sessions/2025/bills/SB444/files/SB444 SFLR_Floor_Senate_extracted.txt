6 An Act relating to controlled dangerous substances;
amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 2-315, which relates
7 to submission of out-of-date controlled dangerous
substances for destruction; modifying applicability
8 of certain provisions; modifying terminology;
removing certain limitations on disposal of
9 controlled dangerous substances; requiring compliance
with federal regulation; allowing ultimate user to
10 submit own drugs for disposal under certain
conditions; permitting disposal of decedent’s
11 prescribed drugs under certain conditions;
authorizing qualified hospice program to dispose of
12 decedent’s prescribed drugs under certain conditions;
requiring completion, witness, and submission of
13 certain form; defining terms; and providing an
effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 2-315, is
18 amended to read as follows:
19 Section 2-315. A. Except as otherwise provided by law, any
20 person required to obtain an annual registration pursuant to Section
21 2-302 of this title, or any group home, or residential care home as
22 defined by Section 1-820 of this title shall submit for destruction
23 dispose of, in accordance with 21 C.F.R., Part 1317, all controlled
24 dangerous substances which are out of date, which are unwanted,
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB444 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 unus ed or which are abandoned by their owner at their facility due
2 to death or other circumstances.
3 B. All controlled dangerous substances described in subsection
4 A of this section shall be submitted to the Oklahoma City laboratory
5 of the Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation, along with all
6 required information on forms provided by the Oklahoma State Bureau
7 of Investigation, to the federal Drug Enforcement Administration, to
8 a duly registered reverse distributor, to the original registered
9 supplier or their registered agent, to a duly registered retail
10 pharmacy, or to a hospital or clinic with an on-site pharmacy
11 pursuant to the rules set forth in Part 1317 of Title 21 of the Code
12 of Federal Regulations. When any such substance is transported by
13 private contract or common carrier or United States Postal Service
14 for the purpose of destruction or disposal, the sender shall require
15 a receipt from such private contract or common carrier or United
16 States Postal Service, and such receipt shall be retained as a
17 permanent record by the sender.
18 C. 1. Controlled dangerous substances submitted to the
19 Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation pursuant to the provisions of
20 this section shall be destroyed pursuant to the procedures provided
21 in subsection A of Section 2-508 of this title.
22 2. Controlled dangerous substances submitted to any
23 distributors, reverse distributors or their original registered
24 suppliers pursuant to the provisions of this section shall be
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB444 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 dest royed by incineration so as to make the substance absolutely
2 unusable for human purposes in accordance with 21 C.F.R., Part 1317.
3 An official record listing the property destroyed, the location of
4 destruction and disposal, and the name and title of the person
5 supervising the destruction and disposal shall be submitted to the
6 Oklahoma State Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs Control and
7 the federal Drug Enforcement Administration office located nearest
8 the destruction site.
9 D. The Office of the Chief Medical Examiner is hereby
10 authorized to perform on-site incineration of all controlled
11 dangerous substances which are obtained in the discharge of the
12 official duties of the Chief Medical Examiner. Any record relating
13 to destruction of a controlled dangerous substance shall be
14 maintained as required by the state or federal government and shall
15 be available for inspection by appropriate state or federal
16 government regulatory agencies.
17 E. 1. An ultimate user who has lawfully obtained a controlled
18 dangerous substance in accordance with state and federal law may,
19 without being registered under Section 2-302 of this title, deliver
20 the controlled dangerous substance to an authorized person for the
21 purpose of disposal of the controlled dangerous substance under the
22 conditions provided by 21 U.S.C., Section 822(g)(1).
23 2. If a person dies while lawfully in possession of a
24 controlled dangerous substance for personal use, any person lawfully
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB444 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 enti tled to dispose of the decedent’s property may deliver the
2 controlled dangerous substance to another person for the purpose of
3 disposal under the conditions provided by 21 U.S.C., Section
4 822(g)(4).
5 3. a. In the case of a decedent who was receiving hospice
6 care at the time of death, an employee of a qualified
7 hospice program, acting within the scope of his or her
8 employment, may handle, without being registered under
9 Section 2-302 of this title, any controlled dangerous
10 substance that was lawfully dispensed to the decedent
11 prior to death, under the conditions provided by 21
12 U.S.C., Section 822(g)(5). Such employee shall, under
13 penalty of perjury, complete, sign, and submit to the
14 Oklahoma State Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs
15 Control a form to be prescribed by the Bureau. The
16 form shall also be signed by a witness who is either a
17 second employee of the qualified hospice program or a
18 family member of the decedent.
19 b. As used in this paragraph:
20 (1) “employee of a qualified hospice program” has the
21 same meaning as provided by 21 U.S.C., Section
22 822(g)(5)(B), and
23 (2) “qualified hospice program” means a qualified
24 hospice program as defined in 21 U.S.C., Section
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB444 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 822(g)(5)(B) that is licensed under the Oklahoma
2 Hospice Licensing Act and registered under
3 Section 2-302 of this title.
4 F. This section shall constitute a part of the Uniform
5 Controlled Dangerous Substances Act.
6 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

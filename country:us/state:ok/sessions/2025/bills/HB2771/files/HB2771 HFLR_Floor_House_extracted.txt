12 An Act relating to courts; amending 20 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 122, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 430,
13 O.S.L. 2024 (20 O.S. Supp. 2024, Section 122), which
relates to special judges; increasing the number of
14 special judges in specific counties; determining a
date certain for the increase; providing an effective
15 date; and declaring an emergency.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 20 O.S. 2021, Section 122, as
20 amended by Section 1, Chapter 430, O.S.L. 2024 (20 O.S. Supp. 2024,
21 Section 122), is amended to read as follows:
22 The number of special judges that may be appointed in each
23 judicial administrative district shall be determined as follows:
24
HB2771 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 1. A special judge shall be appointed on the basis of one
2 special judge for each county within the administrative district
3 with a population of at least twenty-four thousand (24,000), as
4 determined by the 1960 Federal Decennial Census. An additional
5 special judge shall be appointed for each additional fifty thousand
6 (50,000) in population in a county within the administrative
7 district, as determined by the 1960 Federal Decennial Census. Such
8 appointment may be made from any county in the administrative
9 district. Such appointments shall be made by the district judges in
10 their respective judicial administrative districts. Any judge of a
11 special sessions court shall be one of the special judges for the
12 balance of his or her term and shall be within the number prescribed
13 for such district.
14 2. In addition to the special judges that may be appointed
15 pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 1 of this section, there
16 shall be:
17 a. one (1) special judge appointed in the Northwest-
18 Panhandle Judicial Administrative District comprised
19 of District Court Judicial Districts Numbers One (1),
20 Two (2) and Four (4), to serve in Custer County,
21 b. beginning July 1, 2025, three (3) one (1) special
22 judge judges appointed in the Oklahoma-Canadian
23 Counties Judicial Administrative District comprised of
24 District Court Judicial District Number Seven (7),
HB2771 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 c. beginning July 1, 2024, five (5) special judges
2 appointed in the Tulsa-Pawnee Counties Judicial
3 Administrative District comprised of District Court
4 Judicial District Number Fourteen (14),
5 d. beginning January 11, 1999, one (1) special judge
6 appointed in the Northeastern Judicial Administrative
7 District comprised of District Court Judicial
8 Districts Numbers Ten (10), Eleven (11), Twelve (12)
9 and Thirteen (13), to serve in Rogers County,
10 e. one (1) special judge appointed in the North-Central
11 Judicial Administrative District comprised of District
12 Court Judicial District Numbers Eight (8), Nine (9)
13 and Twenty-three (23), to serve in Lincoln and
14 Pottawatomie Counties,
15 f. beginning January 1, 2006, one (1) special judge
16 appointed in the East-Central Judicial Administrative
17 District comprised of District Court Judicial District
18 Numbers Fifteen (15), Eighteen (18) and Twenty-four
19 (24), to serve in Pittsburg and McIntosh Counties,
20 g. beginning January 1, 2006, one (1) special judge
21 appointed in the Northeastern Judicial Administrative
22 District comprised of District Court Judicial District
23 Numbers Ten (10), Eleven (11), Twelve (12) and
24 Thirteen (13), to serve in Washington County, and
HB2771 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 h. beginning January 1, 2007, one (1) special judge
2 appointed in the Southeastern Judicial Administrative
3 District comprised of District Court Judicial District
4 Numbers Sixteen (16), Seventeen (17), Nineteen (19),
5 and Twenty-five (25), to serve in Le Flore County.
6 3. If a vacancy occurs in the office of associate district
7 judge, or if an associate district judge becomes unable to perform
8 the duties of his or her office, as determined by the presiding
9 judge of the judicial administrative district, a special judge may
10 be appointed within the judicial administrative district to hold
11 office for the duration of such vacancy or incapacity. After the
12 vacancy is filled, or after the associate district judge becomes
13 able to perform the duties of his or her office, the special judge
14 shall have the power to act in regard to any case which he or she
15 has already tried, but the presiding judge of the judicial
16 administrative district may transfer such a case to any other judge
17 in the judicial administrative district.
18 4. The Chief Justice of the Supreme Court may authorize the
19 appointment of such additional special judges as may be necessary
20 for the proper administration of justice. Such additional special
21 judges shall be appointed after application by a majority of the
22 district judges of a judicial administrative district, stating the
23 reason why an additional special judge is needed. Such additional
24 judges need not be based upon population figures.
HB2771 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
2 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
3 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
4 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
5 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
6
7 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON JOINT COMMITTEE ON APPROPRIATIONS
AND BUDGET, dated 05/19/2025 - DO PASS, As Amended.

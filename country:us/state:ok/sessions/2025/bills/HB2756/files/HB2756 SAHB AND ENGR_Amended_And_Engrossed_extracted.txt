9 Facility Act; defining terms; providing for certain

10 certificates; requiring certain information for

8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
9 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of a new law not to be
10 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
11 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “High Voltage
12 Electric Transmission Facility Act”.
13 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 850 of Title 17, unless there is
15 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 As used in this act:
17 1. “Certificate of authority” means authorization for a
18 transmission developer to comply with the provisions of this act by
19 an order of the Corporation Commission through filing a case with an
20 application, notice, and hearing. The Commission shall establish a
21 unique and identifiable designation for the filing of such cases;
22 2. “Electric transmission facility” means a high-voltage
23 transmission line or high-voltage associated transmission facility
24 with a rating of greater than three hundred (300) kilovolts;
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 2
1 3. “FERC” means the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission;
2 4. “Transfer” means any sale, assignment, or change in
3 ownership of the electric transmission facility by a transmission
4 developer; and
5 5. “Transmission developer” means any person, firm,
6 corporation, or entity that develops, owns, operates, controls,
7 manages, or maintains an electric transmission facility within this
8 state and is licensed to do business in Oklahoma.
9 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 851 of Title 17, unless there is
11 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 A. This section shall not be construed to require any Oklahoma
13 retail electric supplier or rural electric cooperative to secure a
14 certificate of authority for any extension, rebuild, or upgrade to
15 an electric transmission facility.
16 B. A transmission developer shall file an application to obtain
17 a certificate of authority from the Corporation Commission for each
18 electric transmission facility. No electric transmission facility
19 shall be commenced without obtaining a certificate of authority.
20 Such application shall include, but shall not be limited to, the
21 following:
22 1. The name, address, and contact information of a named
23 representative or representatives of the transmission developer and
24
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 3
1 a te lephone number and electronic email address for each such
2 representative or representatives;
3 2. A description and a map or plat of the proposed route or
4 routes or alternative route or routes for any electric transmission
5 facility regardless of whether or not a transmission developer has
6 made a submission or received approval from the Southwest Power Pool
7 or any other applicable regional planning process or other federal
8 authority;
9 3. Identification of any and all such counties within the
10 electric transmission facility’s route or routes or alternative
11 route or routes;
12 4. Identification of any energy resource or resources to which
13 the electric transmission facility is to be directly connected or,
14 to the extent known, resources to which the electric transmission
15 facility could be connected to integrate existing natural gas or
16 other resource generation; and
17 5. An estimate of the costs, including potential costs or
18 savings impacts, to any Oklahoma electric customers and the
19 estimated time frame for the electric transmission facility being
20 developed.
21 C. If the transmission developer is required to file subsequent
22 forms with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) or any
23 other agency or organization due to changing the route or height of
24 individual structures from the locations or heights originally
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 4
1 prop osed, the transmission developer shall, within ten (10) days of
2 filing with the FERC or other agency or organization, file such
3 subsequent forms with the Corporation Commission. A transmission
4 developer shall not be required to start the notification process
5 over unless the subsequent FERC or other agency or organization
6 application expands the project beyond the original boundaries in
7 the application filed with the Corporation Commission.
8 D. Prior to or at the time of the hearing on the certificate of
9 authority, the transmission developer shall provide:
10 1. Within thirty (30) days after filing an application with the
11 Commission as provided for in subsection B of this section, proof of
12 publication that the transmission developer published notice of the
13 application in a newspaper of general circulation in the county or
14 counties in which all or a portion of the electric transmission
15 facility is to be located;
16 2. Within sixty (60) days after filing an application with the
17 Commission as provided in subsection B of this section, proof of
18 service that the transmission developer published notice to be sent
19 by certified mail to:
20 a. the board of county commissioners of every county in
21 which all or a portion of the electric transmission
22 facility is to be located and, if all or a portion of
23 the electric transmission facility is to be located
24
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 5
1 within the incorporated area of a municipality, the
2 governing body of the municipality,
3 b. all surface landowners, as reflected in the public
4 land records of the county clerk’s office, upon which
5 all or any part of the surface estate is located
6 within the electric transmission facility route or
7 routes or alternative route or routes,
8 c. any operator, as reflected in the records of the
9 Commission, who is conducting oil and gas operations
10 upon all or any part of the surface estate as to which
11 the transmission developer intends the construction of
12 the electric transmission facility, and
13 d. any operator, as reflected in the records of the
14 Commission, of an unspaced unit, or a unit created by
15 order of the Commission, who is conducting oil and gas
16 operations for the unit where all or any part of the
17 unit area is within the geographical boundaries of the
18 surface estate as to which the transmission developer
19 intends the construction of the electric transmission
20 facility.
21 If the transmission developer makes a search with reasonable
22 diligence and the whereabouts of any party entitled to any notice
23 described in this paragraph cannot be ascertained or such notice
24
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 6
1 cann ot be delivered, then an affidavit attesting to such diligent
2 search for the parties shall be filed with the Commission;
3 3. Within ninety (90) days of publishing the notice in a
4 newspaper as provided for in paragraph 1 of subsection D of this
5 section, a public meeting in the county or counties in which all or
6 a portion of the electric transmission facility is to be located.
7 Notice of the public meeting shall be published in a newspaper of
8 general circulation and submitted to the board of county
9 commissioners in the county or counties in which all or a portion of
10 the electric transmission facility is to be located. The notice
11 shall contain the place, date, and time of the public meeting.
12 Proof of the publication of the notice shall be submitted to the
13 Commission;
14 4. An attestation that the transmission developer has or shall
15 secure or maintain sufficient insurance coverage to cover any and
16 all aspects of the electric transmission facility from construction
17 through operation and maintenance, or any transfer of ownership;
18 5. Evidence that impacts to the environment and public safety
19 will be addressed by the transmission developer;
20 6. A safety plan that will be in place and will be updated for
21 the electric transmission facility and will be provided to the
22 Commission upon request;
23 7. That the electric transmission facility is beneficial and in
24 the public interest;
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 7
1 8. A decommission plan that identifies the methodology used to
2 mitigate potential impacts resulting from the cessation of operation
3 at the end of the electric transmission facility’s useful life,
4 which includes, but is not limited to:
5 a. identification of specific project components that
6 will be removed,
7 b. a description of the decommission process in the event
8 of abandonment during construction and abandonment
9 during operation,
10 c. a description of the process used for land and road
11 restoration, and
12 d. the financial capability of a transmission developer,
13 including a financial surety guarantee, if required by
14 the Commission prior to commencing construction or
15 upon any transfer of ownership, in a form and amount
16 to be determined by the Commission, which shall remain
17 in effect until release is authorized by the
18 Commission; and
19 9. Any additional information required by the Commission from
20 the transmission developer prior to or during the hearing,
21 including, but not limited to, information related to potential
22 impacts to ad valorem taxes to surface owners.
23 E. Approval through an order of the Commission is required
24 prior to the effectiveness of any transfer.
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 2756 Page 8
1 F. Within two hundred (200) days of filing the original
2 application or any amended application with the Commission, if the
3 transmission developer has met the aforementioned requirements, the
4 Commission shall issue an order granting a certificate of authority.
5 Failure to issue an order on a completed application by the
6 Commission shall result in the certificate of authority being deemed
7 granted.
8 G. The Commission is authorized to assess a filing fee for any
9 certificate of authority, not to exceed One Thousand Dollars
10 ($1,000.00). The Commission shall promulgate rules to implement
11 this act. Nothing in this act will be deemed to authorize any
12 transmission developer the power to exercise eminent domain,
13 condemnation, siting, or project-specific approval not already
14 provided in the Oklahoma Statutes.
15 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.”

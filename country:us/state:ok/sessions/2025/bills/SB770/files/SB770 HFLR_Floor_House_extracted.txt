9 An Act relating to rehabilitation services; amending
74 O.S. 2021, Section 166.2, which relates to the
10 Commission for Rehabilitation Services; expanding
membership of the Commission; removing obsolete
11 language; specifying duration and dates of terms;
stipulating process for appointment of additional
12 members; updating statutory language; and declaring
an emergency.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 166.2, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 166.2. A. Effective July 1, 1993, there is hereby
19 created the Commission for Rehabilitation Services, an agency of the
20 State of Oklahoma this state, a body corporate and politic, with
21 powers of government and with the authority to exercise the rights,
22 privileges and functions as herein specified, with its lawful
23 operations deemed to be an essential governmental function of the
24 State of Oklahoma this state with all the attributes thereof.

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. The Commission shall appoint and remove the Director of the
2 State Department of Rehabilitation Services, approve programs,
3 policy and budget, and perform the necessary functions of a
4 governing board for the State Department of Rehabilitation Services.
5 C. l. The Commission shall consist of three (3) seven (7)
6 members, to be appointed by June 15, 1993, as follows:
7 a. one member two members shall be appointed by the
8 President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate for
9 a three-year term,
10 b. one member two members shall be appointed by the
11 Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives for a
12 three-year term, and
13 c. one member three members shall be appointed by the
14 Governor for a three-year term.
15 2. Thereafter, beginning with the expiration of the terms of
16 the three members initially appointed, the Commission shall consist
17 of three (3) members, appointed as follows:
18 a. one member shall be appointed by the President Pro
19 Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate and shall serve a
20 term of one (1) year,
21 b. one member shall be appointed by the Speaker of the
22 Oklahoma House of Representatives and shall serve a
23 term of two (2) years, and
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 c. one member shall be appointed by the Governor and
2 shall serve a term of three (3) years.
3 3. Except as otherwise provided by this subsection, members
4 shall be appointed for terms of three (3) years. Each term shall
5 begin on July 1 of the respective year and end on June 30 three (3)
6 years later.
7 3. The current appointee of the Speaker of the House of
8 Representatives shall serve until June 30, 2025. The current
9 appointee of the Governor shall serve until June 30, 2026. The
10 current appointee of the President Pro Tempore of the Senate shall
11 serve until June 30, 2027.
12 4. For the purpose of expanding the Commission to seven (7)
13 members, the following additional members shall be appointed:
14 a. one member shall be appointed by the President Pro
15 Tempore of the Senate for a term beginning on July 1,
16 2025, and ending on June 30, 2027,
17 b. two members shall be appointed by the Governor, each
18 for a term beginning on July 1, 2025, and ending on
19 June 30, 2026, and
20 c. one member shall be appointed by the Speaker of the
21 House of Representatives for a term beginning on July
22 1, 2025, and ending on June 30, 2028, in addition to
23 the member the Speaker shall appoint to fill the seat
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 of the current member whose term expires on June 30,
2 2025.
3 5. Thereafter, at the expiration of the term, or termination of
4 the member’s service for any reason, the original appointing
5 authority shall appoint a successor for a term of three (3) years,
6 or for the remainder of an unexpired term.
7 D. Members of the Commission shall be knowledgeable of and have
8 concern for rehabilitation issues and disability issues; provided,
9 that such requirement shall not exclude participation of lay persons
10 as Commission members. All members shall be residents of the state
11 and qualified electors at the time of their appointment. Before
12 entering upon the duties of their office, members of the Commission
13 shall take the Constitutional oath of office and the same shall be
14 filed with the Secretary of State. A member of the Commission may
15 be reappointed to succeed himself or herself. Commission members
16 shall be reimbursed for travel expenses incurred in the performance
17 of their duties as provided in the State Travel Reimbursement Act.
18 E. The original appointing authority may remove any
19 commissioner for misconduct, incompetency or neglect of duty, after
20 giving such commissioner a written statement of charges, and
21 opportunity for a hearing.
22 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
23 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby

14 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1151.25a of Title 59, unless
16 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. Except as otherwise provided in the Roofing Contractor
18 Registration Act, on and after November 1, 2025, every roofing
19 contractor offering to engage in or engaging in performing
20 residential roofing contractor work in this state shall be required
21 to receive a residential roofing endorsement. To receive a
22 residential roofing endorsement the roofing contractor shall be
23 required to take and successfully pass a residential roofing
24 examination approved under the provisions of this act, and be in
Req. No. 10105 Page 16
1 comp liance with all other provisions of the act and rules thereof
2 before acting or being endorsed as a residential roofing contractor.
3 For applicants who have been registered and in good standing with
4 the Construction Industries Board since November 1, 2022, there will
5 be a twelve-month grandfathering-in period from November 1, 2025, to
6 either pass the exam or complete an additional ten (10) hours of
7 continuing education credits to be granted a residential roofer
8 endorsement.
9 B. Examinations for a residential roofer endorsement shall be
10 uniform and practical in nature and sufficiently strict to test the
11 qualifications and fitness of the applicants for endorsement.
12 Examinations shall be in whole or in part in writing. Examinations
13 shall be offered at least monthly; dates, times, and locations are
14 to be determined by the registrar or third-party vendor with
15 approval of the registrar.
16 C. Any applicant initially failing to pass the examination
17 shall not be permitted to take another examination for a period of
18 thirty (30) days. Any applicant subsequently failing to pass the
19 examination shall not be permitted to take another examination for a
20 period of ninety (90) days. However, in a declared state of
21 emergency, the examination shall be available as often as possible
22 as determined by the registrar.
23 D. No residential roofer endorsement shall be renewed unless
24 the qualifying party has completed the required hours of continuing
Req. No. 10105 Page 17
1 educ ation, as determined and approved by the Committee of Roofing
2 Examiners and the Construction Industries Board.
3 E. For all new applicants not registered and in good standing
4 with the Construction Industries Board prior to November 1, 2025,
5 the Construction Industries Board will issue a residential roofer
6 endorsement to qualifying applicants no sooner than thirty (30) days
7 from the date of the application. New applicants shall not be
8 allowed to sell, offer, bid, inspect or perform any residential
9 roofing contractor work in this state until a residential roofer
10 endorsement is issued.
11 F. For all applicants registered and in good standing with the
12 Construction Industries Board prior to November 1, 2025:
13 1. The residential roofer endorsement shall be issued
14 immediately upon compliance with the provisions of this section; and
15 2. Shall be allowed to continue residential roofing contractor
16 work at all times during the residential roofer endorsement
17 application process.
18 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

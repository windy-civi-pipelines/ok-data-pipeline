13 WHEREAS, Catherine Ann "Kate" Barnard was born on May 23, 1875,
14 in Geneva, Nebraska, and moved to Oklahoma at the age of 12 where
15 she became a devout Catholic and lifelong servant of the people; and
16 WHEREAS, Kate Barnard was the first woman elected to statewide
17 office in the United States, serving two terms as the Oklahoma
18 Commissioner of Charities beginning in 1907 at a time when women in
19 Oklahoma did not yet have the right to vote; and
20 WHEREAS, during her tenure, Kate Barnard was responsible for the
21 passage of over thirty (30) statutory laws, many of which laid the
22 foundation for the modern Department of Corrections, Department of
23 Human Services, and Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
24 Services; and

1 WHEREAS, the fearless advocacy of Kate Barnard extended to
2 improving conditions for the working class, addressing child labor,
3 expanding access to education, and reforming the prison and mental
4 health systems; and
5 WHEREAS, despite retaliation by powerful political and
6 industrial interests, including defunding her office, Kate Barnard
7 remained steadfast in her mission to return stolen oil lands and
8 financial assets to Native American children, securing over One
9 Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00) on their behalf; and
10 WHEREAS, the work of Kate Barnard continues to inspire
11 generations of advocates, policymakers, and public servants, and as
12 such, her legacy is honored with a statue in the Oklahoma State
13 Capitol and the naming of the Kate Barnard Community Corrections
14 Center; and
15 WHEREAS, the enduring message from Kate Barnard to future
16 generations stating, "I bequeath the example of my public life to
17 the youth of the world …" calls us to uphold justice, integrity, and
18 compassion in public service.
19 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
20 OF THE 1ST SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
21 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives hereby honors the
22 life and legacy of Kate Barnard, a trailblazing woman of faith,
23 courage, and conviction.
24

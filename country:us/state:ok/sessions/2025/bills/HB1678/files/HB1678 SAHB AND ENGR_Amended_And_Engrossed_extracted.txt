Section 2:
Section 8-105, which relates to tie votes; directing
8 for a second election to be held in the case of tie

14 “An Act relating to election certifications; amending
26 O.S. 2021, Section 8-105, which relates to tie
15 votes; modifying procedures for selection of nominee
or electee in certain elections; providing exception
16 to applicability of certain procedures; updating
statutory language; and providing an effective date.
17
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 8-105, is
21 amended to read as follows:
22 Section 8-105. A. When a tie vote is certified in the
23 nomination or election of any candidate in any Runoff Primary,
24 General Election, or any Primary Election, the election board which
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1678 Page 1
1 that is authorized by law to issue the certified list or certificate
2 of election shall, at a public meeting of the board and in the
3 presence of the candidates involved or their designee, if they or
4 any of them desire to be present, select the nominee or electee by
5 lot.
6 B. When a nominee or electee is to be selected by lot pursuant
7 to the provisions of this section, the following procedures shall be
8 observed:
9 1. The secretary of the appropriate election board shall, on or
10 before the tenth day following the election, notify each of the
11 tying candidates for which the vote was tied. The notice shall
12 include the time, date, and location of the selection, shall be made
13 in writing by registered or certified mail, and shall be postmarked
14 not fewer than five (5) days prior to the meeting;
15 2. A candidate may designate one person as a witness to attend
16 the meeting on the candidate’s behalf. The designation shall be
17 made in writing, signed by the candidate, and presented to the
18 secretary of the appropriate election board;
19 3. The secretary of the appropriate election board shall, in
20 full view of those present at the meeting, clearly write or print
21 the name of each tied candidate on separate pieces of paper
22 measuring approximately equal size. The names of the candidates
23 shall be written or printed on the same color and type of paper.
24 The papers shall be folded in half one time so that the written
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1678 Page 2
1 name s are not visible and shall each be placed into a separate small
2 opaque container of equal characteristics selected by the secretary
3 of the appropriate election board. The opaque containers shall then
4 be placed inside a larger transparent container in which the opaque
5 canisters shall be tumbled or shaken so that the opaque containers
6 change position with respect to each other;
7 4. The secretary shall draw, or may designate a person other
8 than the candidates, witnesses, or other person directly interested
9 in the election to draw, one paper opaque container from inside the
10 transparent container, and the name of the nominee or electee
11 appearing on the first drawn paper inside the first drawn opaque
12 container shall be declared the winner. The secretary secretary’s
13 designee shall then expose the other name or names not drawn to all
14 witnesses present; and
15 5. The meeting shall be held on a weekday, holidays excepted,
16 between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m.
17 C. When there are three (3) or more candidates and a tie for
18 first place occurs for the nomination of a candidate at a Primary
19 Election for which a Runoff Primary will be held, the names of the
20 tied candidates shall be placed on the Runoff Primary ballot.
21 D. The procedures prescribed in this section shall not apply to
22 elections of state officers pursuant to Section 5 of Article VI of
23 the Oklahoma Constitution.
24 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.”

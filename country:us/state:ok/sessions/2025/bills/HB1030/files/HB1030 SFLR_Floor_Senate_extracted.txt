8 An Act relating to sunset; amending 59 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 199.2, which relates to the State Board of
9 Cosmetology and Barbering; re-creating the Board; and
modifying the termination date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 199.2, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 199.2. A. 1. There is hereby re-created, to continue
16 until July 1, 2024 2028, in accordance with the provisions of the
17 Oklahoma Sunset Law, a State Board of Cosmetology and Barbering
18 which shall be composed of eleven (11) members to be appointed by
19 the Governor and to serve at the pleasure of the Governor.
20 2. One member shall be appointed from each congressional
21 district and the additional members shall be appointed at-large.
22 However, when congressional districts are redrawn, each member
23 appointed prior to July 1 of the year in which such modification
24 becomes effective shall complete the current term of office and
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1030 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 appo intments made after July 1 of the year in which such
2 modification becomes effective shall be based on the redrawn
3 districts. Appointments made after July 1 of the year in which such
4 modification becomes effective shall be from any redrawn districts
5 which are not represented by a board member until such time as each
6 of the modified congressional districts are represented by a board
7 member. One member shall be a barber appointed at-large.
8 3. At the time of appointment, the members shall be citizens of
9 this state, at least twenty-five (25) years of age, and shall be
10 high school graduates. Six members shall, at the time of
11 appointment, have had at least five (5) years' continuous practical
12 experience in the practice of cosmetology in this state; one member
13 shall be appointed at large and, at the time of the appointment,
14 have had at least five (5) years' continuous practical experience in
15 the practice of barbering in this state; one member shall be a lay
16 person; one member shall be an administrator of a licensed private
17 cosmetology school; one member shall be an administrator of a
18 licensed barber school; and one member shall be an administrator of
19 a public school licensed to teach cosmetology or barbering.
20 4. No two members shall be graduates of the same cosmetology
21 school, nor shall they be organizers of or promote the organization
22 of any cosmetic, beauty, or hairdressers' association. Each of the
23 eight cosmetology appointees shall continue to be actively engaged
24 in the profession of cosmetology while serving. No two members
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1030 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 enga ged in the profession of barbering shall be organizers of or
2 promote the organization of any barbering association. Each of the
3 two barbering appointees shall continue to be actively engaged in
4 the profession of barbering while serving.
5 5. If any member retires or ceases to practice his or her
6 profession during the term of membership on the Board, such terms
7 shall automatically cease and the Governor shall appoint a like-
8 qualified person to fulfill the remainder of the term.
9 B. The terms of office for Board members shall be four (4)
10 years ending June 30.
11 C. Each member shall serve until a successor is appointed and
12 qualified.
13 D. Six members of the Board shall constitute a quorum for the
14 transaction of business.
15 E. The Governor may remove any member of the Board at any time
16 at the Governor's discretion. Vacancies shall be filled by
17 appointment by the Governor for the unexpired portion of the term.
18 F. The Board shall organize by electing from its membership a
19 chair and vice-chair, each to serve for a period of one (1) year.
20 The presiding officer shall not be entitled to vote upon any
21 question except in the case of a tie vote.
22 Members shall be reimbursed for their actual and necessary
23 traveling expenses as provided by the State Travel Reimbursement
24 Act.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1030 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 G. Within thirty (30) days after the end of each fiscal year,
2 the Board shall make a full report to the Governor of all its
3 receipts and expenditures, and also a full statement of its work
4 during the year, together with such recommendations as the Board
5 deems expedient.
6 H. The Board may expend funds for suitable office space for the
7 transaction of its business. The Board shall adopt a common seal
8 for the use of the executive director in authenticating Board
9 documents.
10 I. The Board shall meet at its office for the transaction of
11 such business as may come before it on the second Monday in January,
12 March, May, July, September, and November and at such other times as
13 it may deem advisable.
14 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON ADMINISTRATIVE RULES

13 a license; and providing an effective date.

6 Section 624. A. Except as otherwise provided in this section,

10 Part 493 verifying that the applicant is not infected with the human
11 immunodeficiency virus (HIV), the hepatitis B virus or the hepatitis
12 C virus. The testing shall be performed no more than Testing shall
13 be conducted within three hundred sixty-five (365) days before the
14 application is submitted. A statement from a doctor of osteopathy
15 or medical doctor indicating that the applicant has successfully
16 completed a full course of vaccinations for hepatitis B may be
17 submitted in lieu of the results of testing for hepatitis B before
18 the combative sports contest or exhibition the participant is
19 participating in. Except as otherwise provided in this section, an
20 applicant who receives positive results from any of the tests
21 required by this section shall be denied a license for the
22 particular contest.
23 B. If a participant must be replaced within twenty-four (24)
24 hours of an event, a promoter may replace the participant that has
Req. No. 10325 Page 14
1 been pulled with a new participant that has complied with the
2 testing requirements found in subsection A of this section.
3 C. Notwithstanding the requirement for testing stated in
4 subsection A of this section, the participant and opponent, ring
5 official, and ringside physician may agree, in writing, to waive the
6 denial of licensure for failure to timely submit the required
7 certified test results, and by such signed written agreement, the
8 participant and opponent shall be granted a license to participate
9 in their particular contest.

11 An Act relating to the Judicial Nominating
Commission; providing for recusal of member based
12 upon certain relationship based on consanguinity or
affinity; providing for removal of member based on
13 violation of requirement with respect to recusal;
requiring disclosure with respect to certain judicial
14 campaign contributions; prescribing requirement
related to address for purposes of determining
15 eligibility of members of the Oklahoma Bar
Association for membership on the Judicial Nominating
16 Commission; providing for codification; and providing
an effective date.
17
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 26 of Title 20, unless there is
22 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. No member of the Judicial Nominating Commission who is
24 related to an applicant for any position for which the Commission is

5 to the applicant within the third degree of consanguinity or

7 participates in the decision-making process with respect to an

15 Commission shall make full disclosure of the date and amount of any
16 contribution made by such member to the judicial campaign of any
17 person making application to the Commission for their possible
18 nomination to an appointing authority pursuant to the provisions of
19 Section 3 of Article VII-B of the Constitution of the State of
20 Oklahoma. The Administrative Director of the Courts shall prescribe

3 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 27 of Title 20, unless there is
5 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 With respect to members of the Oklahoma Bar Association who may
7 be eligible for appointment or election to the Judicial Nominating
8 Commission, the address for the person as reflected in the records
9 of the State Election Board shall be used for purposes of
10 determining the residence of such person for purposes of eligibility
11 for membership on the Judicial Nominating Commission.
12 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

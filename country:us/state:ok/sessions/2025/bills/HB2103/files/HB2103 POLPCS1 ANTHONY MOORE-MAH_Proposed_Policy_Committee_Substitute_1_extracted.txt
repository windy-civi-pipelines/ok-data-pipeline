8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to the Judicial Nominating
Commission; providing for recusal of member based
10 upon certain relationship based on consanguinity or
affinity; providing for removal of member based on
11 violation of requirement with respect to recusal;
requiring disclosure with respect to certain judicial
12 campaign contributions; prescribing requirement
related to address for purposes of determining
13 eligibility of members of the Oklahoma Bar
Association for membership on the Judicial Nominating
14 Commission; providing for codification; and providing
an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 26 of Title 20, unless there is
20 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. No member of the Judicial Nominating Commission who is
22 related to an applicant for any position for which the Commission is
23 responsible to submit nominations to an appointing authority
24
Req. No. 12371 Page 1
1 purs uant to Section 3 of Article VII-B of the Constitution of the
2 State of Oklahoma shall participate in the decision-making process
3 with respect to such applicant if the Commission member is related
4 to the applicant within the third degree of consanguinity or
5 affinity. If a member of the Judicial Nominating Commission
6 participates in the decision-making process with respect to an
7 applicant in contravention of the requirement of this subsection,
8 the member shall be immediately removed from the Commission and
9 shall be permanently forbidden from being appointed to the
10 Commission.
11 B. No later than ten (10) days after receiving the final list
12 of applications for an open position which is set to come before the
13 Judicial Nominating Commission, a member of the Judicial Nominating
14 Commission shall make full disclosure, upon such form as may be
15 prescribed by the Administrative Director of the Courts, of the date
16 and amount of any contribution made by such member to the judicial
17 campaign of any person making application to the Commission for
18 their possible nomination to an appointing authority pursuant to the
19 provisions of Section 3 of Article VII-B of the Constitution of the
20 State of Oklahoma.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 27 of Title 20, unless there is
23 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 12371 Page 2
1 With respect to members of the Oklahoma Bar Association who may
2 be eligible for appointment or election to the Judicial Nominating
3 Commission, the address for the person as reflected in the records
4 of the State Election Board shall be used for purposes of
5 determining the residence of such person for purposes of eligibility
6 for membership on the Judicial Nominating Commission.
7 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

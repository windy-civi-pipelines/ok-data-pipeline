11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3319 of Title 70, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. There is hereby established the Program of American Civic
16 Thought and Leadership at the University of Oklahoma. It is the
17 intent of the Legislature that the purpose of the program shall be
18 to:
19 1. Educate students in the principles, ideals, and institutions
20 of the American political order including the character traits and
21 durable habits necessary to sustain that order;
22 2. Educate students in the philosophical foundations of
23 responsible leadership and informed citizenship;
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 244 Page 1
1 3. Promote and provide programming related to civic education,
2 engagement, and discourse;
3 4. Enhance education in the fields of political thought and
4 science, American history and government, free market economics, and
5 other related fields as appropriate, with a focus on the foundations
6 of Western and American civilization including its great books, the
7 rights and responsibilities of American citizenship, fundamental
8 democratic principles and mores, and the ways in which those
9 principles and mores are expressed in and through mediating
10 institutions at the federal, state, and local levels;
11 5. Provide viewpoint and ideologically diverse resources to
12 students, faculty, state government agencies and actors, educators,
13 and the general public regarding civic affairs; and
14 6. Assist in ensuring that the University of Oklahoma serve as
15 a robust marketplace of ideas for all students and faculty.
16 B. To carry out the purposes set forth in subsection A of this
17 section, the program is authorized to:
18 1. Enroll students;
19 2. Hire tenure-track and non-tenured faculty and supporting
20 staff;
21 3. Develop and offer in-person courses and online instruction
22 in new graduate and undergraduate major and minor programs;
23 4. Develop an undergraduate American institutions and civic
24 knowledge course in anticipation of a future mandate requiring all
ENGR. S. B. NO. 244 Page 2
1 Univ ersity of Oklahoma students to complete such a course to
2 graduate;
3 5. Offer general education and honors courses including courses
4 intended for incoming freshman students;
5 6. Offer concurrent enrollment;
6 7. Provide and facilitate internships and other relevant
7 experiences for students and the general public;
8 8. Hold events; and
9 9. Take other actions as appropriate subject to the supervision
10 of the director of the program and the academic college at the
11 University of Oklahoma.
12 C. Upon its establishment, the Program of American Civic
13 Thought and Leadership may be physically housed on the University of
14 Oklahoma’s Norman campus as a stand-alone program.
15 D. The Program of American Civic Thought and Leadership shall
16 operate as an academic unit within the University of Oklahoma.
17 E. The Program of American Civic Thought and Leadership and its
18 director shall be advised by an advisory board on matters related to
19 the program including, but not limited to, staffing, curriculum,
20 policy, and programming in accordance with the policies and
21 procedures of the University of Oklahoma. The advisory board shall
22 be comprised of six (6) members appointed by the Board of Regents of
23 the University of Oklahoma. Members shall be appointed by December
24 31, 2025.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 244 Page 3
1 F. By March 1, 2027, and by each March 1 thereafter, the
2 program director, in consultation with the academic college and
3 advisory board, shall develop and provide an annual strategic plan
4 for the program to the Board of Regents of the University of
5 Oklahoma. Initial course offerings shall be available to students
6 in the fall semester of 2027.
7 G. Faculty of the program may be awarded tenure subject to the
8 tenure policies of the University of Oklahoma as adopted by the
9 Board of Regents of the University of Oklahoma and shall not be
10 required to gain joint appointment in another division of the
11 University of Oklahoma. If a faculty member is awarded tenure and
12 the program later dissolves, the faculty member shall be relocated
13 within another academic unit of the University of Oklahoma.
14 H. The Board of Regents of the University of Oklahoma may use
15 funds allocated to it by the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher
16 Education and charitable donations to fund the program. Allocated
17 and donated funds shall only be used for the direct operation of the
18 program.
19 I. By September 1, 2027, and by each September 1 thereafter,
20 the program director, in consultation with the the advisory board of
21 the program, shall electronically submit a report to the Governor,
22 the President Pro Tempore of the Senate, the Speaker of the House of
23 Representatives, and the chairs of the Senate Appropriations
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 244 Page 4
1 Comm ittee and the House of Representatives Appropriations and Budget
2 Committee.
3 SECTION 2. There is hereby appropriated to the Oklahoma State
4 Regents for Higher Education from any monies not otherwise
5 appropriated from the General Revenue Fund of the State Treasury for
6 the fiscal year ending June 30, 2023, the sum of Three Million
7 Dollars ($3,000,000.00) or so much thereof as may be necessary for
8 the purpose of implementing the provisions of Section 1 of this act.
9 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
10 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
11 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
12 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
13 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
14 Passed the Senate the 27th day of March, 2025.

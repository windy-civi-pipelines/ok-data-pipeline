6 An Act relating to the Department of Rehabilitation
Services; amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section 166.2, which
7 relates to Commission of Rehabilitation Services,
creation, and members; increasing the number of
8 members; and providing an effective date.
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
11 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 166.2, is
12 amended to read as follows:
13 Section 166.2. A. Effective July 1, 1993, there is hereby
14 created the Commission for Rehabilitation Services, an agency of the
15 State of Oklahoma, a body corporate and politic, with powers of
16 government and with the authority to exercise the rights, privileges
17 and functions as herein specified, with its lawful operations deemed
18 to be an essential governmental function of the State of Oklahoma
19 with all the attributes thereof.
20 B. The Commission shall appoint and remove the Director of the
21 Department of Rehabilitation Services, approve programs, policy and
22 budget, and perform the necessary functions of a governing board for
23 the State Department of Rehabilitation Services.
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2387 Page 1
1 C. l. The Commission shall consist of three (3) seven (7)
2 members, to be appointed by June 15, 1993, as follows:
3 a. one member two members shall be appointed by the
4 President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate for
5 a three-year term,
6 b. one member two members shall be appointed by the
7 Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives for a
8 three-year term, and
9 c. one member three members shall be appointed by the
10 Governor for a three-year term.
11 2. Thereafter, beginning with the expiration of the terms of
12 the three members initially appointed, the Commission shall consist
13 of three (3) members, appointed as follows:
14 a. one member shall be appointed by the President Pro
15 Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate and shall serve a
16 term of one (1) year,
17 b. one member shall be appointed by the Speaker of the
18 Oklahoma House of Representatives and shall serve a
19 term of two (2) years, and
20 c. one member shall be appointed by the Governor and
21 shall serve a term of three (3) years Except as
22 otherwise provided by this subsection, members shall
23 be appointed for terms of three (3) years. Each term
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2387 Page 2
1 shall begin on July 1 of the respective year and end
2 on June 30 three (3) years later.
3 3. The current appointee of the Speaker of the House of
4 Representatives shall serve until June 30, 2025. The current
5 appointee of the Governor shall serve until June 30, 2026. The
6 current appointee of the President Pro Tempore of the Senate shall
7 serve until June 30, 2027.
8 4. For the purpose of expanding the Commission to seven (7)
9 members, the following additional members shall be appointed:
10 a. one member shall be appointed by the President Pro
11 Tempore of the Senate for a term beginning on July 1,
12 2025 and ending on June 30, 2027,
13 b. two members shall be appointed by the Governor, each
14 for a term beginning on July 1, 2025 and ending on
15 June 30, 2026, and
16 c. one member shall be appointed by the Speaker of the
17 House of Representatives for a term beginning on July
18 1, 2025 and ending on June 30, 2028, in addition to
19 the member the Speaker shall appoint to fill the seat
20 of the current member whose term expires on June 30,
21 2025.
22 3. 5. Thereafter, at the expiration of the term, or termination
23 of the member's service for any reason, the original appointing
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2387 Page 3
1 auth ority shall appoint a successor for a term of three (3) years,
2 or for the remainder of an unexpired term.
3 D. Members of the Commission shall be knowledgeable of and have
4 concern for rehabilitation issues and disability issues; provided,
5 that such requirement shall not exclude participation of lay persons
6 as Commission members. All members shall be residents of the state
7 and qualified electors at the time of their appointment. Before
8 entering upon the duties of their office, members of the Commission
9 shall take the Constitutional oath of office and the same shall be
10 filed with the Secretary of State. A member of the Commission may
11 be reappointed to succeed himself or herself. Commission members
12 shall be reimbursed for travel expenses incurred in the performance
13 of their duties as provided in the State Travel Reimbursement Act.
14 E. The original appointing authority may remove any
15 commissioner for misconduct, incompetency or neglect of duty, after
16 giving such commissioner a written statement of charges, and
17 opportunity for a hearing.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

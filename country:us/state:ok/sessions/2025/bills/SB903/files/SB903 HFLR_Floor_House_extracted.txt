9 An Act relating to the state Medicaid program;
amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 5009.2, which relates
10 to the Advisory Committee on Medical Care for Public
Assistance Recipients; adding specified member;
11 updating statutory language; and providing an
effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 5009.2, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 5009.2. A. The Advisory Committee on Medical Care for
18 Public Assistance Recipients, created by the Oklahoma Health Care
19 Authority pursuant to 42 Code of Federal Regulations, Section
20 431.12, for the purpose of advising the Authority about health and
21 medical care services, shall include among its membership of no more
22 than fifteen (15) sixteen (16) the following:
23 1. Board-certified physicians and other representatives of the
24 health professions who are familiar with the medical needs of low-
SB903 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 inco me population groups and with the resources available and
2 required for their care. The Advisory Committee shall, at all
3 times, include at least one physician from each of the six classes
4 of physicians listed in Section 725.2 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma
5 Statutes. All such physicians and other representatives of the
6 health professions shall be participating providers in the State
7 Medicaid Plan;
8 2. Members of consumers’ groups, including, but not limited to:
9 a. Medicaid recipients, and
10 b. representatives from consumer organizations including
11 a member representing nursing homes, a member
12 representing individuals with developmental
13 disabilities and a member representing one or more
14 behavioral health professions;
15 3. The Director of the Department of Human Services or
16 designee;
17 4. The Commissioner of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
18 Services or designee;
19 5. A member approved and appointed by a state organization or
20 state chapter of a national organization of pediatricians dedicated
21 to the health, safety and well-being of infants, children,
22 adolescents and young adults, who shall:
23 a. monitor provider relations with the Oklahoma Health
24 Care Authority, and
SB903 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 b. create a forum to address grievances; and
2 6. A member who is a member or citizen of a federally
3 recognized American Indian tribe or nation whose primary tribal
4 headquarters is located in this state; and
5 7. A member representing a contracted entity as defined in
6 Section 4002.2 of Title 56 of the Oklahoma Statutes or a health plan
7 association representing more than one such contracted entity.
8 Beginning on January 1, 2022, appointments made to the Advisory
9 Committee shall be for a duration not to exceed four (4) consecutive
10 calendar years.
11 B. The Advisory Committee shall meet bimonthly to review and
12 make recommendations related to:
13 1. Policy development and program administration;
14 2. Policy changes proposed by the Authority prior to
15 consideration of such changes by the Authority;
16 3. Financial concerns related to the Authority and the
17 administration of the programs under the Authority; and
18 4. Other pertinent information related to the management and
19 operation of the Authority and the delivery of health and medical
20 care services.
21 C. 1. The Administrator of the Authority shall provide such
22 staff support and independent technical assistance as needed by the
23 Advisory Committee to enable the Advisory Committee to make
24 effective recommendations.
SB903 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. The Advisory Committee shall elect from among its members a
2 chair and a vice-chair vice chair who shall serve one-year terms. A
3 member may serve more than one (1), but not more than four (4),
4 consecutive one-year terms as chair or vice-chair vice chair. A
5 majority of the members of the Advisory Committee shall constitute a
6 quorum to transact business, but no vacancy shall impair the right
7 of the remaining members to exercise all of the powers of the
8 Advisory Committee.
9 3. Members shall not receive any compensation for their
10 services but shall be reimbursed pursuant to the provisions of the
11 State Travel Reimbursement Act, Section 500.1 et seq. of Title 74 of
12 the Oklahoma Statutes.
13 D. The Authority shall give due consideration to the comments
14 and recommendations of the Advisory Committee in the Authority’s
15 deliberations on policies, administration, management and operation
16 of the Authority.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

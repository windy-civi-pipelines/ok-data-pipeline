8 [ transportation - state highway system property –
requiring identification of military members and
9 groups - effective date ]
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 69 O.S. 2021, Section 1600.1, is
13 amended to read as follows:
14 Section 1600.1. A. Highways, bridges and other facilities
15 which are part of the state highway system may be considered for
16 dedication in honor of individuals deserving of commendation for
17 their active involvement in the project or for outstanding service
18 to the nation, this state or their community. Proposals for the
19 dedication of state highway facilities should specifically state the
20 accomplishments upon which the proposal is based.
21 B. The Oklahoma Department of Transportation shall maintain a
22 digital map identifying all designations approved under the Memorial
23 Highways and Bridges Program and shall be made available on the
24 agency website. This map shall provide the name of the honorees,
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2832 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 the location and description of the segment of highway, bridge, or
2 facility being named, and the reasons for designation, including any
3 historical significance. Upon the availability of funding, the map
4 shall be upgraded with interactive multimedia capabilities and other
5 enhancements to be shared with the public through a web-based
6 mapping application so that the public can learn more about the
7 individuals or groups being honored. Upon its availability, a link
8 to the enhanced mapping system shall be shared with the Oklahoma
9 Tourism and Recreation Department to promote the State of Oklahoma.
10 C. All military members and groups who have been honored in the
11 Memorial Highways and Bridges Program shall be identified in the
12 Veterans Military Trail Segment which shall be specified on the
13 interactive map.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

9 An Act relating to dual office-holding; amending 51
O.S. 2021, Section 6, which relates to exemptions;
10 adding exemption for campus police officers serving

11 updating statutory reference; and providing an
effective date.

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 51 O.S. 2021, Section 6, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 6. A. Except as may be otherwise provided, no person
18 holding an office under the laws of the state and no deputy of any
19 officer so holding any office shall, during the person’s term of
20 office, hold any other office or be the deputy of any officer
21 holding any office, under the laws of the state. The provisions of
22 this section shall not apply to:
23 1. Notaries public;
24 2. Members of the State Textbook Committee;

4 29. Members of the Council on Judicial Complaints;

9 30. 31. Any person who is a state employee but not a member of
10 the state military forces, including district attorneys, assistant
11 district attorneys, district court judges, associate district court
12 judges, and special judges, when detailed as a military trial judge
13 pursuant to Section 826 of Title 44 of the Oklahoma Statutes or when
14 serving as an appellate military judge pursuant to Section 866 of
15 Title 44 of the Oklahoma Statutes when the Military Court of Appeals
16 is convened. The rules of procedure prescribed by the State Judge
17 Advocate pursuant to subsection L of Section 866 of Title 44 of the
18 Oklahoma Statutes shall define what constitutes the Military Court
19 of Appeals being “convened” for purposes of this paragraph.
20 The provisions of this section shall not prohibit any person
21 holding an office under the laws of the state or any deputy of any
22 officer so holding any office from serving upon the board of
23 Oklahoma Futures or upon the board of directors of the Oklahoma
24 Center for the Advancement of Science and Technology. The
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2083 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 prov isions of this section shall not prohibit a member of the board
2 of directors of the Oklahoma Center for the Advancement of Science
3 and Technology from serving upon the board of Oklahoma Futures.
4 B. Except as provided in subsection C of this section,
5 salaries, emoluments, or benefits that would otherwise be paid by
6 the agency or political subdivision to a loaned employee or officer
7 shall instead be paid to the regular employer of such employee. The
8 loaned employee shall in turn be paid regular salary and benefits
9 the same as if continuing regular employment with the permanent
10 employer.
11 C. Any person excepted pursuant to paragraph 30 31 of
12 subsection A of this section, when retained as a military trial
13 judge or when serving as a military appellate judge when the
14 Military Court of Appeals is convened, shall be entitled to military
15 judicial leave in accordance with Section 209 of Title 44 of the
16 Oklahoma Statutes.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2025.

16 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
17 1ST SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
18 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
19 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law,
20 the repeal of Section 3 of Article VII-B and the following proposed
21 amendment to Sections 1, 2, and 4 of Article VII-B of the Oklahoma
22 Constitution to read as follows:
23 Section 1. (a) The A. On or after the effective date of this
24 resolution, the provisions of this Article shall govern the

1 sele ction and tenure of all Justices of the Supreme Court and Judges
2 of the Court of Criminal Appeals and the Court of Civil Appeals of
3 the State of Oklahoma, to which the provisions hereof may be
4 extended as hereinafter provided, other provisions of the
5 Constitution or statutes of the State of Oklahoma to the contrary
6 notwithstanding, and the provisions of Article VII as proposed by
7 House Joint Resolution No. 508 of the First Session of the Thirty-
8 first Oklahoma Legislature to the contrary notwithstanding.
9 (b) B. As used in this Section Article, “Judicial Office” means
10 the offices of Justice of the Supreme Court and Judges of the Court
11 of Criminal Appeals and Court of Civil Appeals and “Judicial
12 Officer” means a Justice or Judge of each such court, excluding
13 retired or supernumerary Justices or Judges.
14 Section 2. A. At the general election next before his or her
15 term expires, any Judicial Officer may seek retention in office by
16 filing with the Secretary of State, not less than sixty (60) days
17 before the date of such election, a declaration of candidacy to
18 succeed himself or herself. Thereupon, at such election, there
19 shall be submitted to the qualified electors of the State this
20 state, on a separate ballot, without party designation, this
21 question:
22 “Shall (Here insert name of Justice or Judge) of (Here
23 insert the title of the court) be retained in Office?”
24 □ YES

(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 □ NO
2 The question shall be decided by a majority of those voting
3 thereon. If the decision is “yes” the Judicial Officer shall be
4 retained in office for the next ensuing six (6) year six-year term.
5 If the decision is “no”, or if no declaration of candidacy is filed,
6 the office shall be vacant upon expiration of the term then being
7 served, and the former Judicial Officer shall not be eligible for
8 appointment to succeed himself or herself. Retention in office may
9 be sought for successive terms without limit as to number, except
10 for retirement as may be provided by the Legislature for a maximum
11 retirement age.
12 B. Subject to subsection (c) of Section 11 of Article VII of
13 the Oklahoma Constitution, Judicial Officers appointed or retained
14 pursuant to this Article on or after the effective date of this
15 resolution shall hold their offices during good behavior, and shall,
16 at stated times, receive a compensation for their services which
17 shall not be diminished during their continuance in office.
18 Section 4. When a vacancy in any Judicial Office, however
19 arising, occurs or is certain to occur, the Judicial Nominating
20 Commission shall choose and submit to the Governor and the Chief
21 Justice of the Supreme Court three (3) nominees, each of whom has
22 previously notified the Commission in writing that he will serve as
23 a Judicial Officer if appointed. The the Governor shall appoint one
24 (1) of the nominees to fill the vacancy, but if he fails to do so

(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 with in sixty (60) days the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court shall
2 appoint one (1) of the nominees, the appointment to be certified by
3 the Secretary of State nominate and, with the advice and consent of
4 the Senate, shall appoint a Judicial Officer. If the Senate is not
5 in session when a nomination is made, the Governor may call the
6 Senate into special session to advise and consent on any such
7 nomination.
8 SECTION 2. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
9 amendment as set forth in SECTION 1 of this resolution shall be in
10 the following form:
11 BALLOT TITLE
12 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
13 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
14 This measure would amend Article 7-B of the Oklahoma
15 Constitution. The measure would amend Sections 1, 2, and 4 of
16 Article 7-B to establish a new process for appointing Supreme
17 Court Justices and Judges of the Court of Criminal Appeals and
18 Court of Civil Appeals to reflect the process established by the
19 United States Constitution. Judicial Officers appointed or
20 retained after the effective date of this resolution will serve
21 during good behavior. The Governor will nominate new Judicial
22 Officers, subject to confirmation by the Senate. The measure
23 would repeal Section 3 of Article 7-B, which established the
24 Judicial Nominating Commission.

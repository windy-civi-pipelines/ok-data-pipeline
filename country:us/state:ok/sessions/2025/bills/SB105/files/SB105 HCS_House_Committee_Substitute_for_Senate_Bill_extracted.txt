12 An Act relating to the Lindsey Nicole Henry
Scholarships for Students with Disabilities Program;
13 deleting references to public school; replacing
references to disability with special need; removing
14 prior enrollment in public school requirement;
modifying eligibility requirements for scholarships;
15 removing certain effect after acceptance of a
scholarship; providing an effective date; and
16 declaring an emergency.

20 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 13-101.2, as

1 The Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with Disabilities
2 Program is established to provide a scholarship to a private school
3 of choice for students with disabilities for whom an individualized
4 education program (IEP) in accordance with the Individuals with
5 Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) or an individualized service plan
6 pursuant to Section 1-4-704 of Title 10A of the Oklahoma Statutes

8 Department of Education of the intent to participate in the Program
9 and the IEP is in effect at the time the request for a scholarship
10 is received by the State Department of Education special needs who
11 meet at least one of the criteria of subparagraphs a, b, c, d, e, f,
12 or g of paragraph 1 of subsection B of this section. Scholarships
13 shall be awarded beginning with the 2010-2011 school year.
14 B. The parent or legal guardian of a public school student with
15 a disability special need may exercise their his or her parental
16 option and request to have a Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship
17 awarded for the child to enroll in and attend a private school in
18 accordance with this section and the scholarship shall be awarded
19 if:
20 1. The a. the student has spent the prior school year in
21 attendance at a public school in this state. For

23 attendance" means that the student was enrolled in and

5 plan (ISP), or meets the eligibility standards for
6 special education services in accordance with the
7 Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA),
8 b. the student has had an individualized service plan
9 pursuant to Section 1-4-704 of Title 10A of the
10 Oklahoma Statutes any time prior to notifying the
11 State Department of Education of the intent to
12 participate in the program,
13 c. the student who is a child of a member of the United
14 States Armed Forces who transfers to a school in this
15 state from out of state or from a foreign country
16 pursuant to a permanent change of station orders of
17 the parent shall be exempt from the requirements of
18 this paragraph but shall be required to meet all other
19 eligibility requirements to participate as provided
20 for in this section. A student who has been provided
21 services under an Individual Family Service Plan

23 was evaluated and determined to be eligible for school
24 district services shall be exempt from the

1 requirements of this paragraph but shall be required
2 to meet all other eligibility requirements to

8 f. the student is enrolling or is enrolled in a school
9 that exclusively serves students experiencing

11 g. the student was in out-of-home placement with the
12 Office of Juvenile Affairs shall be exempt from the
13 requirements of this paragraph but shall be required
14 to meet all other eligibility requirements to
15 participate as provided for in this section; and
16 2. The parent or legal guardian has obtained acceptance for
17 admission of the student to a private school that is eligible for
18 the program as provided in subsection H of this section and has
19 notified the State Department of Education of the request for a
20 scholarship. Requests to participate in the program made after
21 December 1 shall be granted, but funding for scholarships requested

23 next school year. The request shall be through a communication
24 directly to the Department in a manner that creates a written or

2 request. The Department shall notify the school district in which
3 the student is enrolled, if applicable, upon receipt of the request.
4 For purposes of continuity of educational choice, the scholarship
5 shall remain in force until the student enrolls in a public school,
6 graduates from high school, or reaches the age of twenty-two (22),
7 whichever occurs first. At any time, the parent or legal guardian
8 of the student may remove the student from the private school and
9 place the student in another private school that is eligible for the
10 program as provided in subsection H of this section or place the
11 student in a public school.
12 C. A student shall be eligible for a scholarship if the parent
13 or legal guardian of the student made a request for a scholarship
14 for the 2010-2011 school year and the student transferred to an
15 eligible private school but was subsequently denied a scholarship
16 because the student did not have an IEP in effect on October 1,
17 2009, but did meet all other eligibility requirements as set forth
18 in the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with
19 Disabilities Program Act.
20 D. A student shall not be eligible for a Lindsey Nicole Henry
21 Scholarship if the student is not having regular and direct contact
22 with the private school teachers at the physical location of the
23 private school.

1 E. School districts shall notify the parent or legal guardian
2 of a public school student with a disability special need of all
3 options available pursuant to this section and inform the parent or
4 legal guardian of the availability of information about the program
5 from the State Department of Education through the toll-free
6 telephone number or website. The notification shall be provided
7 with or included in the copy of the "Parents Rights in Special
8 Education: Notice of Procedural Safeguards" document given to
9 parents at least annually or as otherwise required by law.

15 parent or legal guardian shall assume full financial responsibility
16 for the education of the student including, but not limited to,
17 transportation to and from the private school.

19 Henry Scholarship and the student is accepted by the private school

1 scho ol. If notification is made after December 1, payment of the
2 scholarship shall not begin until the next school year.
3 H. 1. To be eligible to participate in the Lindsey Nicole
4 Henry Scholarships for Students with Disabilities Program, a private
5 school shall notify the State Department of Education of its intent
6 to participate. The notice shall specify the grade levels and
7 services that the private school has available for students with
8 disabilities special needs who are participating in the scholarship
9 program. The State Department of Education shall approve a private
10 school as eligible to participate in the scholarship program upon
11 determination that the private school:
12 a. meets the accreditation requirements set by the State
13 Board of Education or another accrediting association
14 approved by the State Board of Education. The State
15 Department of Education shall list on its website all
16 accrediting associations approved by the Board,
17 b. demonstrates fiscal soundness by having been in
18 operation for one (1) school year or providing the
19 State Department of Education with a statement by a
20 certified public accountant confirming that the
21 private school desiring to participate is insured and
22 the owner or owners have sufficient capital or credit
23 to operate the school for the upcoming year by serving
24 the number of students anticipated with expected

1 revenues from tuition and other sources that may be
2 reasonably expected. In lieu of a statement, a surety
3 bond or letter of credit for the amount equal to the
4 scholarship funds for any quarter may be filed with
5 the Department,
6 c. complies with the antidiscrimination provisions of 42
7 U.S.C., Section 2000d,
8 d. meets state and local health and safety laws and
9 codes,
10 e. will be academically accountable to the parent or
11 legal guardian for meeting the educational needs of
12 the student,
13 f. employs or contracts with teachers who hold
14 baccalaureate or higher degrees, or have at least
15 three (3) years of teaching experience in public or
16 private schools, or have special skills, knowledge, or
17 expertise that qualifies them to provide instruction
18 in subjects taught,
19 g. complies with all state laws relating to general
20 regulation of private schools, and
21 h. adheres to the tenets of its published disciplinary
22 procedures prior to the expulsion of a scholarship
23 student.

1 2. Upon approval of a private school, the State Department of
2 Education shall provide on its website all information and material
3 submitted by the private school with its application.
4 I. 1. Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarship Program participants
5 shall comply with the following:
6 a. the parent or legal guardian shall select the private
7 school from the schools approved for eligibility
8 pursuant to subsection H of this section and apply for
9 the admission of the child,
10 b. the parent or legal guardian shall request the
11 scholarship no later than December 1 of the school
12 year during which the scholarship is requested,
13 c. any student participating in the scholarship program
14 shall attend throughout the school year, unless
15 excused by the school for illness or other good cause,
16 and shall comply fully with the code of conduct for
17 the school,
18 d. the parent or legal guardian shall fully comply with
19 the parental involvement requirements of the private
20 school, unless excused by the school for illness or
21 other good cause, and
22 e. upon issuance of a scholarship warrant, the parent or
23 legal guardian to whom the warrant is made shall
24 restrictively endorse the warrant to the private

1 school for deposit into the account of the private
2 school. The parent or legal guardian may not
3 designate any entity or individual associated with the
4 private school as the attorney in fact for the parent
5 or legal guardian to endorse a warrant. A parent or
6 legal guardian who fails to comply with this
7 subparagraph shall forfeit the scholarship.
8 2. A participant who fails to comply with this subsection
9 forfeits the scholarship.
10 J. Provisions governing payment of a Lindsey Nicole Henry
11 Scholarship shall be as follows:
12 1. The State Department of Education shall calculate the total
13 cost of all scholarships for all eligible students in the state.
14 The State Department of Education shall then reserve or retain from
15 the total amount appropriated to the State Board of Education for
16 State Aid purposes and any other revenue available for allocation
17 for State Aid purposes the total cost for all scholarship payments;
18 2. The maximum scholarship granted for an eligible student with
19 disabilities special needs shall be a calculated amount equivalent
20 to the total State Aid factors for the applicable school year
21 multiplied by the grade and disability weights for which the student
22 is eligible for the applicable school year. The disability weights
23 used in calculating the scholarship amount shall include all
24 disability weights which correspond to the disabilities included in
Req. No. 13550 Page 10
1 the multidisciplinary evaluation and eligibility group summary for
2 the student at the time the request for a scholarship is made by the
3 parent or legal guardian. The maximum scholarship amount shall be
4 calculated by the State Board of Education for each year the student
5 is participating in the scholarship program;
6 3. The amount of the scholarship shall be the amount calculated
7 in paragraph 2 of this subsection or the amount of tuition and fees
8 for the private school, whichever is less, minus up to two and one-
9 half percent (2 1/2%) of the scholarship amount which may be
10 retained by the State Department of Education as a fee for
11 administrative services rendered. If a private school does not
12 charge tuition, the amount of the scholarship shall be the amount
13 calculated in paragraph 2 of this subsection minus up to two and
14 one-half percent (2 1/2%) of the scholarship amount which may be
15 retained by the State Department of Education as a fee for
16 administrative services rendered. The amount of any assessment fee
17 required by the private school and the amount associated with
18 providing services and therapies to address the disabilities special
19 needs of the student may be paid from the total amount of the
20 scholarship. The amount of the scholarship shall be prorated to
21 reflect the number of days remaining in the current school year, if
22 the scholarship request is granted after the beginning of the school
23 year;
24
Req. No. 13550 Page 11
1 4. The State Department of Education shall notify the private
2 school of the amount of the scholarship within ten (10) days after
3 receiving the request for a scholarship, when the total State Aid
4 factors have been determined for the current fiscal year. The
5 initial payment shall be made after the Department verifies
6 admission acceptance and enrollment. Quarterly payments shall be
7 made upon verification of continued enrollment and attendance at the
8 private school. Payment shall be made by the Department with an
9 individual warrant made payable to the parent or legal guardian of
10 the student and mailed by the Department to the private school that
11 the parent or legal guardian chooses. The parent or legal guardian
12 shall restrictively endorse the warrant to the private school for
13 deposit into the account of the private school;
14 5. The State Department of Education shall not be responsible
15 for any additional costs associated with special education and
16 related services incurred by the private school for the student
17 including the cost of teachers, equipment, material, and special
18 costs associated with the special education class;
19 6. The State Department of Education shall establish a toll-
20 free telephone number or website that provides parents or legal
21 guardians and private schools with information about the program;
22 7. The State Department of Education shall require an annual,
23 notarized, sworn compliance statement by participating private
24 schools certifying compliance with state laws and shall retain all
Req. No. 13550 Page 12
1 reco rds received from a participating private school. The State
2 Department of Education shall post on its website the annual
3 compliance statement for each participating private school; and
4 8. The State Department of Education shall cross-check the list
5 of participating scholarship students with the public school
6 enrollments prior to each scholarship payment to avoid duplication.
7 K. 1. The State Superintendent of Public Instruction shall
8 deny, suspend, or revoke the participation of a private school in
9 the scholarship program if it is determined that the private school
10 has failed to comply with the provisions of this section. However,
11 in instances in which the noncompliance is correctable within a
12 reasonable amount of time and in which the health, safety, or
13 welfare of the students is not threatened, the Superintendent may
14 issue a notice of noncompliance which shall provide the private
15 school with a time frame within which to provide evidence of
16 compliance prior to taking action to suspend or revoke participation
17 in the scholarship program.
18 2. If the Superintendent intends to deny, suspend, or revoke
19 the participation of a private school in the scholarship program,
20 the Department shall notify the private school of the proposed
21 action in writing by certified mail and regular mail to the private
22 school's address of record with the Department. The Department
23 shall also notify any parents or legal guardians of scholarship
24 students attending the private school. The notification shall
Req. No. 13550 Page 13
1 incl ude the reasons for the proposed action and notice of the
2 timelines and procedures set forth in this subsection.
3 3. The private school that is adversely affected by the
4 proposed action shall have fifteen (15) days from receipt of the
5 notice of proposed action to file with the Department a request for
6 an administrative hearing proceeding pursuant to the Administrative
7 Procedures Act.
8 4. Upon receipt of a request for a hearing, the State Board of
9 Education shall commence a hearing within sixty (60) days after the
10 receipt of the formal written request and enter an order within
11 thirty (30) days after the hearing.
12 5. The Board may immediately suspend payment of scholarship
13 funds if it is determined that there is probable cause to believe
14 that there is an imminent threat to the health, safety, or welfare
15 of the students or fraudulent activity on the part of the private
16 school.
17 L. No liability shall arise on the part of the state, the State
18 Board of Education, the State Department of Education, or a school
19 district based on the award or use of any scholarship provided
20 through the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students with
21 Disabilities Program.
22 M. The inclusion of private schools within options available to
23 public school students in Oklahoma shall not expand the regulatory
24 authority of the state or any school district to impose any
Req. No. 13550 Page 14
1 addi tional regulation of private schools beyond those reasonably
2 necessary to enforce the requirements expressly set forth in this
3 section.
4 N. If the State Department of Education determines that a
5 school district prior to August 26, 2011, has failed to comply with
6 the provisions of the Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships for Students
7 with Disabilities Program Act and has failed to make full or partial
8 scholarship payments for eligible students, the Department shall
9 have authority to reduce the amount of State Aid allocated to the
10 school district or require the school district to make repayment to
11 the Department of State Aid allocations in an amount equal to the
12 amount of scholarship payments the school district failed to make.
13 The Department shall make payment to the parent or legal guardian in
14 the amount the school district failed to make in the manner as
15 provided for in subsection J of this section.
16 O. The State Department of Education shall annually prepare and
17 post on its website a report of Lindsey Nicole Henry Scholarships
18 for Students with Disabilities Program. The annual report shall
19 include, but not be limited to, the following information:
20 1. The total number and amount of scholarships awarded and
21 reported for each participating private school;
22 2. The total number of scholarships denied;
23 3. The total number and amount of scholarship payments
24 suspended for each participating private school; and
Req. No. 13550 Page 15
1 4. Data on participating students, disaggregated by years of
2 participation in the program, grade level, economically
3 disadvantaged status, racial and ethnic groups, and disability
4 category.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
6 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
8 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
